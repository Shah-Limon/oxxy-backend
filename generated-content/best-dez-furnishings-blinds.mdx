## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Dez Furnishings Blinds

Choosing the right blinds involves more than just picking a color. To ensure you're happy with your purchase for years to come, consider these key factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about the room and its purpose. For a bedroom or media room, a blackout shade like the **DEZ FURNISHINGS Blackout Cellular Shade** is ideal for blocking light. In a bathroom or kitchen where moisture is present, a faux wood option is essential because it resists warping. For a living room, a light-filtering cellular shade or a faux wood blind can provide a warm ambiance while maintaining privacy. For metal doors, the **No-Tools Magnetic Blinds** are a specialized and perfect solution.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
DEZ Furnishings offers a range of price points. Basic faux wood blinds might range from $40-$80 depending on size. More specialized options like the large blackout cellular shades or signature embossed blinds can range from $70-$150 or more. Set a budget per window, but be prepared to invest a bit more for features like blackout capabilities or premium textures, which add significant value.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that match your lifestyle. The cordless lift system is a crucial safety feature for homes with children or pets and is standard on all these DEZ models. -   **Living/Dining Rooms:** Choose light-filtering options like faux wood or standard cellular shades to create a warm, inviting atmosphere.

-   **Metal Doors:** Use the specialized **Magnetic Mini Blinds** for a no-drill solution.

**Step 2: Measure Your Windows Accurately**
This is the most critical step. A common mistake is measuring incorrectly.
-   **For an Inside Mount:** Measure the width inside the window frame at the top, middle, and bottom. Use the narrowest measurement. Measure the height at the left, middle, and right, and use the longest measurement. The factory will make the necessary deduction (usually 1/2 inch) for a proper fit.
-   **For an Outside Mount:** Measure the width of the area you want to cover. It's recommended to add at least 1.5-3 inches to each side of the window frame for better light control and privacy. Measure the desired height, again adding a few inches above and below the frame.

**Step 3: Select the Right Style and Material**
Choose a style that complements your home's decor.
-   **Faux Wood Blinds:** Offer a classic, timeless look that works with almost any style.
-   **Cellular Shades:** Provide a soft, modern, and clean aesthetic. They are also the best choice for energy efficiency.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
-   **Guessing Measurements:** Never estimate. Always use a steel tape measure for accuracy.
-   **Ignoring Mount Type:** Deciding between an inside or outside mount affects your measurements and the final look.
-   **Choosing the Wrong Material for the Room:** Putting a non-moisture-resistant blind in a bathroom will lead to damage.
