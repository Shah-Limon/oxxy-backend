## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Plastic Containers For Water

Choosing the right plastic water container involves more than just picking a size. To ensure you get a product that is safe, durable, and meets your specific needs, consider these important factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you will primarily use the container. Are you looking for bulk storage for emergency preparedness? You'll want a large, stackable container like a 5-gallon Hedpak. Do you need something for daily use in the refrigerator? A 1-quart pitcher with an easy-pour lid is ideal. For camping and hiking, a lightweight, collapsible container is the best choice. **How long do plastic containers for water typically last?**
A high-quality, well-maintained plastic water container can last for many years. Hard-sided containers made from HDPE can last a decade or more if stored properly away from direct sunlight, which can degrade the plastic over time. Collapsible containers may have a shorter lifespan of 3-5 years depending on the frequency of use and care, as folding can eventually stress the seams.

**Is it safe to reuse all plastic water containers?**
It is safe to reuse containers specifically designed for reuse, such as those made from HDPE (plastic #2), LDPE (#4), or PP (#5), as long as they are labeled food-grade and BPA-free. You should avoid reusing single-use bottles (like typical disposable water bottles, often #1 PET), as they can break down over time and are difficult to clean properly, potentially harboring bacteria.

**How should I store water for emergencies in plastic containers?**
For emergency storage, use a clean, sanitized, food-grade plastic container. Fill it with tap water, leaving a small amount of air space. Seal it tightly and label it with the date. Store it in a cool, dark, and dry place, away from direct sunlight and any chemicals. It's recommended to replace the water every 6-12 months to ensure freshness.
