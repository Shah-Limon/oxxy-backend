## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Outdoor Heater With Lights

Choosing the right heater involves more than just picking the most powerful one. Consider these factors to ensure you find a model that perfectly suits your space, needs, and budget.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your space. How large is the area you need to heat? A small balcony will require a less powerful heater than a large, open patio. Think about placement. Do you have a wall or ceiling for a mounted unit, or is a freestanding tower a better fit? Consider your primary use. Is it for quiet evening relaxation, or for hosting lively parties where extra features like speakers and lights would be a bonus?

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Outdoor heaters with lights come in a wide price range.
- **$50 - $150:** In this range, you'll find basic hanging models or component parts like heat lamp bulbs. They are functional but may lack advanced features, multiple heat settings, or high durability ratings.
- **$150 - $300:** This is the sweet spot for most high-quality heaters. You can expect features like remote controls, multiple power settings, good weatherproofing (IP44 or higher), and durable construction.
- **$300+:** Premium models in this category often include smart features like app control, multi-functional designs with speakers and RGB lights, and superior IP65 waterproof ratings for maximum durability.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Heating Technology:** Infrared (carbon or halogen) is the most common and efficient for outdoor use, as it heats objects directly rather than the air, which can be blown away by wind.
- **Power (Wattage):** Most patio heaters range from 750W to 1500W. 1500W is a standard for good heat output in an average-sized patio space. Look for models with multiple settings for flexibility.
- **IP Rating (Weatherproofing):** The Ingress Protection rating indicates how well the heater is sealed against dust and water. IP44 is splash-resistant, while IP65 is fully protected against jets of water, making it suitable for more exposed locations.
- **Control Methods:** A remote control is a standard convenience. Advanced models may offer control via a smartphone app, which is great for pre-heating your space.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for heaters made from durable, corrosion-resistant materials like aluminum or stainless steel. These materials will withstand the elements and last longer. Check the quality of the mounting brackets and chains if you're choosing a hanging or wall-mounted unit. A stable, weighted base is crucial for freestanding tower models to prevent tipping.

Freestanding tower heaters offer more flexibility in placement but require floor space and may need to be stored during the off-season. Ensure the model you choose fits comfortably in your designated area without being a safety hazard.
