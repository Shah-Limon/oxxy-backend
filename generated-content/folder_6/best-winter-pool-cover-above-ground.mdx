## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Winter Pool Cover Above Ground

Choosing the right winter cover is crucial for the health and longevity of your pool. Here are the key factors to consider to ensure you make the best choice.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First and foremost, you must know the exact size and shape of your pool. A cover for a 24-foot round pool will not work for a 21-foot or an oval pool. Measure the widest points of your pool to confirm its dimensions. Consider your local climate. If you live in an area with heavy snowfall, a thicker, more durable cover with a high scrim count is essential. If high winds are your primary concern, look for covers with advanced fastening systems like ratchets or extra grommets.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Winter pool covers come in a wide price range. You can find basic, entry-level covers for around $50-$75. Mid-range, more durable options with better warranties, like many from Robelle or Winter Block, typically fall in the $75-$150 range. For premium, heavy-duty covers with the highest scrim counts and longest warranties, like those from Yankee Pool Pillow or JTW PRODUCT, expect to pay $150-$250 or more. While a higher price often means better quality, our list shows you can find excellent value at various price points.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Scrim Count:** This is the number of threads per square inch in the weave (e.g., 8x8, 14x14). A higher scrim count means a tighter weave and a stronger, more tear-resistant cover.
- **Material & Density:** Most covers are made of polyethylene. Look for the weight or density, often measured in grams per square meter (GSM). A higher GSM (like 185 GSM) indicates a thicker, more durable material.
- **Overlap:** This is the extra material that extends beyond your pool's edge. A 3- to 4-foot overlap is ideal, as it makes the cover much easier to secure with the cable and winch.
- **Fastening System:** All covers should include a winch and a vinyl-coated steel cable. Look for upgraded winches (e.g., zinc-alloy) or alternative systems (ratchets) for a more secure fit.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Pay attention to the seams. They should be heat-sealed or double-stitched to prevent splitting under stress. The grommets, which hold the cable, should be made of a rust-proof metal like aluminum or brass and be reinforced to prevent them from tearing out. A UV-resistant coating is also crucial to prevent the sun from degrading the material over the winter and spring months.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Robelle have been in the business for decades and have a proven track record of quality and customer service. Choosing an established brand often means you are getting a product that has been refined over time and is backed by a reliable warranty. While newer brands can offer innovation, a long-standing reputation provides a level of trust and assurance.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
A thicker, more durable cover will be heavier and bulkier. Consider how easy it will be for you to handle the cover during installation and removal. You will also need a clean, dry place to store it during the swimming season. Ensure you have adequate space, and keep in mind that a heavier cover might require two people to manage safely.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy winter pool cover above ground?

Investing in a dedicated winter pool cover is one of the wisest decisions an above ground pool owner can make. While a simple tarp might seem like a cheap alternative, it lacks the durability, features, and proper fit to adequately protect your pool. A proper winter cover is specifically designed to withstand harsh weather, from heavy snow and ice to high winds and UV radiation.

The long-term benefits far outweigh the initial cost. A good cover keeps debris out, which means your spring clean-up will be drastically easier. You'll spend less time scooping leaves and more time enjoying the water. By blocking sunlight, a solid cover prevents algae growth, saving you significant money on chemicals and shock treatments needed to clear up a green, swampy pool. Most importantly, it protects your pool's liner from sun damage and debris that could cause punctures, extending the life of your entire pool structure. Compared to the cost of a major cleaning, chemical rebalancing, or liner replacement, a quality winter cover pays for itself many times over.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose winter pool cover above ground?

Navigating the options for a winter pool cover can be simple if you follow a clear process. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you select the perfect one.

**1. Accurately Measure Your Pool:** Before you even start shopping, get the exact dimensions of your pool. For round pools, measure the diameter across the widest point. For oval pools, measure the length and width. This is the single most important step.

**2. Assess Your Climate and Needs:** Be realistic about your winter weather. If you get several feet of snow, prioritize a high scrim count (12x12 or 14x14) and high material density (GSM). If wind is the main issue, focus on covers with a large overlap (4 feet) and robust fastening systems.

**3. Set Your Budget:** Decide what you're comfortable spending. Remember that investing a little more upfront for a cover with a longer warranty (e.g., 10 or 12 years vs. 5) can save you money in the long run by avoiding premature replacement.

**4. Compare Key Features:** Don't just look at the price. Compare the scrim count, overlap size, warranty length, and included hardware. A cover that's $20 cheaper but has a smaller overlap and a shorter warranty may not be the better value.

**5. Read Reviews, but with Context:** User reviews are helpful, but look for patterns. A few complaints about a winch might be a minor issue, but widespread reports of tearing are a major red flag.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Buying the Exact Size:** Never buy a cover that is the exact same size as your pool. You need the overlap to secure it. Always buy a cover specifically labeled for your pool's dimensions (e.g., a '24-foot cover' which will actually be 27-28 feet in diameter).
- **Ignoring the Warranty:** The warranty period is often a good indicator of the manufacturer's confidence in the product's lifespan. A longer warranty is usually worth paying a bit more for.
- **Forgetting the Air Pillow:** For solid covers, an air pillow (sold separately) is essential. It goes under the cover in the center of the pool to absorb the pressure from expanding ice, which can damage your pool walls.

## FAQ

**What is the best winter pool cover above ground for beginners?**
For beginners, the best option is a cover that is easy to install. The Robelle series is excellent because of its generous 4-foot overlap, which provides more room for error and makes securing the cable much simpler. A complete kit like the POOLACC cover, which includes accessories, can also simplify the process for first-time pool closers.

**How much should I spend on winter pool cover above ground?**
A good quality winter cover typically costs between $75 and $150. Budget options can be found for under $75, while premium, ultra-durable covers for harsh climates can exceed $200. Spending in the $100-$125 range usually gets you a great balance of durability, features, and a strong warranty.

**What features are most important in winter pool cover above ground?**
The most important features are the material's scrim count and density (GSM), the size of the overlap, and the quality of the fastening system (winch and cable). A higher scrim count and GSM mean more durability. A larger overlap (4 feet is ideal) makes installation easier, and a strong winch ensures a tight, secure fit.

**How do I maintain my winter pool cover above ground?**
During winter, periodically use a soft broom or leaf rake to remove excess debris and snow to prevent stress. Use a cover pump to remove any large accumulations of water. When you remove it in the spring, clean it with mild soap and water, let it dry completely, and store it in a cool, dry place away from rodents.

**Are expensive winter pool cover above ground worth it?**
Yes, if you live in an area with harsh winters. Expensive covers, like the Yankee Pool Pillow or JTW PRODUCT models, use thicker materials, have higher tear resistance, and often come with longer warranties. This extra durability can prevent mid-winter failures and mean the cover lasts many more seasons, making it a better long-term investment.

**What brands make the best winter pool cover above ground?**
Robelle is a highly trusted brand with decades of experience and a reputation for quality and ease of use. Yankee Pool Pillow and POOLACC are known for their heavy-duty, premium materials. Winter Block offers a great balance of quality and affordability. These brands consistently receive positive feedback for their performance and durability.

**How long do winter pool cover above ground typically last?**
A cover's lifespan depends on its quality and your climate. A basic, entry-level cover might last 1-3 seasons. A good quality, mid-range cover should last 3-7 seasons. A premium, heavy-duty cover can last 7-10 years or even longer with proper care. The warranty length is often a good, though prorated, indicator of expected lifespan.

**Can I find good winter pool cover above ground on a budget?**
Absolutely. Brands like Winter Block offer very capable covers at a budget-friendly price. They provide solid construction, good warranties, and all the necessary hardware. While they may not be as thick as premium options, they offer excellent protection for their cost and are a significant step up from a generic tarp.

**What is the purpose of the 4-foot overlap?**
The overlap is the extra material that extends past the edge of your pool. A generous 4-foot overlap makes installation much easier. It ensures you have enough material to rest on the water and drape over the side, allowing you to easily thread the cable through the grommets and tighten it without a struggle. Covers with less overlap can be very difficult to secure properly.

**Do I need a pool pillow with my winter cover?**
Yes, if you use a solid cover in a climate where the water freezes, a pool pillow (also called an ice equalizer) is essential. You place it in the center of the pool under the cover. When the water freezes and expands, it compresses the pillow instead of pushing outward on your pool walls, which could cause serious damage.
