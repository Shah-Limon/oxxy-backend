## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Small Countertop Microwaves

Choosing the right small microwave involves more than just picking the smallest one. Consider these key factors to ensure you find an appliance that meets your needs and fits your space perfectly.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess how you'll use the microwave. Are you primarily reheating coffee and leftovers? Or do you plan to cook full meals and defrost large items? For simple tasks, a basic 700-watt, 0.7 Cu. Ft. model is sufficient. For more involved cooking, look for a model with at least 900 watts and 0.9 Cu. Ft. of capacity. If you want to replace other appliances, a multi-functional combo unit like an air fryer microwave could be the perfect solution.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Small countertop microwaves have a wide price range. Basic, compact models can be found for as little as $70-$90. Mid-range options with more power, features like sensor cooking, and better build quality typically fall between $100 and $150. High-end combo units that include convection or air frying capabilities can cost $180 or more. Set a budget based on your needs, but be prepared to invest a little more for features that add significant convenience.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Wattage:** Higher wattage (900W-1000W) means faster and more even cooking. Lower wattage (700W) is fine for reheating but will take longer.
- **Capacity (Cu. Ft.):** A 0.7 Cu. Ft. model is very compact, while 0.9 to 1.2 Cu. Ft. offers more flexibility for larger plates and dishes.
- **Presets & Controls:** Look for intuitive controls and one-touch presets for common foods like popcorn, potatoes, and beverages.
- **Special Functions:** Features like a mute button, sensor cooking, multi-stage cooking, or an eco mode can greatly enhance the user experience.
- **Turntable:** Check the turntable diameter to ensure it can accommodate your typical dinner plates. A removable, dishwasher-safe turntable makes cleaning easier.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for microwaves with a solid build. Stainless steel exteriors tend to be more durable and easier to clean than plastic ones. Check the door mechanism; it should open and close smoothly but securely. A well-constructed appliance will not only last longer but will also operate more quietly. Reading user reviews can provide insight into the long-term durability of a specific model.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with well-known brands like TOSHIBA, Chefman, Farberware, and COMFEE' often ensures better quality, more reliable performance, and better customer support. These companies have a long history of making kitchen appliances and typically offer warranties, providing peace of mind with your purchase.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your available counter space—width, depth, and height—before you buy. Remember to leave a few inches of clearance around the back and sides for proper ventilation. If you live in a very small space like an RV or dorm, the external dimensions will be your most critical factor. Consider the weight if you plan on moving it frequently.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy small countermicrowaves?

Investing in a small countertop microwave is an exceptionally wise decision for a multitude of modern living situations. The primary advantage is, of course, space efficiency. In apartments, dorm rooms, RVs, or even larger kitchens that are already cluttered with gadgets, a compact microwave provides essential functionality without demanding a large footprint. It frees up valuable counter real estate that can be used for meal prep or other appliances.

Compared to the alternative of using a full-sized oven or stovetop for simple tasks like reheating a cup of coffee or a plate of leftovers, a microwave is vastly more energy-efficient and significantly faster. This translates to long-term savings on your utility bills and precious time saved on busy mornings or evenings. Furthermore, for those who don't cook elaborate meals, a small microwave is often all that's needed, rendering a large, expensive oven almost redundant.

While the initial cost might seem like just another expense, the convenience and efficiency it offers provide a substantial return on investment. The ability to quickly prepare food encourages home cooking over costly takeout, and the appliance's durability means it will serve you for years. A small microwave isn't just an appliance; it's a tool for a more efficient, convenient, and cost-effective lifestyle.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose small countermicrowaves?

Choosing the best small countertop microwave requires a methodical approach to ensure you get the right fit for your kitchen and lifestyle. Follow these steps to make a confident decision.

**1. Measure Your Space First:** Before you even start browsing, get out a tape measure. Record the maximum width, depth, and height of the space where the microwave will live. Don't forget to account for the manufacturer's recommended clearance for ventilation (usually a few inches on the top, back, and sides). This single step will narrow down your options immediately.

**2. Define Your Primary Use:** Be honest about what you'll use it for 90% of the time. Is it for reheating drinks and leftovers? Defrosting meat? Making popcorn? If your needs are basic, a 700W, 0.7 Cu. Ft. model will suffice. If you plan on cooking vegetables or full meals, aim for a more powerful 900W-1000W model with a slightly larger capacity.

**3. Set Your Feature Priorities:** Make a list of must-have features versus nice-to-have features.
    - **Must-Haves:** Might include a specific capacity, a turntable, or simple one-touch controls.
    - **Nice-to-Haves:** Could be a mute function, sensor cooking, a stainless steel finish, or multi-functional capabilities like air frying.

**4. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
    - **Ignoring Interior Height:** A microwave might be wide enough for your plates, but is it tall enough for your favorite coffee mug or a bowl of soup? Check the interior dimensions.
    - **Choosing Power Over Finesse:** Don't assume more watts is always better. A microwave with 10-12 power levels offers more precise control for delicate tasks like melting butter or softening ice cream.
    - **Forgetting About Usability:** A control panel with dozens of tiny buttons can be frustrating. Look for a clean, intuitive interface that is easy to read and operate.

**5. When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:** A budget option is perfect for a dorm, a first apartment, or as a secondary unit in an office. Invest in an upgraded model with more power and features like sensor cooking if the microwave is your primary cooking appliance or if you value convenience and consistently perfect results. If you want to save space by combining appliances, a multi-functional unit is a worthy upgrade.

## FAQ

**What is the best small countermicrowaves for beginners?**
For beginners, the best small countertop microwave is one that is simple and intuitive. The COMFEE' EM720CPL-PMB is an excellent choice. It has straightforward one-touch buttons for common tasks, a clear digital display, and isn't overloaded with complex functions. Its compact size and affordable price make it a low-risk, high-reward option for anyone new to using microwaves regularly.

**How much should I spend on small countermicrowaves?**
You can find a reliable small countertop microwave for between $70 and $150. Basic models with 700 watts and 0.7 Cu. Ft. capacity are typically under $100. For more power (900-1000W), a larger capacity, and extra features like a mute function or a stainless steel finish, expect to spend in the $100 to $150 range. Multi-functional combo units will cost more, often starting around $180.

**What features are most important in small countermicrowaves?**
The most important features are adequate wattage for your needs (at least 700W for reheating, 900W+ for cooking), a capacity that fits your typical plates, and an easy-to-use control panel. A removable turntable is crucial for even cooking and easy cleaning. Features like one-touch express cook buttons and a +30 seconds option add significant everyday convenience.

**How do I maintain my small countermicrowaves?**
Maintenance is simple. Wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild soap after spills to prevent buildup. For tough, cooked-on food, heat a bowl of water with lemon juice or vinegar for a few minutes; the steam will loosen the grime. Wash the glass turntable regularly in the sink or dishwasher. Keep the exterior and vents clean and free of dust.

**Are expensive small countermicrowaves worth it?**
It depends on your needs. More expensive models are worth it if you will use their advanced features. For example, a model with sensor cooking like the TOSHIBA EM131A5C-BS provides foolproof reheating, which is a huge benefit for busy individuals. A multi-functional unit like an air fryer combo is worth the cost if it replaces other appliances and saves you counter space. If you only need basic reheating, a budget model is sufficient.

**What brands make the best small countermicrowaves?**
Several brands are known for making reliable and high-quality small microwaves. TOSHIBA is a leader, especially for models with smart features and solid build quality. Chefman and Farberware offer great value with a good balance of features and performance. COMFEE' is an excellent brand for budget-conscious buyers who still want convenient features like a mute button.

**How long do small countermicrowaves typically last?**
A well-maintained small countertop microwave should last between 5 to 10 years. The lifespan depends on the build quality of the model and how heavily it is used. Microwaves from reputable brands often have better durability. To maximize its life, avoid running it empty, slamming the door, and ensure it has proper ventilation.

**Can I find good small countermicrowaves on a budget?**
Absolutely. Brands like COMFEE' and some entry-level models from Chefman and Farberware offer excellent value. You can find a good, reliable microwave with all the essential features for under $100. These budget-friendly options are perfect for dorms, offices, or anyone who doesn't need high-end features but still wants a dependable appliance for daily use.
