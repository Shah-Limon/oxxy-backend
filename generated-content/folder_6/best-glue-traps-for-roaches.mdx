## What Is the Best Way to Choose glue traps for roaches?

Choosing the best glue traps for roaches involves a strategic approach rather than just grabbing the first box you see. Following a few simple steps can ensure you get the right product for your situation.

**1. Assess Your Environment:**
The first step is to analyze your home. Do you have pets or small children? If so, your top priority should be non-toxic traps, preferably with a covered or tunnel design to prevent accidental contact. Note the areas where you suspect roach activity—are they damp areas like under sinks, or dusty places like a basement? This will determine if you need water-resistant or dust-proof (foldable) traps.

**2. Determine the Scale of the Problem:**
Be realistic about your infestation. A few roaches sighted occasionally might be handled with a small pack of 5-10 traps. If you're seeing them daily or in multiple rooms, you need to think bigger. Opt for a bulk pack of 30 or more to ensure you can place traps along all potential pathways and in all suspected harborage areas.

**3. Prioritize Glue Quality and Attractants:**
The trap's effectiveness hinges on its glue. Look for products known for their strong, deep, and long-lasting adhesive. Traps with built-in attractants, whether a food-based bait pouch or an infused scent, generally have a higher capture rate than those that rely solely on a roach stumbling upon them.

**4. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
A frequent mistake is poor placement. Don't just place traps in the middle of the floor. Roaches are thigmotactic, meaning they prefer to travel along edges and in contact with surfaces. Place traps flush against walls, in corners, under cabinets, and behind large appliances. Another mistake is not using enough traps. A single trap in a large room is unlikely to be effective. Use multiple traps to cover all potential entry and travel points.

**5. Know When to Upgrade:**
If your budget-friendly traps aren't catching anything despite evidence of roaches, it might be time to upgrade to a premium option like a HOY HOY trap with a more potent bait system. Conversely, if you have a massive area to cover, a cost-effective bulk pack like Catchmaster is a smarter choice than a few expensive traps.
