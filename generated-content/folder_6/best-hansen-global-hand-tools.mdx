## What Is the Best Way to Choose hansen global hand tools?

Choosing the best Hansen Global tool organizer is a straightforward process if you follow a few simple steps. This will ensure you get a system that perfectly matches your collection and workflow.

**Step 1: Assess Your Collection**
Dump out your socket drawer. Separate your tools by drive size (1/4", 3/8", 1/2") and then by measurement system (SAE/Standard and Metric). This visual inventory will immediately show you where your biggest organizational needs are. You might find you have a huge collection of 3/8" metric sockets but only a few 1/4" SAE ones.

**Step 2: Prioritize Your Most-Used Tools**
You don't have to organize everything at once. Start with the tools you use every single day. For most people, this is the 3/8" drive set. Buying a dedicated tray for these tools first will give you the biggest and most immediate improvement in your daily workflow.

**Step 3: Measure Your Storage Space**
Get a tape measure and check the dimensions of your tool chest drawers, especially the depth. This will help you decide between 2-row and 3-row trays and ensure that what you buy will fit perfectly.

**Step 4: Consider Color Coding**
A pro tip for ultimate efficiency is to use color to your advantage. A common strategy is to use green trays for all your SAE/Standard tools and orange trays for all your Metric tools. This allows for instant identification at a macro level, saving you even more time.

**Step 5: Start Small and Expand**
Don't feel pressured to buy a massive 261-piece organization set if you only have 50 sockets. Start with the individual trays you need most. The beauty of the Hansen system is its modularity. You can always buy more trays as your tool collection grows, ensuring your organizational system evolves with you. A common mistake is buying a huge set you can't fill, which just wastes space.
