## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 22 Inch Wide Microwave Built In

Choosing the right built-in microwave involves more than just picking a model that looks good. To ensure you get an appliance that fits your space and meets your cooking needs, consider these crucial factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, measure your cabinet cutout precisely—height, width, and depth. A built-in microwave needs to fit perfectly to look and function correctly. Check the manufacturer's required cutout dimensions for any model you're considering. Think about how you'll use it. Do you just reheat and defrost, or do you want to bake, roast, or air fry? Your cooking habits will determine whether a basic model is sufficient or if a multi-functional convection model is a better investment.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Built-in microwaves vary widely in price. Basic models can start around $300-$500, while high-end, multi-functional units with convection and air fry capabilities can exceed $1,000. Remember to factor in the additional cost of a trim kit, which is often sold separately and can cost $150-$300.
