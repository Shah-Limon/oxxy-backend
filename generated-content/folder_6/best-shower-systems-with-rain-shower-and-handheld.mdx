## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Shower Systems With Rain Shower And Handheld

Choosing the right shower system involves more than just picking a style you like. To ensure you get a product that meets your needs and lasts for years, consider these crucial factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your current plumbing. Some systems, particularly retrofit models like the BRIGHT SHOWERS system, are designed to attach to your existing shower arm and valve, making for a simple DIY installation. Others, like most Delta and SR SUN RISE systems, require a complete valve replacement, which means opening up the wall and is best for new constructions or major remodels. Think about who will be using the shower. If you have children, pets, or individuals with mobility issues, a system with an adjustable slide bar and a long hose is essential for flexibility.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Shower systems with a rain shower and handheld can range widely in price. You can find budget-friendly, all-metal options like the Aolemi system for around $150-$250. Mid-range systems with more features or from well-known brands typically fall between $300 and $600. Premium systems, especially those with luxury finishes like Champagne Bronze from brands like Delta, can cost $700 or more. Set a realistic budget, but remember that investing in quality materials and a good warranty can save you money on repairs and replacements in the long run.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for key technical features that enhance safety and performance. A pressure-balance valve is non-negotiable; it prevents scalding by automatically adjusting to changes in hot or cold water pressure. Consider the size of the rain shower head (8, 10, or 12 inches) for your desired coverage. Check the GPM (gallons per minute) flow rate—lower numbers (like 1.8 GPM) are more water-efficient, while higher numbers (2.5 GPM) deliver a more drenching spray. Multiple spray settings on the handheld wand, like massage and mist, add to the versatility.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The materials used are a direct indicator of a system's longevity. Solid brass for the valve body is the gold standard for durability and reliability. For shower heads, arms, and hoses, 304 stainless steel is an excellent, corrosion-resistant choice. While some high-quality ABS plastic is acceptable for handhelds (to keep them lightweight), systems that emphasize all-metal construction, like HammerHead Showers, often provide a more premium feel and longer lifespan. Pay attention to the finish as well; look for PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) finishes or proprietary technologies like Delta's SpotShield, which are designed to resist scratches, tarnish, and water spots.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like Delta have a long history of quality control, reliable customer service, and readily available replacement parts. They often back their products with robust warranties, such as a Lifetime Limited Warranty, which provides significant peace of mind. While newer or lesser-known brands can offer great value, it's important to check their certifications (like cUPC), warranty terms, and customer reviews to gauge their reliability and support.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While shower systems aren't portable, their size is a critical consideration for your bathroom's space. A large 12-inch rain shower head might feel overwhelming in a small, 3x3 foot shower stall. Measure your shower space and consider the scale of the fixtures. The length of the shower arm determines how far the rain head projects from the wall. A 16-inch arm is great for a spacious feel, but might not work in a compact shower. Similarly, ensure the slide bar or handheld holder can be mounted at a comfortable height without interfering with other fixtures.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose shower systems with rain shower and handheld?

Choosing the best shower system requires a methodical approach to ensure it fits your bathroom, budget, and lifestyle. Follow these steps for a successful selection.

**1. Assess Your Plumbing and Installation Needs:**
Before you start shopping, understand your current setup. Is this a new build, a full remodel, or a simple fixture swap? If you're not opening up the walls, you'll need a surface-mount or retrofit system that connects to your existing plumbing. If you are doing a remodel, you have the flexibility to choose a system with a new, integrated valve. This is the most important first step as it dictates which type of system is compatible with your project.

**2. Define Your Must-Have Features:**
Make a list of what's most important to you. Do you need a system that performs well under low pressure? Is an adjustable-height slide bar essential for your family? Do you want the ability to run both the rain shower and handheld simultaneously? Prioritizing features will help you narrow down the options and focus on what truly matters for your daily use.

**3. Set a Realistic Budget:**
Determine how much you are willing to spend. Remember to factor in potential installation costs if you are not doing it yourself. While it's tempting to go for the cheapest option, a mid-range system from a reputable brand often provides the best balance of quality, features, and longevity.

**4. Research Materials and Brands:**
Look for systems with solid brass valves and stainless steel components. Read reviews and compare the warranties and customer support reputations of brands like Delta, SR SUN RISE, and others. A lifetime limited warranty is a strong indicator of a manufacturer's confidence in their product.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
A common mistake is buying a system without checking its compatibility with US plumbing standards (look for 1/2-inch NPT connections and cUPC certification). Another error is choosing a shower head that is too large for the shower stall, leading to water splashing out. Finally, don't underestimate the importance of a pressure-balance or thermostatic valve for safety—never choose a system without one.

Other important features include an adjustable slide bar for the handheld sprayer to accommodate different user heights, multiple spray settings for versatility, and easy-to-clean nozzles to prevent mineral buildup.

### How do I maintain my shower systems with rain shower and handheld?
Maintenance is relatively simple. Regularly wipe down the fixtures with a soft, damp cloth to prevent water spot buildup. For the nozzles, most modern systems feature soft silicone or rubber jets (like Delta's Touch-Clean) that allow you to dislodge mineral deposits simply by rubbing them with your finger. Avoid using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals, as they can damage the finish.

A well-made shower system from a reputable brand can last for 15 to 20 years or even longer. Systems made from solid brass and stainless steel will significantly outlast those with primarily plastic components. A lifetime warranty is a good indicator of expected longevity.
