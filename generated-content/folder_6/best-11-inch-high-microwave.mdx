## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 11 Inch High Microwave

Choosing the right microwave, especially for a space with height restrictions, involves more than just measuring. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to consider to ensure you get a product that fits your space and your lifestyle perfectly.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, define what you need. Are you looking for a primary microwave for daily cooking, or a secondary unit for an RV, dorm, or office? If it's for an RV, you'll want to look at models designed for built-in installation, like those from RecPro or Tough Grade. For a countertop, a model like the Commercial Chef or COSTWAY Retro is more suitable. Also, consider the accessories. Do you need a splatter cover to keep things clean or unbreakable plates for travel? Your primary use case will dictate the features and type of product you should focus on.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Microwaves and related accessories in this category have a wide price range. Simple, compact countertop models can be found for as little as $70-$100. More advanced units, especially those with convection or air frying capabilities designed for RVs, can range from $250 to over $400. Accessories like silicone covers or replacement plates typically fall in the $15-$30 range. Set a realistic budget based on your needs; if you only need to reheat coffee, a basic model is fine. If you plan to cook full meals in an RV, investing in a multi-functional unit is a wise choice.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to these specs:
*   **Wattage:** Higher wattage (900W-1000W) means faster cooking. Lower wattage (600W-700W) is fine for reheating but will take longer.
*   **Capacity:** Measured in cubic feet, this tells you the internal size. 0.6-0.7 cu. ft. is very compact, while 1.0-1.4 cu. ft. is more spacious.
*   **Controls:** Do you prefer simple mechanical dials or a digital touchpad with presets?
*   **Special Functions:** Convection, grilling, or air frying can replace other appliances, saving space.
*   **Turntable Size:** Ensure the turntable is large enough for your typical plates (an 11-inch plate fits in most, but check).

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for sturdy construction. For microwaves, a solid door and quality interior lining are important. For accessories like covers and plates, look for materials like food-grade, BPA-free silicone or durable wheat straw composite. Brands with a history of quality, like Nordic Ware, or those specializing in rugged use, like RecPro and Tough Grade for RVs, are often a good bet. Reading user reviews can also provide insight into the long-term durability of a product.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While a well-known brand isn't always a guarantee of quality, established manufacturers often have better customer support and more reliable products. Brands like Commercial Chef are known for simple, no-frills appliances. RecPro and Tough Grade have built a reputation in the RV community for creating products that withstand the rigors of travel. Sticking with reputable brands can provide peace of mind and an easier process if you ever need warranty service or replacement parts.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
This is paramount for the 11-inch high category. Always double-check the external dimensions (width, height, and depth) and compare them to your available space, leaving a few inches for ventilation around the unit. For built-in models, check the required cabinet cutout dimensions. For accessories, consider storage. A collapsible cover like the Kichwit model is a brilliant space-saver, as is the clever ceiling-mounted Nordic Ware cover.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 11 inch high microwave?

Investing in a microwave specifically designed for a height-restricted space, or in accessories that optimize its use, is an exceptionally wise decision for many. The primary value proposition lies in maximizing functionality within a constrained area. For those living in small apartments, dorms, or traveling in an RV, space is the ultimate luxury. A compact or built-in microwave that fits perfectly under a cabinet or into a designated slot is not just a convenience; it's a necessity that unlocks culinary possibilities that would otherwise be unavailable.

Compared to the alternative of going without a microwave, the time-saving benefits are immense. You can quickly reheat leftovers, defrost ingredients, and prepare simple meals without firing up a full-size oven, which saves both time and energy. For RV owners, a multi-functional unit that also works as a convection oven or air fryer is even more valuable. It consolidates multiple appliances into one footprint, freeing up precious counter and storage space. While some might balk at the price of a specialized RV model, the cost is easily justified when you consider the expense and space required for buying a separate microwave, toaster oven, and air fryer. Ultimately, these appliances and their companion accessories are about making life more efficient and enjoyable, regardless of the size of your kitchen.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 11 inch high microwave?

Choosing the best 11-inch high microwave or accessory requires a methodical approach. Follow these steps to ensure you make the right choice for your specific needs.

**1. Measure Your Space Meticulously:**
Before you do anything else, measure the height, width, and depth of the space where the microwave will go. Remember to account for ventilation—most manufacturers recommend at least 1-2 inches of clearance on the sides, top, and back. For over-the-range or built-in models, check the precise cutout dimensions required.

**2. Define Your Primary Use:**
Be honest about how you'll use it. Is it just for reheating coffee and popcorn? A simple, low-wattage model will suffice. Do you plan to cook actual meals, bake, or air fry? Then you need to invest in a higher-wattage, multi-functional unit. This decision will be the biggest factor in determining your budget and required features.

**3. Compare Key Features vs. Cost:**
Create a shortlist of 2-3 models that fit your space and needs. Compare them based on wattage, capacity, control type (dials vs. touchpad), and special functions. Ask yourself if paying extra for a convection feature is worth it for your cooking style. Often, a mid-range model offers the best balance of performance and price.

**4. Read User Reviews for Real-World Insights:**
Product descriptions are great, but user reviews reveal the truth about long-term durability and quirks. Look for reviews from people who have a similar living situation (e.g., other RV owners or apartment dwellers). They can offer valuable insights into installation challenges, noise levels, and how evenly the microwave cooks.

**5. Don't Forget the Accessories:**
Consider what will make your microwave experience better. A splatter cover is almost essential for keeping the interior clean. If you're buying for an RV or have kids, unbreakable plates are a smart purchase. A well-chosen accessory can be just as important as the microwave itself.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Forgetting Ventilation:** Squeezing a microwave into a tight space without proper airflow can cause it to overheat and fail prematurely.
*   **Buying Based on Looks Alone:** That stylish retro microwave might look great, but if it's underpowered for your needs, you'll be disappointed.
*   **Ignoring Installation Type:** A built-in microwave cannot be used on a countertop, and vice-versa. Ensure you buy the correct type for your setup.

### How do I maintain my 11 inch high microwave?
Maintenance is simple. Wipe up spills immediately to prevent them from baking on. Regularly clean the interior with a solution of water and vinegar or a microwave-safe cleaner. ### How long do 11 inch high microwave typically last?
A well-maintained compact microwave should last anywhere from 5 to 10 years. The lifespan depends on the quality of the unit and how heavily it is used.
