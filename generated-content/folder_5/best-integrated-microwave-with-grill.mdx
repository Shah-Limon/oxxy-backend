## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Integrated Microwave With Grill

Choosing the right integrated microwave with a grill requires careful consideration of your needs, space, and budget. Use this guide to help you navigate the key factors and make a choice you'll be happy with for years to come.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you plan to use it. Are you looking for a primary cooking appliance or a secondary unit for quick meals? If you want to replace your oven for small-batch baking, a model with convection and air frying capabilities like the Tough Grade or Chefman is ideal. If you simply want to add a crispy finish to pizzas and toast sandwiches, a standard microwave with a grill function like the Emerson models will suffice. Consider the types of dishes you cook most often to determine the features you'll actually use.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Integrated microwave grills can range significantly in price. You can find basic, compact models for around $150-$250. Mid-range options with more power, larger capacity, and additional features typically fall between $250 and $400. High-end, multi-functional units, especially those designed for built-in applications like RVs, can exceed $500. For those on a tight budget, clever accessories like the Maconee grill pans offer a taste of grilling functionality for under $40, provided you already own a microwave.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Power (Wattage):** For microwaves, higher wattage (1000W or more) means faster cooking. For the grill, look for at least 1000W for effective browning and crisping.
- **Capacity:** Measured in cubic feet, capacity determines how much you can fit inside. 0.8-1.0 cu. ft. is good for singles or couples, while 1.1 cu. ft. or more is better for families.
- **Presets and Auto-Cook Menus:** These take the guesswork out of cooking common items like popcorn, potatoes, and pizza.
- **Additional Functions:** Look for features like convection cooking, air frying, or combination modes that can greatly expand the appliance's versatility.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose integrated microwave with grill?

Choosing the best integrated microwave with a grill involves a methodical approach to ensure it meets your specific needs. Follow these steps to make an informed decision.

2.  **Define Your Primary Use:** How will you use it most? If it's mainly for reheating leftovers with an occasional crispy finish, a standard model with a good grill is sufficient.
