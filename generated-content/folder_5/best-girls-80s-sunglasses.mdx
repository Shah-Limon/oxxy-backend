## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Girls 80s Sunglasses

Choosing the right pair of 80s sunglasses involves more than just picking the brightest color. To get the best value and style, consider these key factors before you make a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine the primary use for the sunglasses. Are you looking for a fun, disposable accessory for a one-time 80s themed party? In that case, bulk packs of shutter shades or novelty glasses are perfect. If you need sunglasses for a child's everyday wear, prioritize durability and UV protection. For sports or active use, look for designs with a secure fit, lightweight frames, and impact-resistant lenses.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($5-$30 ranges)
The price for girls' 80s sunglasses can vary significantly. Novelty party packs can cost as little as $5-$15 for a dozen pairs, making them highly affordable. A single pair of durable, everyday retro-style sunglasses with UV protection will typically range from $10 to $20. For higher-end options with features like polarized lenses or specialized sports designs, you can expect to pay between $20 and $30. Set your budget based on the intended use and desired features.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
The most critical feature for any sunglasses, even novelty ones, is UV protection. Look for lenses that offer UV400 protection, which blocks 100% of harmful UVA and UVB rays. For enhanced visual clarity and reduced glare, especially near water or on sunny days, consider polarized lenses. Also, check the material of the frame and lenses—polycarbonate is a good choice for durability and light weight.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Examine the construction of the sunglasses. For kids' glasses, sturdy plastic frames and strong hinges are essential to withstand rough handling. The lenses should be scratch-resistant if they are intended for frequent use. For party favors, while durability is less of a concern, you still want glasses that won't break the moment they are put on. Check user reviews for feedback on how well the glasses hold up over time.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While many 80s-style sunglasses come from generic or lesser-known brands, some established names specialize in children's eyewear or retro styles. A brand with a history of producing quality products is more likely to offer reliable UV protection and durable construction. Reading reviews can give you insight into a brand's reputation, even if it's not a household name.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the size and fit, especially when buying for a child. Product descriptions often provide dimensions like lens width, bridge size, and temple length. Ensure these measurements are appropriate for the intended wearer. For storage, some sunglasses come with a soft microfiber pouch, which is great for preventing scratches and keeping them clean when not in use.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose girls 80s sunglasses?

Choosing the best girls' 80s sunglasses is simple if you follow a few steps. By thinking through your needs and priorities, you can find the perfect pair.

**1. Define the Purpose:** First, ask yourself why you need the sunglasses. Is it for a single party, regular wear, or sports? This will be the most important factor in your decision. Party glasses can be cheap and cheerful, while daily-wear glasses need to be durable and protective.

**2. Prioritize Eye Protection:** For any sunglasses that will be worn outdoors for more than a few minutes, UV protection is non-negotiable. Look for a label or description that explicitly states "UV400" or "100% UV protection." This ensures the wearer's eyes are shielded from harmful rays.

**3. Consider the Style:** The 80s had many iconic styles. Do you want classic wayfarers, fun shutter shades, or sporty wraparounds? Choose a style that fits the occasion and the wearer's personality. For kids, bright neon colors are often a huge hit.

**4. Check the Size and Fit:** Read the product dimensions carefully. Sunglasses that are too big will fall off, and ones that are too small will be uncomfortable. Look for measurements or age recommendations to ensure a good fit.

**5. Read User Reviews:** Before buying, scan through recent user reviews. They provide real-world insight into the product's durability, comfort, and appearance. Pay attention to comments about quality and whether the product matches the description.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring UV Protection:** Never assume a dark lens provides UV protection. Always check for the UV400 rating.
*   **Buying the Wrong Size:** Don't guess the size. Check the dimensions, especially for children, to avoid a poor fit.
*   **Focusing Only on Price:** The cheapest option isn't always the best, especially if it breaks easily or lacks safety features. Balance cost with quality and purpose.
