## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Door Handles

Choosing the perfect door handle involves more than just picking a style you like. To ensure you get a product that is secure, durable, and fits your needs, consider these key factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine the handle's function. Is it for an exterior front door, a private bedroom, or a simple closet?
*   **Entry Handles:** Require a keyed lock or deadbolt for security.
*   **Privacy Handles:** Have a turn-button lock on the inside, ideal for bathrooms and bedrooms.
*   **Passage Handles:** Do not lock and are used for hallways and closets.
*   **Dummy Handles:** Are single-sided and used for pulling or pushing, like on a pantry or closet door.
Also, check your door's specifications: thickness (typically 1-3/8" to 1-3/4"), and backset (the distance from the door's edge to the center of the hole, usually 2-3/8" or 2-3/4").

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Door handle prices can vary widely.
*   **Budget-Friendly (under $20 per handle):** Often found in multi-packs, these are great for outfitting multiple interior doors. They offer basic functionality and style.
*   **Mid-Range ($20 - $70 per handle):** This range includes higher-quality materials, better finishes, and enhanced features like antimicrobial coatings or advanced security. Most reputable brands fall in this category.
*   **Premium ($70+ per handle):** These are typically high-security entry handlesets from top-tier brands like Schlage, featuring superior materials, designer finishes, and the highest durability ratings.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that add convenience and security.
*   **Reversible Handing:** Allows the lever to be installed on both left-handed and right-handed doors.
*   **Adjustable Latch:** Ensures the handle will fit standard door preparations without modification.
*   **Security Ratings:** Look for BHMA (Builders Hardware Manufacturers Association) certifications. Grade 3 is standard for residential, Grade 2 is better, and Grade 1 is the highest level of security.
*   **Specialty Keys:** Features like Kwikset's SmartKey allow you to re-key your own locks.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The material and finish determine a handle's longevity.
*   **Material:** Solid brass and zinc alloy are excellent choices, offering strength and corrosion resistance. Avoid handles with significant plastic components in the internal mechanism.
*   **Finish:** An electroplated or PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) finish will be far more resistant to scratches, chipping, and fading than a simple paint or powder coat. This is especially important for exterior handles exposed to the elements.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like Schlage and Kwikset have a long history of producing reliable, high-quality hardware. They often back their products with extensive warranties, such as a lifetime mechanical and finish warranty, and provide excellent customer support. While newer brands can offer great value, a trusted name often provides greater peace of mind.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While not portable, the size and projection of the handle matter. A large, ornate handle might not be suitable for a narrow hallway where it could be bumped into. Ensure the lever or knob provides enough clearance for your hand to operate it comfortably without scraping your knuckles on the door frame. The rosette (the plate behind the handle) size should also be considered to ensure it covers any old marks or holes from previous hardware.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy door handles?

Investing in quality door handles is one of the wisest, most impactful decisions a homeowner can make. It's an upgrade that pays dividends in security, functionality, and aesthetics every single day. While it might seem like a minor detail, door handles are one of the most frequently touched and used items in any home. A cheap, flimsy handle can lead to constant frustration, from sagging levers and squeaky mechanisms to premature failure that could leave you locked in or out.

Compared to the alternative of keeping old, worn-out, or mismatched hardware, new door handles offer significant long-term benefits. They enhance your home's security, especially entry handles with modern features like anti-pick technology and robust deadbolts. Aesthetically, they can completely transform the look and feel of a space, providing a cohesive and updated design that increases your home's overall value. The cost, when spread over the lifetime of the product—often guaranteed by a lifetime warranty from reputable brands—is minimal. A quality door handle isn't just an expense; it's a small, one-time investment in your daily comfort, safety, and satisfaction with your living space.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose door handles?

Choosing the best door handles for your home can be a simple and rewarding process if you follow a structured approach. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you make the right choice.

**1. Assess Your Needs for Each Door:**
Go through your home room by room and list what type of handle each door needs.
*   **Exterior Doors (Front, Back, Garage):** Need a keyed entry handle and deadbolt for maximum security.
*   **Bedrooms & Bathrooms:** Need a privacy handle with an interior lock.
*   **Hallways & Closets:** Need a non-locking passage handle.
*   **Pantry or French Doors:** May need dummy handles (non-turning pulls).

**2. Measure Your Doors:**
Before you shop, get two key measurements from your doors:
*   **Backset:** The distance from the edge of the door to the center of the handle's borehole. This is typically 2-3/8" or 2-3/4". Most new handles have an adjustable latch for both.
*   **Door Thickness:** Standard doors are 1-3/8" to 1-3/4" thick. Ensure the handle you choose is compatible.

**3. Define Your Style and Finish:**
Consider your home's overall aesthetic. Are you aiming for modern, traditional, rustic, or minimalist? Choose a style (lever vs. knob, square vs. round rosette) and finish (e.g., matte black, satin nickel, aged bronze) that complements your decor, light fixtures, and cabinetry. Consistency is key to a polished look.

**4. Set a Realistic Budget:**
Decide how much you're willing to spend. Remember that investing a little more in a reputable brand like Schlage or Kwikset can pay off in durability and longevity. If you're outfitting the whole house, look for value packs from brands like Plustool or Sopoby.

**5. Prioritize Quality and Security:**
For exterior doors, look for a BHMA Grade 1 or 2 rating. For all handles, favor solid metal construction (zinc alloy, brass) over plastic parts. Check for features like anti-sag mechanisms and durable finishes (electroplated is better than painted).

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Handing:** While most modern levers are reversible, some are not. Double-check if you need a left-handed or right-handed lever.
*   **Buying Inconsistent Styles:** Don't mix and match too many different styles and finishes; it can make your home feel disjointed.
*   **Forgetting the Deadbolt:** For front doors, a handleset is often just the grip; you need to ensure you have a compatible, high-quality deadbolt.

## FAQ

### What is the best door handles for beginners?
For beginners, the best door handles are those with a simple, all-in-one installation kit and clear instructions. Brands like Kwikset and Plustool are excellent choices as they often feature reversible levers and adjustable latches, which removes the guesswork. Look for models that only require a screwdriver for installation, making the process straightforward and accessible for any DIY skill level.

### How much should I spend on door handles?
For interior passage or privacy handles, expect to spend between $15 and $40 per handle for good quality. For a secure exterior front entry handleset with a deadbolt, a reasonable budget is between $60 and $200. Investing in a reputable brand is often worth the slightly higher cost for the added durability and warranty protection.

### What features are most important in door handles?
For exterior doors, security is paramount; look for a strong deadbolt and a high BHMA rating (Grade 1 or 2). For all handles, durability is key, so choose solid metal construction. Features like reversible handing and an adjustable latch are important for ensuring an easy fit. For high-touch areas, an antimicrobial coating like Microban is a valuable bonus.

### How do I maintain my door handles?
Maintaining door handles is simple. Regularly wipe them down with a soft, damp cloth and mild soap to remove dirt and oils. Avoid using harsh chemical cleaners or abrasive materials, as they can damage the finish. Periodically check that all screws are tight to prevent wobbling, and lubricate the latch mechanism with graphite lubricant if it starts to stick.

### Are expensive door handles worth it?
Often, yes. Expensive door handles from premium brands like Schlage typically use superior materials, more durable finishes, and have higher security ratings (e.g., BHMA Grade 1). They are engineered to last a lifetime and are backed by robust warranties. While a budget handle works, a more expensive one is an investment in long-term reliability, smoother operation, and better security.

### What brands make the best door handles?
Schlage and Kwikset are two of the most trusted and reputable brands in the industry, known for their quality, security features, and warranties. For excellent value, especially in multi-packs, brands like Plustool, Sopoby, and Estmoon offer modern styles and solid construction at a more accessible price point.

### How long do door handles typically last?
A high-quality door handle from a reputable brand should last for decades, and many come with a lifetime mechanical warranty. The finish may show wear over time, especially on exterior handles exposed to weather, but the internal mechanism should remain functional. Cheaper, lower-quality handles may fail in as little as a few years.

### Can I find good door handles on a budget?
Absolutely. You can find excellent door handles on a budget by looking for multi-packs from brands like Estmoon or Sopoby. These packs significantly lower the cost per handle while still offering durable materials like zinc alloy, modern finishes, and reliable privacy or passage functions for interior doors.
