## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Full Size Microwaves

Choosing the right full-size microwave involves more than just picking the largest one. It's about finding the right balance of size, power, features, and durability to match your specific needs. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to consider to ensure you make a wise investment.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, assess how you'll use the microwave. Are you part of a large family that needs to reheat big casseroles? Do you often cook for guests? Or do you primarily use it for defrosting meat and making popcorn? A family of five will benefit from a capacity of 2.0 cu. ft. or more, while a couple might find a 1.2 to 1.6 cu. ft. model perfectly adequate. Also, measure your available counter space, including height, width, and depth, ensuring you leave a few inches of clearance on all sides for proper ventilation.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Full-size microwaves can range in price significantly. You can find basic, large-capacity models for around $150, while high-end units with advanced features like sensor cooking, convection capabilities, and premium finishes can cost $300 or more. Set a realistic budget based on the features that are most important to you. A higher price often gets you more power (wattage), smarter cooking technology, and better build quality, which can be a worthwhile long-term investment.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Wattage:** This determines cooking speed. For a full-size microwave, look for at least 1000 watts. Models with 1200 watts or more will cook and reheat food noticeably faster and more evenly.
- **Sensor Cooking:** This is a must-have feature for convenience. A built-in sensor detects the humidity released by food and automatically adjusts the cooking time and power level. This prevents overcooking and delivers perfect results.
- **Turntable:** A large, removable turntable (12 inches or more) ensures even cooking. Some advanced models offer features like a position memory turntable or the option to turn it off for rectangular dishes.
- **Presets and Controls:** Look for intuitive controls and one-touch buttons for common tasks like popcorn, beverage, and defrost. An easy-to-read digital display is also essential.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The build quality of a microwave affects its longevity and performance. Stainless steel exteriors are not only stylish but also more durable and easier to clean than plastic. Check the feel of the door—it should open and close smoothly and securely. A sturdy handle is generally more durable than a push-button release mechanism. The quality of the interior finish also matters; some have easy-clean coatings that resist splatters and stains.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with reputable brands like Toshiba, Panasonic, GE, and Whirlpool is often a safe bet. These companies have a long history of producing reliable appliances and typically offer better customer support and warranty coverage. Reading user reviews for specific models can also provide valuable insight into their long-term reliability and any common issues.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While "full-size" implies a large appliance, their external dimensions can vary. Double-check the product specifications (width x depth x height) against your available space. Remember to account for the space the door needs to swing open fully. If you have limited counter space, you might consider a mid-size model that still offers a generous capacity without the massive footprint.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy full size microwaves?

Investing in a full-size microwave is an exceptionally wise decision for anyone who regularly cooks for more than one or two people. The primary advantage is capacity. Unlike their compact counterparts, which struggle with anything larger than a dinner plate, a full-size model (typically 1.6 to 2.2 cubic feet) can easily accommodate large casserole dishes, family-sized platters, and even whole poultry. This versatility transforms the microwave from a simple reheating device into a genuine cooking tool, capable of handling significant portions of a meal.

Furthermore, full-size microwaves almost always come with higher wattage, usually between 1000 and 1250 watts. This increased power translates directly to faster, more even cooking. Food is heated more thoroughly without the frustrating hot and cold spots common in less powerful units. When you compare this to smaller microwaves or alternative appliances like toaster ovens, the speed and convenience for large-scale tasks are unmatched. While the initial cost may be higher, the long-term benefits—time saved, better cooking results, and the ability to prepare larger meals efficiently—provide a substantial return on investment, making it an essential appliance for any busy, modern kitchen.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose full size microwaves?

Choosing the best full-size microwave requires a methodical approach to ensure it meets your specific kitchen needs and lifestyle. Follow these steps to make an informed choice.

**1. Measure Your Space First:** Before you fall in love with a model, measure the countertop or shelf space where it will live. Record the maximum width, depth, and height. Remember to add 2-3 inches of clearance around the back and sides for proper ventilation to prevent overheating.

**2. Define Your Primary Use:** Be honest about how you'll use it. If you're a family of five, capacity (2.0+ cu. ft.) and power (1200W+) are paramount. If you're a foodie who loves perfect results, prioritize models with smart sensor cooking. If convenience is key, look for a wide variety of one-touch presets.

**3. Set a Clear Budget:** Determine your price range. Entry-level full-size models start around $150, mid-range models with smart sensors are typically $200-$250, and premium, high-power units can exceed $300. Knowing your budget helps narrow down the options.

**4. Compare Key Features:** Create a checklist of must-have features. This might include:
- **Smart Sensor:** For automatic, perfect reheating.
- **High Wattage:** For speed and evenness.
- **Specific Presets:** For your go-to items like popcorn or pizza.
- **Convenience Features:** Such as a mute button, position memory turntable, or an easy-clean interior.

**5. Read Recent User Reviews:** Once you have a few models in mind, read reviews from the last 6-12 months. Look for patterns in feedback regarding reliability, noise level, and customer service experiences. This provides real-world insight beyond the marketing claims.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Ignoring Dimensions:** Buying a microwave that doesn't fit is a frustrating and common error.
- **Focusing Only on Wattage:** While important, a high-wattage microwave with poor energy distribution is still a bad choice. Look for features like inverter technology or good user reviews on cooking evenness.
- **Overlooking the Interface:** A confusing control panel will lead to daily frustration. Choose a model with an intuitive layout.

### How much should I spend on full size microwaves?
You can expect to spend between $150 and $300 for a quality full-size microwave. Models in the $150-$200 range will offer large capacity and solid power. Moving into the $200-$300+ range typically gets you more advanced features like highly accurate smart sensors, higher wattage for faster cooking, premium finishes like black stainless steel, and innovative convenience features like a position memory turntable.

### What features are most important in full size microwaves?
The three most important features are capacity, wattage, and sensor cooking. A large capacity (at least 1.6 cu. ft.) ensures you can fit family-sized dishes. High wattage (1000W or more) guarantees faster and more even cooking. Sensor cooking technology is a game-changer, as it automatically adjusts time and power for perfect results, taking all the guesswork out of reheating and cooking.

### How do I maintain my full size microwaves?
Maintaining your microwave is simple. Wipe up spills immediately to prevent them from baking on. For a deep clean, place a microwave-safe bowl with a mixture of water and vinegar or lemon juice inside and heat for a few minutes. The steam will loosen grime, which you can then easily wipe away. Regularly wash the glass turntable by hand or in the dishwasher if it's dishwasher-safe.

### Are expensive full size microwaves worth it?
Yes, they can be worth it if you value speed, precision, and convenience. More expensive models like the Panasonic NN-SD987SA offer higher wattage for faster cooking and often include inverter technology for more even heating. They also tend to have better build quality and more sophisticated sensors, leading to better cooking results and a longer lifespan. If you use your microwave frequently for more than just popcorn, the investment is often justified.

### What brands make the best full size microwaves?

### How long do full size microwaves typically last?
A good quality full-size microwave should last between 7 and 10 years with regular use. The lifespan depends on the build quality, how frequently it's used, and how well it's maintained. Models from reputable brands are generally built with more durable components, which contributes to a longer operational life.

### Can I find good full size microwaves on a budget?
Absolutely. Brands like BLACK+DECKER and Farberware offer excellent value, providing large capacities and strong performance at a more accessible price point. You may forego some premium features like advanced sensors or the highest wattage, but you can still get a powerful and reliable appliance that meets the needs of most households without breaking the bank.
