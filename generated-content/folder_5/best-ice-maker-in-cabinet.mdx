## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Ice Maker In Cabinet

Choosing the right under-counter ice maker requires careful consideration of your specific needs and kitchen layout. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to ensure you select a machine that you'll be happy with for years to come.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine how you'll use the ice maker. Are you a large family that goes through a lot of ice daily, an avid entertainer who hosts frequent parties, or a small business owner? Your daily ice consumption will dictate the required production capacity (measured in pounds per 24 hours). Also, consider the type of ice you prefer. Most under-counter models produce clear, square cubes, which are great for general use, but specialty models like the GE Opal produce chewable nugget ice.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Under-counter ice makers are a significant investment. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose ice maker in cabinet?

Choosing the best ice maker in cabinet involves a methodical approach to match the right product to your specific needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

**Step 1: Assess Your Ice Needs**
Before you even look at models, quantify your usage. How many people are in your household? Do you host large gatherings often? A family of four might be happy with a 50 lbs/day machine, while a frequent host should look for something in the 80-100 lbs/day range.

**Step 2: Measure Your Space and Check Utilities**
Accurately measure the height, width, and depth of the cabinet space. Remember to account for ventilation clearance (usually 1-2 inches around the sides and back). Crucially, identify your utility access. You will need a cold water line and a standard electrical outlet. Most importantly, determine your drainage situation. If you have a floor drain nearby, a gravity drain model will work. If not, you must filter your search for models with a built-in drain pump.

**Step 3: Compare Key Features and Set a Budget**
With your size and drain requirements in mind, start comparing models. Look at production capacity, storage bin size, cycle time, and control features (e.g., adjustable ice thickness, self-cleaning). Set a budget, but be flexible. Sometimes spending a little more on a model with a drain pump or better insulation can save you installation headaches and energy costs down the line.

**Step 4: Read Reviews and Consider Brand Reputation**
Look for user reviews that discuss long-term reliability, noise levels, and customer service experiences. A reputable brand may cost more upfront but often provides better support and a more durable product.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Ignoring the Drain:** The most common mistake is buying a gravity drain model without a floor drain. This leads to costly plumbing work or an unusable machine.
- **Forgetting Ventilation:** Squeezing an ice maker into a tight space without proper airflow will cause it to work harder, run less efficiently, and fail prematurely.
- **Underestimating Capacity:** It's always better to have a little more ice-making capacity than you think you need. Running out of ice during a party is a major inconvenience.
