## What Is the Best Way to Choose danco all toilets?

Choosing the best Danco toilet kit is a straightforward process if you follow a few logical steps. Taking a methodical approach will ensure you buy the right product the first time and achieve a successful repair.

**Step 1: Diagnose the Problem**
Before you shop, understand what's wrong with your toilet.
*   **Constantly Running Water:** This is often a leaky flapper or a fill valve that isn't shutting off. A dual-flush converter or a complete repair kit is a great solution.
*   **Weak or Incomplete Flush:** This could be a misadjusted flapper chain, low water level in the tank, or a failing flush valve. A complete kit like the Danco 12094 can restore flushing power.
*   **"Ghost" Flushing:** If your toilet flushes by itself, you have a slow leak from the tank into the bowl, almost always caused by a bad flapper seal. A kit with a new flush valve seal is needed.
*   **Noisy Refill:** An old ballcock-style fill valve is likely the culprit. Any kit with a modern fill valve will solve this.
*   **Leaking at the Base:** This is a flange or wax ring issue. You need the Danco HydroSeat or the All-in-One Installation Kit.

**Step 2: Check Your Toilet's Specifications**
This is the most critical step. Open the tank and find the following:
*   **Flush Valve Size:** Is it a standard 2-inch opening or a larger 3-inch one? Most Danco kits fit 2-inch valves.
*   **Toilet Brand:** Is it a Mansfield? If so, be aware that many Danco conversion kits are not compatible.
*   **Tank Clearance:** Measure from the top of the overflow pipe/flush valve to the inside of the tank lid. You typically need 10 inches of clearance.
*   **Gallons Per Flush (GPF):** Some newer 1.28 GPF toilets have specific requirements. Check the kit's compatibility.

**Step 3: Match the Kit to Your Needs and Skill Level**
With your diagnosis and specs in hand, select the right kit.
*   **For Beginners:** A simple dual-flush converter like the HYR271T or HYR270 is incredibly easy to install.
*   **For a Full Upgrade:** The HYR451T or HYR460, which include a new fill valve, offer a complete performance overhaul.
*   **For a Total Fix:** If multiple parts seem old or worn, the Danco 12094 replaces everything for a like-new feel.
*   **For a New Installation:** The All-in-One Installation Kit is the only choice to ensure a mess-free, easy setup.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Compatibility:** Don't just assume a kit will fit. Skipping Step 2 is the number one reason for a failed DIY repair.
*   **Buying Only One Part:** If your toilet is over 10 years old, its internal components are likely all nearing the end of their life. Replacing just the flapper might solve the immediate problem, but the fill valve could fail next month. A complete kit is often a smarter long-term choice.
*   **Over-tightening Connections:** Plastic fittings can crack if you over-tighten them. Hand-tight plus a quarter-turn with a wrench is usually sufficient.
