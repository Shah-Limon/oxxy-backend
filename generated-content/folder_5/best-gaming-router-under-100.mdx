## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Gaming Router Under 100

Choosing the right gaming router is crucial for a smooth online experience. While our title focuses on the popular "under 100" category, we've included a range of options to help you understand the market. Use this guide to identify the features that matter most to you.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, assess your specific situation. What is your internet plan's maximum speed? A 10Gbps router is useless if you only have a 100Mbps plan. How many devices will be connected at once? A family of four with smartphones, laptops, smart TVs, and consoles needs a more robust router than a single person. Consider the types of games you play. Fast-paced shooters like Call of Duty are more sensitive to latency (ping) than slower strategy games. Your primary need will dictate which features are essential and which are just nice to have.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
While it's possible to find a good gaming router under $100, it's important to have realistic expectations.
- **$30-$70 Range:** In this bracket, you'll find solid entry-level AC (Wi-Fi 5) routers like the TP-Link Archer A54. These are a huge step up from ISP-provided equipment and are great for internet plans up to 100-300 Mbps.
- **$70-$150 Range:** This is the sweet spot for many, offering powerful Wi-Fi 6 routers like the GL.iNet Flint 2. These routers provide lower latency, better performance with multiple devices, and often include multi-gig ports for faster internet plans.
- **$150+ Range:** This is premium territory. Here you'll find the latest Wi-Fi 6E and Wi-Fi 7 routers with multiple multi-gig ports, dedicated gaming bands, and advanced software. These are for enthusiasts who want the absolute best performance and to future-proof their network.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Wi-Fi Standard:** Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac) is the minimum. Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) is the current standard, offering better efficiency and speed. Wi-Fi 6E adds the 6 GHz band for less interference. Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be) is the cutting edge, offering the highest speeds and lowest latency.
- **Bands:** Dual-band is standard. Tri-band or Quad-band routers add extra 5 GHz or 6 GHz bands to reduce congestion, which is great for dedicated gaming traffic.
- **QoS (Quality of Service):** This is a critical gaming feature. It allows the router to prioritize gaming traffic over other data, like a file download, ensuring your game has the bandwidth it needs.
- **Ports:** Look for Gigabit Ethernet ports (1000 Mbps) as a minimum. If you have an internet plan faster than 1Gbps, you'll need a router with a 2.5G, 5G, or 10G WAN port to take full advantage of it.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A gaming router is an investment that runs 24/7, so build quality matters. Look for routers with ample ventilation to prevent overheating, which can throttle performance. Sturdy, well-designed external antennas often provide better signal direction and coverage than internal ones. Check reviews for comments on the longevity and reliability of the hardware.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with reputable brands like ASUS, TP-Link, NETGEAR, and GL.iNet is a wise choice. These companies have a long history of producing reliable networking gear and, crucially, provide ongoing firmware updates. These updates are essential for patching security vulnerabilities and improving performance and stability over the life of the router.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
