### What is the difference between a C-clamp and locking pliers?
Locking pliers (like curved or straight jaw types) are designed for gripping and turning objects, such as pipes or rounded-off bolts. Both use a similar locking mechanism but serve different primary purposes.

### Are swivel pads necessary on C-clamps?
Swivel pads are not always necessary, but they are highly recommended when working with softer materials like wood or finished surfaces. The pads distribute pressure more evenly and have a soft face that prevents marring or denting the workpiece. For rough metalwork or welding, standard steel jaws are perfectly fine, but for any kind of finish work, swivel pads are a huge advantage.
