## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Built In Microwave Above Stove

Choosing the right over-the-range microwave involves more than just picking a brand. It's about finding the perfect fit for your kitchen's layout, your cooking habits, and your budget. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to consider to ensure you make a confident and satisfying purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, assess your kitchen and your cooking style. Measure the space above your stove meticulously—width, height, and depth. The standard width is 30 inches, but it's crucial to confirm. Consider the vertical clearance; a slimline model might be better if you have low cabinets or need more space over your cooktop. Think about what you cook most often. Do you need a large capacity for family meals? Do you need advanced features like convection or air frying to save counter space? Matching the appliance to your specific needs is the first step to success.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Over-the-range microwaves have a wide price spectrum. You can find basic, reliable models in the $250-$400 range. These will cover all essential functions like reheating, defrosting, and have a standard 300 CFM vent. Mid-range models, from $400-$650, often include upgrades like more powerful 400 CFM fans, sensor cooking, larger capacities, and more premium finishes. High-end and smart models can range from $650 to over $800, offering features like industry-leading ventilation (500+ CFM), Wi-Fi connectivity, voice control, and multi-functional cooking modes like convection and air frying. Set a budget that aligns with the features you truly need.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Focus on the specifications that impact daily use.
*   **Wattage:** Look for 1000 watts or more for fast, even cooking.
*   **Ventilation Power (CFM):** CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) measures how much air the fan moves. 300 CFM is standard and fine for most electric ranges. If you have a gas range or do a lot of high-heat cooking, aim for 400 CFM or higher to effectively manage smoke and odors.
*   **Sensor Cooking:** This feature uses sensors to detect steam and humidity, automatically adjusting cooking time and power. It takes the guesswork out of reheating and cooking for perfect results.
*   **Capacity:** Measured in cubic feet, capacity ranges from about 1.1 cu. ft. (slimline) to 2.2 cu. ft. A capacity of 1.7 to 1.9 cu. ft. is a good sweet spot for most families.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for models with a stainless steel exterior, which is durable and easy to clean. A fingerprint-resistant finish is a huge plus for maintaining a clean look. Inside, a ceramic or enamel interior is often easier to clean than standard painted metal. Check the handle and door construction; they should feel solid and sturdy, as they are the most frequently used parts. Reading user reviews can provide insight into the long-term durability of specific models.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with well-known brands like Samsung, Frigidaire, BLACK+DECKER, and Midea often provides a better experience. These companies generally offer better build quality, more reliable performance, and more accessible customer service and parts availability. A longer warranty, like the 2-year warranty offered by Empava, can also be a strong indicator of a manufacturer's confidence in their product's durability.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While these microwaves aren't portable, their dimensions are critical. Double-check the width (usually 30 inches) to ensure it fits your cabinet opening. Pay close attention to the depth to ensure it doesn't protrude too far from your cabinetry, and the height to ensure you have adequate clearance above your cooktop (most manufacturers recommend 13-16 inches of clearance). Also, consider the door swing. If the microwave is installed next to a wall, a zero-clearance door feature can be a lifesaver.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy built in microwave above stove?

Investing in a built-in microwave above the stove is an exceptionally wise decision for most modern kitchens. Its primary advantage is its brilliant space-saving design. By combining two essential appliances—a microwave and a range hood—into a single unit, it frees up valuable counter space that a traditional countertop microwave would occupy. This creates a more open, uncluttered, and efficient workspace for meal preparation.

Compared to the alternative of having a separate countertop microwave and a dedicated range hood, the over-the-range (OTR) solution is often more cost-effective and creates a more streamlined, integrated look. While a high-end, dedicated range hood might offer superior ventilation, the fans in modern OTR microwaves (ranging from 300 to over 500 CFM) are more than powerful enough for the vast majority of home cooking needs, effectively removing smoke, steam, and odors.

The long-term benefits are significant. You gain a more functional kitchen layout, improved lighting over your cooktop thanks to the built-in task lights, and a cohesive aesthetic. While the initial cost and installation might be higher than a simple countertop model, the value added in terms of space, functionality, and kitchen appeal makes it a worthwhile investment that enhances your daily cooking experience.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose built in microwave above stove?

Choosing the best built-in microwave above the stove requires a methodical approach to ensure it meets all your needs. Follow this step-by-step guide for a seamless selection process.

**1. Measure Your Space Accurately:** Before anything else, measure the width, height, and depth of the cabinet opening above your range. The standard width is 30 inches, but never assume. Note the distance to adjacent walls to account for door swing.

**2. Define Your Cooking Needs and Priorities:** Are you a power user who needs high capacity and advanced features, or do you primarily use a microwave for reheating and popcorn? Make a list of must-have features. Is sensor cooking essential? Do you need a powerful vent for a gas stove? Do you want smart features or multi-cooking functions like an air fryer?

**3. Set a Realistic Budget:** Based on your feature list, determine a budget. Remember to factor in potential installation costs if you aren't doing it yourself. A higher budget will get you more power (CFM and wattage) and advanced features.

**4. Research and Compare Models:** Use your criteria to narrow down the options. Pay close attention to:
*   **CFM Rating:** Match this to your cooktop type (higher for gas).
*   **Wattage:** Aim for 1000W or more.
*   **Capacity (Cu. Ft.):** Choose based on your family size and dish sizes.
*   **User Reviews:** Look for patterns in feedback regarding reliability, noise level, and ease of use.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
*   **Ignoring Ventilation Needs:** Don't buy a low-CFM model if you do a lot of frying or have a gas cooktop.
*   **Forgetting Door Swing:** Ensure the door won't hit a wall or cabinet when opened fully. Look for zero-clearance doors if needed.
*   **Prioritizing Looks Over Function:** A sleek design is great, but make sure it has the power and features you'll use daily.

**6. When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
*   **Choose a Budget Option:** If you use a microwave for basic tasks like reheating, defrosting, and making popcorn, a reliable budget model will serve you perfectly well.
*   **Invest in an Upgrade:** If you want to save counter space by consolidating appliances (air fryer, convection oven), have a high-output gas range requiring powerful ventilation, or are passionate about smart home integration, investing in a premium model is a wise choice that will pay dividends in convenience and performance.

### How much should I spend on built in microwave above stove?
You should expect to spend between $250 and $650 for a quality over-the-range microwave. Models in the $250-$400 range offer solid basic functionality and standard ventilation. Stepping up to the $400-$650 range typically gets you more powerful ventilation (400+ CFM), sensor cooking, and larger capacities. Premium smart or multi-functional models can exceed this, but this range covers the best options for most households.

### What features are most important in built in microwave above stove?
The most important features are adequate ventilation (CFM), sufficient wattage (at least 1000W), and the right capacity for your needs. After that, sensor cooking is a highly valuable feature that automates cooking for better results. Good cooktop lighting and easy-to-use controls are also crucial for daily convenience and a positive user experience.

### How do I maintain my built in microwave above stove?
To maintain your microwave, regularly clean the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent to prevent food buildup. Most importantly, clean the grease filters underneath at least once a month. Many are dishwasher-safe. For charcoal filters used in recirculating setups, replace them every six months to ensure effective odor removal. Wiping down the exterior with a stainless steel cleaner will keep it looking new.

### Are expensive built in microwave above stove worth it?
Expensive models can be worth it if you will use their advanced features. If you need powerful 500+ CFM ventilation for a professional-style gas range, want the convenience of Wi-Fi and voice controls, or desire to replace countertop appliances with an all-in-one air fryer/convection model, the extra cost is justified by the enhanced performance and functionality. Otherwise, a mid-range model is sufficient for most users.

### What brands make the best built in microwave above stove?
Several brands are known for making high-quality over-the-range microwaves. Samsung is a leader in technology and smart features. Frigidaire and BLACK+DECKER are renowned for their reliable, user-friendly, and durable appliances that offer great value. Brands like ThermoMate and GASLAND are emerging with high-capacity, feature-rich models that compete strongly with established names.

### How long do built in microwave above stove typically last?
An over-the-range microwave typically lasts about 7 to 10 years, depending on usage and maintenance. The key component that can fail is the magnetron, which generates the microwaves. Regular cleaning, especially of the grease filters to ensure proper airflow and prevent overheating, can help extend the lifespan of your appliance. Choosing a model with a good warranty can also provide peace of mind.

### Can I find good built in microwave above stove on a budget?
Absolutely. You can find excellent budget-friendly options, like the **Farberware 1.7 Cu. Ft. OTR Microwave**. These models provide all the core functionality you need, such as 1000W of power, multiple cooking programs, and effective ventilation, without the high cost of premium features like smart connectivity or convection cooking. They offer fantastic value and reliable performance for everyday use.
