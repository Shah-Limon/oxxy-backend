### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Premium paints like the Magnolia Home collection come at a higher price point, typically ranging from $50-$70 per gallon. While this is more than budget brands, the investment often pays off in better coverage, durability, and a more beautiful finish, potentially saving you money on extra coats or future repaints. Buy the right amount of paint for your project to avoid waste and storage issues. A gallon is standard for painting an average-sized room. A quart is perfect for a large piece of furniture, an accent wall, or trim work. An aerosol can is designed for small decorative items. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose joanna gaines paint colors for living room?

Choosing the right paint color is a process. Following these steps can help you select the perfect shade with confidence.

**Step 1: Gather Inspiration and Assess Your Space**
Before looking at colors, understand the room itself. Note the amount of natural light it gets throughout the day. Look at your existing furniture, flooring, and decor. What are the fixed elements and undertones you need to work with? Gather inspiration from magazines, Pinterest, or the 'The Perfectly Imperfect Home' book to define the mood you want to create—cozy and warm, or light and airy?

**Step 2: Narrow Down Your Choices**
Based on your desired mood, select 3-5 potential colors from the Magnolia Home palette. For a warm and inviting living room, consider neutrals like 'Gatherings' or 'Shiplap'. For a brighter, more open feel, 'Castle Cream' is a great option. If you want a subtle touch of color, look at shades like 'Emmie's Room' or 'Rainy Days'.

**Step 3: Always Test with Samples**
This is the most critical step. Never choose a color based on a small paint chip or a picture online. Purchase sample pots and paint large swatches (at least 2x2 feet) on different walls in your living room. Observe the colors at different times of day—morning, noon, and night—under both natural and artificial light. This is the only way to see how a color will truly look and feel in your specific environment.

**Step-4: Consider the Finish**
The sheen of the paint affects both its appearance and durability. An eggshell or satin finish is ideal for living rooms. They have a slight luster that reflects a small amount of light, and they are significantly more durable and easier to clean than a flat or matte finish, which is better suited for low-traffic areas like ceilings.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Choosing a Color in Isolation:** Don't pick a paint color without considering your furniture, rugs, and curtains.
*   **Ignoring Lighting:** A color can look completely different in a north-facing room versus a south-facing one.
*   **Skipping the Sample Step:** This is the most common and costly mistake. Always test first.
