## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Ives Window Hardwares

Choosing the right window hardware is about more than just looks; it's a critical decision for your home's security, functionality, and energy efficiency. To make an informed choice, consider these key factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, identify the type of window you have. Is it a double-hung (sash) window, a casement window, or something else? Different windows require specific types of hardware. A sash lock is for windows that slide up and down, while a casement fastener is for windows that crank outward. Also, consider the use case. Is this for a ground-floor window that needs maximum security, or a second-story window where ease of use is more important? Matching the hardware to the window type and its location is the first step to success.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($10-$50 ranges)
Ives by Schlage hardware is known for quality, and prices can vary. Basic, effective sash locks can be found for as little as $10-$15. For more advanced locks with features like dual camming action or premium finishes like bright brass or oil-rubbed bronze, expect to pay in the $20-$35 range. Multi-piece casement fastener kits with multiple strike plates may cost between $30 and $50. Set a realistic budget based on the number of windows you need to secure and the level of features you desire.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance security and usability. For sash locks, a dual cam-action mechanism is a significant upgrade over a standard crescent lock, as it provides a much tighter, rattle-free seal. For casement fasteners, the inclusion of multiple strike plates (surface, rim, mortise) offers valuable installation flexibility. Heavy-gauge springs are another key feature to look for, as they ensure the mechanism remains durable and operates smoothly over time.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The material of the hardware is paramount. Solid brass and steel are top choices for durability and strength. They resist bending, breaking, and corrosion far better than cheaper zinc alloys. Check for a solid, heavy feel in your hand—this is often a good indicator of quality construction. A durable finish (like PVD, oil-rubbed bronze, or polished brass) will resist chipping, fading, and tarnishing, keeping your windows looking great for years.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a reputable brand like Ives by Schlage is a wise investment. Established manufacturers have a long history of producing reliable, high-quality products that meet industry standards for security and durability. They stand behind their products and often use superior materials and engineering. Opting for a trusted brand gives you peace of mind that you are installing hardware that is proven to perform and last.

### Size and Fit - Considerations for space and storage
While storage isn't a concern, size and fit are critical. Before buying, measure the existing screw hole spacing on your windows if you are replacing old hardware. This can save you from having to drill new holes. Also, consider the profile of the lock. A low-profile lock is less obtrusive and often stronger. For window lifts or pulls, check the dimensions (width and projection) to ensure they are comfortable to grip and fit the scale of your window frame.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose ives window hardwares?

Choosing the best Ives window hardware involves a methodical approach to ensure you get the perfect match for your needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

**Step 1: Assess Your Windows**
Begin by taking an inventory of your windows. Identify the type (double-hung, casement, etc.), material (wood, vinyl), and condition of each. Measure the existing hardware's dimensions and screw spacing. This initial assessment will define your compatibility requirements.

**Step 2: Define Your Priorities**
What is most important to you? Is it maximum security for ground-floor windows, a specific aesthetic finish to match your home's decor, or eliminating annoying rattles from old sashes? Ranking your priorities will help you focus on the features that matter most, such as dual cam-action for a tight seal or a specific finish like Bright Brass.
