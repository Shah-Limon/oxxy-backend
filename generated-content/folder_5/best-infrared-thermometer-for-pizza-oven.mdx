## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Infrared Thermometer For Pizza Oven

Choosing the right infrared thermometer can significantly impact your cooking success. To ensure you select the best tool for your needs, consider these crucial factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about what you will primarily use the thermometer for. If it is exclusively for a pizza oven, a model with a range up to 1000°F is sufficient. However, if you also plan to use it for automotive work, grilling, or other high-heat applications, you might need a model with a higher maximum temperature. Consider whether you need features like adjustable emissivity for measuring different surfaces accurately (e.g., a matte pizza stone vs. a shiny steel grill).

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Infrared thermometers come in a wide price range. Entry-level models can be found for around $15-$25 and are perfectly suitable for basic cooking tasks. Mid-range options, often with better accuracy, adjustable emissivity, and more robust construction, typically fall between $25-$50. Premium, specialized models like those with dual lasers or color screens can cost $50 or more. Set a budget based on your needs and how often you plan to use the device.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Temperature Range:** Ensure the device's range (-58°F to 1000°F, for example) covers your intended uses.
- **Accuracy:** Look for models with an accuracy of ±1.5% or ±2°F. Higher accuracy is always better.
- **Distance-to-Spot (D:S) Ratio:** A 12:1 ratio is standard and means you can measure a 1-inch spot from 12 inches away. A higher ratio like 16:1 allows for more precise measurements from a greater distance.
- **Emissivity:** Adjustable emissivity (from 0.1 to 1.0) is a crucial feature for getting accurate readings on different types of surfaces. A fixed emissivity of 0.95 is fine for most organic materials but less accurate for shiny metals.
- **Response Time:** Most good thermometers have a response time of 500 milliseconds (0.5 seconds) or less, providing near-instant readings.
