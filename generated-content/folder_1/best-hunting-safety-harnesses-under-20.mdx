## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Hunting Safety Harnesses Under 20

Choosing the right hunting safety harness is a critical decision. It's the one piece of gear that can save your life. Before you make a purchase, consider these key factors to ensure you get a harness that is safe, comfortable, and suited to your specific hunting style.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Think about the type of hunting you do. Are you a bowhunter who needs maximum freedom of movement and a silent harness? Or are you a rifle hunter who sits for long hours and prioritizes comfort and gear accessibility? Consider factors like the weather you hunt in (hot vs. cold), how much gear you carry, and how far you walk to your stand. A lightweight, breathable harness is great for early seasons, while a padded one with gear loops might be better for all-day, late-season sits.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
While safety is priceless, harnesses come in a range of prices. Entry-level, reliable harnesses can be found in the $50-$80 range. These provide all the essential safety features without extra frills. Mid-range options, from $80-$150, often include better padding, more adjustability, lighter materials, and convenience features like quick-release buckles. Premium harnesses and complete kits can cost $150 or more, offering the highest levels of comfort, specialized features like MOLLE webbing, and all-inclusive accessories like lineman's belts.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for key safety and convenience features. A suspension relief strap is a must-have; it allows you to relieve pressure on your legs in the event of a fall, preventing suspension trauma. Check the weight rating to ensure it supports you and your gear (most are rated for 300 lbs). Consider the buckle types: quick-release buckles are fast and convenient, while traditional pass-through buckles are very secure but slower. Also, look at the tether design—some are flexible for more movement, while others are simple webbing.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Inspect the quality of the materials and stitching. The webbing should be thick, strong, and free of frays. Stitching should be heavy-duty and reinforced at all stress points, like where straps and buckles connect. Look for harnesses from reputable brands that adhere to safety standards recognized by the Treestand Manufacturer's Association (TMA) or meet ASTM standards. Durable materials like polyester and high-grade nylon will withstand years of use in harsh conditions.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with well-known, reputable brands like Hunter Safety System, MUDDY, Summit, and Malta Dynamics. These companies have a long history of producing safety equipment and invest heavily in research, development, and testing. They stand behind their products with warranties and have a proven track record of reliability. A harness is not the place to take a chance on an unknown or off-brand manufacturer to save a few dollars.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the weight and bulk of the harness. If you have a long hike to your stand, a lightweight, compact harness (around 2-2.5 lbs) will be much more comfortable to carry. More heavily padded, feature-rich harnesses can be bulkier and heavier. Also, ensure you get the right size. A harness that is too loose won't protect you properly in a fall, and one that is too tight will be uncomfortable and restrict movement. Always check the manufacturer's sizing chart based on your weight and waist/chest measurements.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy hunting safety harnesses under 20?

Investing in a quality hunting safety harness is not just a wise decision; it is an essential one for any treestand hunter. The risk of a fall is real and can have life-altering consequences. While the upfront cost may seem like just another expense in a gear-heavy sport, it pales in comparison to the potential medical bills or loss of livelihood from an accident. A harness is your single most important piece of life-saving equipment, functioning like a seatbelt in a car or a lifejacket on a boat.

Compared to the alternatives—which is essentially hunting without protection—there is no contest. Some hunters may rely on older, single-strap belts or homemade solutions, but these are incredibly dangerous and do not provide the full-body support needed to prevent serious injury or suspension trauma after a fall. Modern full-body harnesses are designed to distribute the force of a fall across your shoulders and thighs, keeping you upright and able to recover.

The long-term benefits are immense. A good harness provides peace of mind, allowing you to focus completely on the hunt without a nagging fear of falling. This mental clarity can lead to a more enjoyable and successful experience. Furthermore, a durable, well-made harness will last for many seasons, making the cost-per-hunt minimal. It's an investment in your safety, your future, and your ability to continue enjoying the sport you love for years to come.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose hunting safety harnesses under 20?

Choosing the best hunting safety harness involves a methodical approach to match your personal needs with the right features. Follow these steps to make a confident choice.

1.  **Assess Your Hunting Style:** First, define your primary needs. Are you an active bowhunter needing silence and mobility? Or do you sit for long periods and need maximum comfort? Your answer will guide whether you lean toward a lightweight, minimalist design or a more padded, feature-rich model.

2.  **Establish Your Budget:** Decide what you are comfortable spending. As we've seen, there are excellent, safe options across different price points. Knowing your budget helps narrow the field and prevents you from overspending on features you may not need.

3.  **Prioritize Fit and Comfort:** The best harness is one you'll wear every time. Try to find a harness with a high degree of adjustability in the shoulder, chest, and leg straps. Check the manufacturer's size chart carefully. If possible, try one on while wearing your typical hunting clothes to check for a snug, but not restrictive, fit.

4.  **Check for Essential Safety Features:** Ensure any harness you consider is TMA-recognized and includes a suspension relief strap. This is a non-negotiable safety feature. Examine the main tether, tree strap, and carabiner to ensure they are robust and well-made.

5.  **Read Reviews and Seek Recommendations:** Look at user reviews for the models you're considering. Pay attention to comments about long-term comfort, durability, and ease of use. Ask other hunters what they use and why they like it.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Buying Based on Price Alone:** Never sacrifice essential safety features for a lower price.
*   **Ignoring Proper Fit:** A poorly fitting harness is both unsafe and uncomfortable. Don't guess your size.
*   **Forgetting to Practice:** Practice putting on your harness and using it at ground level before you go up a tree. Understand how the suspension relief strap works.

**When to Upgrade:** Always inspect your harness before each season. If you see any signs of wear, such as frayed webbing, sun-fading, or damaged buckles, it's time to replace it immediately. Most manufacturers recommend replacing a harness after five years of use, or immediately after it has been involved in a fall.
