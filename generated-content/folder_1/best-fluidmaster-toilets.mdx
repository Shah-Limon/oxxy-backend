## What Is the Best Way to Choose fluidmaster toilets?

Choosing the right Fluidmaster product is a simple process if you follow a few key steps. This guide will help you select the perfect part and avoid common pitfalls.

**Step-by-Step Selection Guide:**

1.  **Diagnose the Problem:** First, figure out what's wrong.
    *   **Toilet runs intermittently ('ghost flushing'):** This is almost always a leaky flapper.
    *   **Toilet runs constantly after a flush:** The fill valve might not be shutting off, or the flapper isn't sealing.
    *   **Tank is slow to refill:** This is a classic sign of an old or clogged fill valve.
    *   **Loud noises during refill:** Your fill valve is likely worn out.
    *   **Weak flush:** This could be a water level issue (adjustable with a new fill valve) or a water-logged flapper.

2.  **Measure Your Flush Valve:** This is the most critical step. Turn off the water, flush the toilet to empty the tank, and measure the diameter of the drain opening at the bottom. It will be either 2 inches (most common) or 3 inches (common in newer toilets).

3.  **Choose Your Product:**
    *   For a simple flapper leak, buy a standalone flapper of the correct size (e.g., Fluidmaster 5403 for 3-inch).
    *   For slow or noisy filling, buy a universal fill valve (e.g., Fluidmaster 400H).
    *   If you have multiple issues or your toilet is over 10 years old, invest in a complete kit (e.g., 400AKR for 2-inch or K-400H-038-T4 for 3-inch). This replaces all the moving parts for a full refresh.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**

*   **Guessing the flush valve size:** Don't guess. Measuring takes 30 seconds and prevents buying the wrong part.
*   **Ignoring the root cause:** Don't just replace the handle if the toilet is running. Diagnose the real issue with the fill valve or flapper.
*   **Over-tightening connections:** The connections are plastic and should only be hand-tightened. Using a wrench can crack them and cause leaks.
