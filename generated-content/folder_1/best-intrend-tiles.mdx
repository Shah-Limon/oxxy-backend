## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Intrend Tiles

Choosing the perfect tile involves more than just picking a color you like. It's an investment that impacts your home's functionality and aesthetic for years. Here are the key factors to consider to ensure you make a choice you'll love.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine exactly where the tile will be installed. A tile that's perfect for a kitchen backsplash might not be suitable for a high-traffic entryway floor. Consider the following:
*   **Floors vs. Walls:** Floor tiles need to be much more durable and slip-resistant than wall tiles. Look for a higher PEI (Porcelain Enamel Institute) rating for floor applications.
*   **Wet vs. Dry Areas:** For bathrooms, showers, and outdoor patios, you need a tile with low water absorption. Materials like quartzite and slate are excellent, but porous stones like travertine will require thorough sealing.
*   **Indoor vs. Outdoor:** Outdoor tiles must be frost-proof and able to withstand temperature fluctuations and UV exposure. Natural stone is often a great choice for this.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations

## What Is the Best Way to Choose intrend tiles?

Choosing the right tile is a process that balances practical needs with personal style. Following a structured approach can help you navigate the options and make a confident choice.

**1. Measure and Plan:**
Start by accurately measuring the square footage of the area you plan to tile. Remember to add 10-15% to this number for cuts and waste. Create a rough sketch of the area, noting any obstacles like outlets, windows, or corners.

**2. Order Samples:**
This is the most critical step. Never choose a tile based on a screen image alone. Order samples of your top 3-4 choices. Place them in the actual room where they will be installed. Observe them at different times of day—morning light, artificial light at night—to see how their color and texture truly look in your space.

**3. Consider the Grout:**
Grout color has a massive impact on the final look. A contrasting grout color will make each tile pop and emphasize the pattern. A matching grout color will create a more seamless, monolithic look. Test grout color sticks next to your tile samples.

**4. DIY or Hire a Pro?**
Be honest about your skill level. While installing tile can be a DIY project, intricate patterns or natural stone can be tricky. Natural stone is heavier and often requires a wet saw for precise cuts. If you're not confident, hiring a professional installer is a worthwhile investment to protect your beautiful new tile.

**5. Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Not ordering enough:** Running out of tile mid-project is a nightmare, and different batches may have slight color variations.
*   **Ignoring subfloor prep:** The surface beneath your tile must be perfectly level, clean, and stable. Any imperfections will translate into cracked tiles later.
*   **Forgetting to seal:** Most natural stone is porous and needs to be sealed before and after grouting to prevent stains and water damage.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
Invest in high-quality, durable tile for high-traffic, high-visibility areas like kitchen floors, entryways, and master bathrooms. This is where the upgrade will be most appreciated and provide the best return. For a lower-traffic guest bathroom or a small laundry room, you might consider a more budget-conscious option without sacrificing the overall aesthetic of your home.
