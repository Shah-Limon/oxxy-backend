## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Gun Safes Under 400

Choosing a gun safe is a significant decision. Within the sub-$400 category, you can find excellent options, but you need to know what to prioritize. Here’s a guide to help you select the perfect safe for your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine what you need to store. Are you securing a single handgun for bedside access, or do you have multiple rifles and shotguns? A compact biometric safe is ideal for a pistol, while an under-bed drawer safe is better for long guns. Consider your primary use case: is this for quick home defense access, long-term storage, or vehicle transport? The right safe for a nightstand is very different from one you'd bolt in a closet or keep in your car.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
While our focus is under $400, prices within this range still vary.
*   **$100 - $200:** You can find excellent portable and single-pistol safes in this range. They offer solid basic security, keypad or biometric access, and are great for vehicles or nightstands.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose gun safes under 400?

Choosing the right gun safe requires a methodical approach. Follow these steps to ensure you make the best choice for your needs and budget.

**Step 1: Assess Your Inventory and Primary Use**
First, list exactly what you need to store. Is it one handgun, multiple pistols, or long guns? Your primary use case is equally important. If it's for rapid access for home defense, a biometric pistol safe is ideal. If it's for discreetly storing rifles, an under-bed safe is your best bet.

**Step 2: Measure Your Intended Location**
Don't just estimate. Get a tape measure and check the exact dimensions of the space where you plan to put the safe—be it under your bed, in a closet, or on a shelf. Compare these measurements against the safe's exterior dimensions.

**Step 3: Prioritize Your Access Method**
Decide which access method best suits you.
*   **Biometric:** Fastest access, but quality varies. Look for reliable scanners.
*   **Electronic Keypad:** Fast and reliable. A good balance of speed and security.
*   **Key Lock:** Simple and fail-proof. Essential as a backup, but slower for primary access.
Always choose a safe with a backup key in case of electronic failure or dead batteries.

**Step 4: Scrutinize Security Features**
Look beyond the lock. Check the steel gauge (lower is better), the presence of pry-resistant doors, and the number and thickness of locking bolts. An audible alarm is a significant deterrent and a valuable feature to look for.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring a Backup Key:** Never buy an electronic-only safe. A dead battery could lock you out when you need access most.
*   **Buying Too Small:** Your firearm collection might grow. It's often wise to buy a safe with slightly more capacity than you currently need.
*   **Not Bolting It Down:** Many safes under $400 are light enough for a determined thief to carry away. If the safe has mounting holes, use them. Securing it to the floor or a wall stud dramatically increases its effectiveness.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
A sub-$400 safe is excellent for preventing access by children and deterring smash-and-grab burglaries. However, if you have a very high-value collection or live in a high-risk area, you may eventually want to upgrade to a safe with advanced fireproofing, thicker steel, and a more complex locking mechanism. For most gun owners, a well-chosen safe in this price range provides more than adequate security for responsible storage.
