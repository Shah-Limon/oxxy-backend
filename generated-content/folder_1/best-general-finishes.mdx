## What to Look for Before Buying the Best General Finishes

Choosing the right finish is crucial for the success of your project. It not only provides the final look but also protects your work for years to come. Consider these factors to ensure you select the perfect General Finishes product for your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations

First, define your project. Are you staining new, unfinished wood, or are you refinishing an old piece of furniture? For new wood, both liquid and gel stains work well. For refinishing over an existing coat, a product with high adhesion like Milk Paint or Gel Stain is ideal. Consider the type of item as well. A high-traffic tabletop needs a highly durable topcoat like Arm-R-Seal or High Performance Topcoat, while a decorative picture frame has less demanding requirements. Finally, decide between an oil-based or water-based product. Water-based options offer fast drying, low odor, and easy cleanup, while oil-based finishes provide a traditional warm look and longer working times.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)

General Finishes products are premium coatings, so expect to pay more than you would for big-box store brands. However, the investment often pays off in quality and durability. Prices can range from around $20 for a half-pint of Gel Stain to over $60 for a quart of Arm-R-Seal. A pint of High Performance Topcoat typically falls in the $30-$40 range. When budgeting, consider the project size. Buying a smaller pint for a small table is more cost-effective than buying a quart you won't use. While the upfront cost is higher, the superior coverage and protection can save you money on reapplications down the line.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications

Pay close attention to a few key specs. **Sheen** (flat, satin, semi-gloss, gloss) dictates the final look and level of reflection. Satin is the most popular for furniture as it offers a warm luster without being overly shiny. **Dry Time** is critical; water-based products with 1-2 hour recoat times allow you to finish projects in a day, while oil-based products may require 12-24 hours between coats. **Base Type** (water, oil, gel) determines the application method, cleanup, and odor. Finally, check the **intended use**—most of these products are for interior use only, but the Milk Paint is rated for exterior use, adding to its versatility.

The quality of a finish is determined by the quality of its resins. General Finishes uses top-tier polyurethane and urethane resins, which is why their products are known for creating hard, protective shells. For topcoats, look for claims of scratch, water, and chemical resistance. A good finish should cure to a hard film that doesn't feel soft or tacky. For stains, quality is measured by the pigment and its ability to provide rich, even color without blotching. The heavy body of the Gel Stain is a key quality indicator of its user-friendly nature.

In the world of wood finishing, brand reputation is built over decades. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose general finishes?

Choosing the right finish doesn't have to be complicated. Following a logical, step-by-step process will guide you to the perfect product for your specific needs.

**1. Identify Your Project's Surface and Goal:**
First, determine what you are working with. Is it raw, new wood? An old piece with an existing finish? Your goal is also key. Are you looking to change the color dramatically, or simply protect the natural wood? For changing color, you'll need a stain or paint. For protection, a clear topcoat is the answer.

**2. Choose Your Base: Water vs. Oil:**
This is a critical decision.
*   **Water-Based:** Choose for fast dry times, low odor, easy soap-and-water cleanup, and a crystal-clear finish that won't yellow. Ideal for light-colored woods and painted surfaces. The High Performance Topcoat is a prime example.
*   **Oil-Based:** Choose for a slightly longer working time, maximum durability, and a traditional warm, amber tone that enhances wood grain. Requires mineral spirits for cleanup and good ventilation. Arm-R-Seal and Gel Stains fall into this category.

**3. Select the Product Type:**
*   **Stain:** To color raw wood or drastically change the color of a finished piece (Gel Stain).
*   **Paint:** To create an opaque, colored finish (Milk Paint).
*   **Topcoat:** To provide a clear, protective layer over stained, painted, or raw wood (High Performance or Arm-R-Seal).

**4. Pick Your Sheen:**
The sheen affects the final appearance. **Flat** offers no shine, for a modern, matte look. **Satin** has a low-to-medium luster and is the most popular choice for furniture. **Semi-Gloss** and **Gloss** are more reflective and are typically used for trim or high-wear areas, though General Finishes focuses on the more popular furniture sheens.

**Professional Tip:** Always test your entire finishing schedule (stain and topcoat) on a scrap piece of wood from your project or an inconspicuous area. This allows you to confirm the color and appearance before committing to the entire piece.

**Common Mistake to Avoid:** A common error is not allowing adequate dry time between coats, especially with oil-based products. Rushing the process can lead to a soft, tacky finish that never fully cures. Another mistake is applying a water-based topcoat over an oil-based stain that isn't fully cured (wait at least 72 hours).

## FAQ

### What is the best general finishes for beginners?
For beginners, the General Finishes Oil Base Gel Stain is fantastic. Its thick, wipe-on formula is incredibly forgiving, preventing the drips and blotchiness common with liquid stains. For a protective topcoat, the High Performance Water Based Topcoat is also very user-friendly due to its fast dry time, smooth leveling, and easy water cleanup.

### How much should I spend on general finishes?
Expect to spend between $20 for a small half-pint and up to $65 for a quart of a premium product like Arm-R-Seal. While this is more than budget brands, the cost is justified by superior coverage, durability, and ease of application. Investing in a quality finish protects the significant time and effort you put into your project.

### What features are most important in general finishes?
The three most important features are durability, ease of application, and dry time. Durability ensures your project withstands use. Ease of application helps you achieve a professional result without frustration. A fast dry time, especially in water-based products, allows you to complete projects much more quickly.

### How do I maintain my general finishes?
Maintaining a cured finish is simple. Clean surfaces with a soft, damp cloth and mild soap and water if needed. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners, ammonia-based products (like Windex), and abrasive scouring pads, as they can dull or damage the finish over time. Use coasters and placemats to prevent heat and water damage on tabletops.

### Are expensive general finishes worth it?
Yes, absolutely. Expensive finishes from reputable brands like General Finishes use higher-quality resins and pigments. This translates to better protection against scratches and chemicals, a more beautiful and clearer final appearance, and a more user-friendly application process. The long-term durability means you won't have to refinish the piece nearly as soon.

### What brands make the best general finishes?
General Finishes is widely considered a top-tier brand in the consumer and professional woodworking communities, hence the focus of this guide. They are renowned for their quality, innovation, and reliability. Other respected brands in the wood finishing space include Minwax, Varathane, and Rubio Monocoat, each with its own strengths and product lines.

### How long do general finishes typically last?
When applied correctly to an interior piece, a high-quality finish from General Finishes can last for many years, even decades, under normal use. The lifespan depends heavily on the amount of wear and tear the piece endures. A high-traffic kitchen table may show wear sooner than a decorative bookcase, but the initial quality of the finish is the biggest factor in its longevity.

### Can I find good general finishes on a budget?
Yes. The most budget-friendly approach is to buy the correct size for your project to avoid waste. The half-pint and pint sizes of General Finishes products offer an affordable way to access their premium quality for smaller projects. While they are not the cheapest brand, their value in terms of performance and results is exceptional.

### What is the difference between oil-based and water-based finishes?
Oil-based finishes penetrate the wood, cure slowly, and typically impart a warm, amber hue. They are known for excellent durability but require mineral spirits for cleanup and have a strong odor. Water-based finishes form a film on top of the wood, dry quickly, clean up with water, have low odor, and dry crystal clear. Modern water-based formulas now rival oil-based in durability.

### Can I apply a water-based topcoat over an oil-based stain?
Yes, you can, but it is critical that the oil-based stain is 100% cured. This can take longer than you think. As a rule of thumb, wait at least 72 hours in ideal conditions (low humidity, good airflow) before applying a water-based topcoat like High Performance over a product like the Gel Stain. Applying too soon can cause adhesion failure.
