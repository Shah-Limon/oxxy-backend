## What Is the Best Way to Choose infrared sauna heater?

Following a structured approach will ensure you select the perfect infrared sauna heater for your needs. Here are the steps and tips to guide you.

1.  **Assess Your Existing Sauna or Plan:** First, determine if you are upgrading, repairing, or building new. For repairs, you need to match the specs of the old heater. For a new build, you need to calculate the total wattage required for your sauna's cubic footage. A general rule is 1kW per 50 cubic feet.

2.  **Verify Electrical Specifications:** This is the most critical step. Check your sauna's control panel and wiring to confirm the required voltage (110V or 220V). Purchasing the wrong voltage is a costly and dangerous mistake. Also, ensure your circuit can handle the amperage draw of the new heaters.

3.  **Choose Your Heater Type:** Decide between carbon panels and carbon fiber tubes. Carbon panels offer a wider, more even heat distribution, which is great for backrests. Tubes can provide more intense, focused heat. Many modern saunas use a combination of both for optimal results.

4.  **Measure Your Space:** Physically measure the locations where you plan to install the heaters. Compare these measurements with the product dimensions to ensure a proper fit. Remember to leave adequate clearance around the heaters as recommended by the manufacturer.

5.  **Prioritize Safety Features:** Always choose heaters that come with or are compatible with protective guards or anti-scald meshes, especially if you have children. Look for materials like Canadian Hemlock, which is a safe and durable choice for sauna construction.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Voltage:** Buying a 220V heater for a 110V system (or vice-versa).
*   **Underpowering Your Sauna:** Not buying enough heaters or sufficient wattage for the size of your room, resulting in slow heating and low temperatures.
*   **Forgetting a Controller:** The heaters reviewed here require a separate sauna control system to function. They do not plug into a wall and turn on by themselves (with the exception of the portable and plug-in models).
