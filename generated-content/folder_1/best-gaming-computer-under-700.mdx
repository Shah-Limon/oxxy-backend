## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Gaming Computer Under 700

Navigating the world of budget gaming PCs can be tricky. To ensure you get the best performance for your money, it's crucial to understand the key components and features that matter most. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to consider.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you look at specs, consider what you'll use the PC for. Are you primarily playing fast-paced esports titles like Valorant or CSGO, which prioritize high frame rates over graphical fidelity? Or do you prefer visually stunning single-player games like Cyberpunk 2077 or Alan Wake 2, which demand more GPU power? Your primary use case will dictate where you should focus your budget. A PC for streaming will benefit more from a CPU with more cores, while a pure gaming machine should prioritize the graphics card.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
While our focus is on computers under $700, prices can fluctuate. Generally, you can expect to find solid entry-level systems in the $550-$650 range, often featuring components like an RTX 2060 or 3050. As you approach the $700 mark, you'll start to see more powerful CPUs, larger SSDs, and sometimes even newer generation graphics cards. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
This is the core of your decision.
*   **CPU (Processor):** Look for at least a modern 6-core processor. Good options in this range include the Intel Core i5 (12th-gen or newer) or the AMD Ryzen 5 (5000 series or newer). A stronger CPU helps prevent bottlenecks and is crucial for strategy games and multitasking.
*   **GPU (Graphics Card):** This is the most important component for gaming. Aim for an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3050, RTX 2060, or an AMD Radeon RX 6600 or better. The amount of VRAM is also important; 6GB is a good minimum, with 8GB being ideal.
*   **RAM (Memory):** 16GB is the standard for modern gaming. Anything less will cause performance issues in many new titles. DDR4 is common and perfectly fine, but DDR5 is a bonus if you can find it.
*   **Storage (SSD):** Do not buy a gaming PC with only a hard disk drive (HDD). A Solid State Drive (SSD) is essential for fast boot times and game loading. Aim for at least a 512GB NVMe SSD, with 1TB being a much more comfortable size for today's large games.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A good gaming PC is more than just its core components. Look for cases that offer good airflow, with multiple fans (at least two or three) to keep parts cool and prevent performance throttling. A tempered glass side panel is a nice aesthetic touch but also check for dust filters, which make maintenance easier. The power supply unit (PSU) is often an area where prebuilt systems cut costs, but a reliable PSU from a known brand is vital for system stability and longevity.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with reputable brands like CyberPowerPC, MSI, Acer, or HP often provides peace of mind. These companies typically offer better customer support, warranty coverage, and more reliable build quality. While smaller or lesser-known brands can offer great value, it's wise to read reviews and check their support policies before purchasing. Established brands also tend to use more standardized parts, making future upgrades easier.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider where you'll be placing your new PC. Most budget gaming PCs come in a standard mid-tower case, which fits on or under most desks. If space is tight, look for smaller form factor (SFF) builds, but be aware they can have limitations on cooling and upgrades. If you need to game in different locations, a gaming laptop is your best bet. Laptops offer incredible portability but often at a slight performance-to-price deficit compared to a desktop with similar specs.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose gaming computer under 700?

Choosing the right gaming PC is a process of matching your needs with the best available hardware within your budget. Following a structured approach can help you make a confident and informed decision.

**1. Define Your Primary Use:**
First, be honest about what you'll play. Are you an esports fanatic who needs 144+ FPS in Valorant? Or a single-player enthusiast happy with a stable 60 FPS in The Witcher 3? This will determine whether you should prioritize the CPU or GPU. For streamers, a CPU with more cores (like a Ryzen 7 or Core i7) is beneficial.

**2. Prioritize the Graphics Card (GPU):**
For a pure gaming machine, the GPU is king. It has the single biggest impact on your in-game frame rates and visual quality. In the under-$700 bracket, look for the best GPU you can find. An RTX 3050 or RX 6600 is a great target. Don't be tempted by a system with a high-end CPU but a very weak, older GPU.

**3. Don't Skimp on RAM and Storage:**
Ensure the system has at least 16GB of RAM. For storage, a 512GB NVMe SSD should be the absolute minimum. If you can find a PC with a 1TB SSD, it's a significant quality-of-life upgrade that prevents you from constantly having to uninstall games to make room for new ones.

**4. Check Connectivity and Upgrade Paths:**
Look at the ports. Does it have enough USB ports for your peripherals? Does it have modern standards like USB-C or Wi-Fi 6? Also, consider the future. A PC with a standard-sized case and a decent power supply will be much easier to upgrade later than a proprietary, compact system.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring the Power Supply (PSU):** A cheap, unrated PSU can be unreliable and even damage your components. Look for systems that mention at least an 80+ Bronze certified power supply.
*   **Falling for RGB Over Performance:** Flashing lights look cool, but they don't make your games run faster. Always prioritize the core components (CPU, GPU, RAM) over aesthetics.
*   **Forgetting the Monitor:** A powerful PC is wasted on an old 60Hz monitor. If your budget allows, factor in a 1080p, 144Hz monitor to truly appreciate the smooth frame rates your new PC can produce.
