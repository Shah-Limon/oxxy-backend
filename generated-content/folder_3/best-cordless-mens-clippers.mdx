### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Cordless clippers come in a wide price range.
*   **$20-$40:** Entry-level models, great for beginners or occasional use. They typically have basic features and shorter runtimes.
*   **$40-$80:** Mid-range clippers, often with better batteries (like lithium-ion), more powerful motors, and more accessories. This is the sweet spot for most home users.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
*   **Battery Life & Type:** Lithium-ion batteries are the gold standard. They offer longer runtimes (90 minutes to 3+ hours), hold a charge longer, and provide consistent power. NiMH batteries are common in budget models and typically offer around 60 minutes of runtime.
*   **Motor Power:** A powerful rotary or magnetic motor is essential for cutting through thick hair smoothly. Look for RPM specs if available (e.g., 6500 RPM) or trust reviews from users with similar hair types.
*   **Blades:** High-carbon steel or stainless steel self-sharpening blades are ideal. They stay sharp longer and resist rust. Some premium models offer ceramic blades, which stay cooler during use.
*   **Attachments:** The number and quality of guide combs are crucial. Look for a set that covers the lengths you need. Secure-fitting guards are a must to prevent them from slipping off mid-cut.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The clipper's housing material affects its longevity. Professional-grade clippers often have metal bodies, which are extremely durable but heavier. High-quality plastic housings are more common for home use; they are lightweight and can be very durable if well-made. Check the quality of the taper lever and power switch, as these are frequent points of failure on cheaper models.
