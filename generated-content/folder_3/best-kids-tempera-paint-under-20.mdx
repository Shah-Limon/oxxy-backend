## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Kids Tempera Paint Under 20

Navigating the world of art supplies can be tricky. To ensure you get the best value and the right product for your needs, consider these important factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about who will be using the paint and for what purpose. Are you buying for a toddler who needs a mess-free option? In that case, tempera paint sticks like the **Shuttle Art** or **Zenacolor** sets are ideal. Are you stocking a classroom or daycare? A bulk set with large bottles, such as the **Easy Playhouse** or **Positive Art** packs, will be the most economical. For general home use with elementary-aged kids, a versatile set with a good color range like the **Nicpro 18-Color Set** is a fantastic all-rounder.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
All the products on our list fall under the $20 mark, but the value proposition differs. You can get a solid starter set like the **Cra-Z-Art 8-Color Pack** for around $10-$15. For a bit more, closer to the $20 limit, you can get a much larger quantity or a specialty item like a 24-pack of paint sticks. Determine if your priority is the lowest initial cost, the best cost-per-ounce for long-term use, or unique features like mess-free application.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
The three most critical features for kids' tempera paint are:
1.  **Washability:** This is non-negotiable for most parents. Look for products that explicitly state they are 'washable' to save yourself from stained clothes and furniture.
2.  **Non-Toxicity:** Always choose paints that are certified non-toxic. Look for designations like 'AP Certified' or conformance to 'ASTM D-4236'. This ensures the product is safe if it accidentally comes into contact with skin or is ingested in small amounts.
3.  **Format:** Decide between traditional liquid tempera and solid paint sticks. Liquid paint is better for mixing colors and traditional brushwork. Paint sticks are unparalleled for their convenience, portability, and mess-free nature.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Paint quality is determined by its pigment concentration and consistency. Good tempera paint should be vibrant and opaque, providing good coverage without being too thin or watery. It should dry to a smooth, matte finish that doesn't crack, chip, or flake off the paper once it's dry. Brands like Prang and Cra-Z-Art have a long-standing reputation for producing consistent, high-quality paint.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While new brands can offer great products, established manufacturers like **Cra-Z-Art**, **Prang**, and **Colorations** have a proven track record of safety and quality. They have been trusted by parents and educators for years. Reading user reviews can also give you insight into a brand's reliability and customer service, regardless of how long they've been around.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the size of the paint bottles or set and where you plan to store it. Large bulk sets with 16oz bottles are economical but require significant shelf space. Smaller sets with 4oz or 8oz bottles, or compact paint stick sets, are much easier to store in a craft bin or drawer. If you plan on taking art supplies on the go—to the park, on vacation, or to a restaurant—paint sticks are by far the most portable and convenient option.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose kids tempera paint under 20?

Choosing the right paint set can be simple if you follow a few key steps. Here’s a guide to help you select the perfect option for your young artist.

**1. Assess the Artist's Age and Skill Level:**
For toddlers and preschoolers (ages 2-4), prioritize ease of use and safety. Mess-free paint sticks are the ideal choice. For older kids (ages 5+), liquid tempera in bottles provides more opportunities for learning color mixing and using different brush techniques.

**2. Prioritize Safety and Washability:**
Before looking at colors or price, check for two things: a non-toxic certification (like ASTM D-4236) and a 'washable' label. This is the most important step for ensuring art time is safe and clean-up is manageable.

**3. Decide Between Bottles and Sticks:**
Consider your tolerance for mess and your child's personality. If you want the easiest possible setup and cleanup, go with paint sticks. If you and your child enjoy the traditional process of pouring paint, using palettes, and mixing colors, then liquid paint in bottles is the way to go.

**4. Evaluate Quantity vs. Variety:**
Do you need a wide range of colors for imaginative projects, or a large quantity of a few basic colors for group activities? A set like the **Nicpro 18-Color set** offers variety, while a bulk pack like the **Easy Playhouse 10-Pack** offers quantity. Choose based on your specific needs.

**5. Read Recent User Reviews:**
Check what other parents and teachers are saying. Reviews can offer practical insights into how well the paint washes out of specific fabrics, how vibrant the colors are in person, and whether the bottles are prone to leaking.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Buying Non-Washable Paint:** Accidentally purchasing acrylic or other non-washable paint for a young child can lead to permanent stains. Always double-check the label.
*   **Ignoring Age Recommendations:** Paint sticks are great for toddlers, but older kids might find them limiting for detailed work. Conversely, liquid paint might be too messy for an unsupervised two-year-old.
*   **Focusing Only on Price:** The cheapest option isn't always the best value. Watery, poor-quality paint can be frustrating for kids and lead to disappointing results. All the options on our list offer a great balance of quality and affordability.
