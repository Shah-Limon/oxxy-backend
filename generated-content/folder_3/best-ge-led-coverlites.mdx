## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Ge Led Coverlites

Choosing the right GE LED Coverlite involves more than just picking a design you like. To ensure you get the perfect fit for your home, consider these key factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about where you'll be using the night light. A high-traffic hallway or staircase would benefit greatly from a motion-sensing model like the GE Enbrighten series. For a bedroom or nursery, a simple dusk-to-dawn model with a soft, warm glow, such as the CandleLite, might be more appropriate to avoid disrupting sleep. For kitchens and bathrooms, a clean, bright light is often preferred. Also, consider the outlet itself. Do you need to keep one socket free for other appliances? If so, look for models that explicitly offer versatile positioning.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($5-$30 ranges)
GE LED Coverlites are generally affordable, but prices can vary based on features. Basic dusk-to-dawn models can be found for as little as $5-$10 per light. Models with decorative finishes or advanced features like motion-sensing technology typically fall in the $10-$20 range. Multi-packs, like the GE Enbrighten 2-Pack, offer a better price per unit and can be a cost-effective way to outfit multiple rooms, usually ranging from $20-$30 for the set. Set a budget based on how many lights you need and what features are most important to you.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look closely at the key technologies offered. A **dusk-to-dawn sensor** is a standard feature that provides automated, energy-saving operation. For enhanced efficiency and safety, **motion-sensing** is a fantastic upgrade. GE's **Motion-Select** technology is particularly useful, offering both fully-activated and boost modes. Also, check the **brightness**, measured in lumens. A 3-lumen light is great for ambiance, while a 40-lumen light is better for safely navigating a dark space.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
While most night lights are made of durable plastic, the quality can vary. Look for products that feel solid and well-constructed. A key indicator of quality and safety is the **UL Listed** certification. This means the product has been tested by Underwriters Laboratories and meets their stringent safety standards.
