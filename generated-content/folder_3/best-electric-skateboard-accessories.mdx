## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Electric Skateboard Accessories

Choosing the right accessories can transform your ride. Before you make a purchase, consider these key factors to ensure you get the best gear for your specific needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about what problem you're trying to solve. Are you a daily commuter who needs to carry your board? A handle like the Maxfind is perfect. Do you often ride at night? A powerful light kit from Eggboards is essential. Always check for compatibility. Ensure the accessory, whether it's a remote, handle, or light, will fit your specific board model and trucks. Read the product descriptions carefully, as many will list compatible brands.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$70 ranges)
Electric skateboard accessories come in a wide price range. Simple, effective items like handles and basic light kits can be found for around $15 to $30. More advanced accessories, such as a high-tech remote with an LED screen like the PerGar, can cost upwards of $50 to $70. Set a budget based on your needs, but remember that investing a little more in critical safety items like lights is often a wise decision.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look closely at the features that matter for each type of accessory. For lights, check the brightness (lumens, if listed), number of modes, and battery life. For remotes, consider the connection technology (Hall sensors are superior), battery life, and any extra features like cruise control or data logging. For bags and handles, the key features are material (e.g., 600D Polyester, aluminum alloy) and carrying capacity or ergonomics.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Your accessories will be exposed to vibrations, impacts, and the elements. Look for high-quality materials that promise longevity. For metal parts like handles, look for aluminum or steel alloy. For plastic parts like remote casings, durable ABS plastic is a good sign. For lights and electronics, check for waterproof or water-resistant ratings (e.g., IPX rating), which indicate they can withstand rain and puddles.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While not always a guarantee, established brands in the e-skate community like Maxfind, Eggboards, and IWONDER often have a track record of producing reliable products. They tend to offer better customer support and stand by their quality claims. Reading user reviews can also give you insight into a product's real-world performance and the manufacturer's reliability.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the size and weight an accessory adds to your setup. A handle should be lightweight so it doesn't make your board noticeably heavier. A backpack, like the BRAVORD, should be functional without being overly bulky when you're riding. Lights should be compact enough that they don't interfere with your stance or get easily knocked off.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy electric skateboard accessories?

Absolutely. Investing in the right electric skateboard accessories is one of the smartest decisions a rider can make. While the initial purchase of the board is the main event, accessories are what elevate the experience from a fun hobby to a safe, convenient, and personalized mode of transportation. Think of it less as an extra cost and more as an essential part of the complete package.

Compared to the alternative of riding without them, the benefits are immense. A good set of lights, for example, can be the difference between a safe ride home and a dangerous accident. The cost of a $25 light kit is negligible compared to the potential for injury or damage. Similarly, a handle might seem like a small luxury, but for a commuter who has to carry their 15-25 lb board daily, it prevents strain and makes the journey far more pleasant.

The long-term benefits are clear: enhanced safety, increased convenience, and greater enjoyment. Accessories like a better remote can even improve the performance and control you have over your board. While it's wise to be budget-conscious, skimping on critical gear is a false economy. These accessories are not just add-ons; they are crucial components that maximize the utility and safety of your primary investment.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose electric skateboard accessories?

Choosing the best electric skateboard accessories involves a thoughtful process of matching your needs with the right products. Follow this step-by-step guide to make a confident choice.

**Step 1: Assess Your Riding Habits**
First, analyze how, where, and when you ride. Are you a daily commuter, a weekend warrior, or a night rider? Your primary use case will dictate your most important needs. A commuter will prioritize a carrying handle and a versatile backpack, while a night rider's first purchase must be a quality light kit.

**Step 2: Prioritize Safety First**
Regardless of your riding style, safety accessories should always be at the top of your list. This means a helmet is non-negotiable, and a bright, reliable light kit like those from Eggboards or IWONDER should be your next priority if you ever ride in low-light conditions. Don't compromise on visibility.

**Step 3: Identify Convenience Gaps**
What parts of your ride are inconvenient or annoying? Is carrying your heavy board a pain? Get a handle. Do you wish you had more precise control or a cruise function for long, flat paths? Upgrade your remote. Identifying these pain points will lead you to the most impactful quality-of-life upgrades.

**Step 4: Set a Realistic Budget**
Determine how much you're willing to spend. You can get excellent, essential accessories without breaking the bank. Prioritize spending on safety items first, then convenience, and finally, aesthetic upgrades like LED strips.

**Step 5: Research and Read Reviews**
Once you know what you need, research specific products. Look at expert reviews (like this one!) and read user feedback on retail sites. Pay attention to comments about durability, real-world battery life, and ease of installation.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Compatibility:** Always double-check that an accessory, especially a remote or handle, is compatible with your specific board model.
*   **Buying the Cheapest Option:** While budget-friendly options exist, the absolute cheapest product is often made with inferior materials that will fail quickly. This is especially true for safety equipment.
*   **Focusing Only on Looks:** A cool-looking accessory is great, but it's useless if it doesn't perform its function well. Prioritize function over form.

## FAQ

**What is the best electric skateboard accessories for beginners?**
For beginners, the most important accessories are safety-focused. A high-quality helmet is number one. After that, a set of bright, easy-to-install LED lights like the Eggboards kit is crucial for visibility, as new riders may not yet be fully aware of their surroundings. Protective gear like knee and elbow pads is also highly recommended to build confidence and prevent injury during the learning phase.

**How much should I spend on electric skateboard accessories?**
You can build a great starter kit for $50-$100. This would typically cover a good set of USB rechargeable lights ($20-$35), a durable carrying handle ($15-$25), and basic protective gear. More advanced accessories like a high-performance remote can cost more, but it's wise to focus on the safety and convenience essentials first.

**What features are most important in electric skateboard accessories?**
This depends on the accessory. For lights, brightness, multiple modes, and being USB rechargeable are key. For handles, look for durable materials like aluminum and an ergonomic design. For remotes, a reliable connection, clear display, and good battery life are most important. For all accessories, durability and ease of installation are universally important features.

**How do I maintain my electric skateboard accessories?**
Maintenance is generally simple. For electronic accessories like lights and remotes, keep them charged and avoid deep discharging the battery. Wipe them down with a damp cloth if they get dirty, and check that mounting hardware or straps are secure before each ride. For backpacks, follow the manufacturer's cleaning instructions. Regularly inspect all gear for signs of wear and tear.

**Are expensive electric skateboard accessories worth it?**
Sometimes, yes. A more expensive remote like the PerGar offers tangible performance benefits (smoother control, better connection) that an experienced rider will appreciate. More expensive lights might offer longer battery life or more robust waterproofing. However, for many accessories, a well-regarded mid-range product often provides the best balance of price and performance.

**What brands make the best electric skateboard accessories?**
Brands that specialize in the e-skate world tend to produce reliable gear. For lighting, Eggboards and IWONDER are very popular and well-reviewed. For convenience items like handles, Maxfind has a strong reputation. For bags, BRAVORD offers a great balance of features and price. It's always a good idea to check reviews for any brand you're considering.

**How long do electric skateboard accessories typically last?**
With proper care, good quality accessories should last for several years. The lifespan of electronic components like rechargeable batteries will degrade over time, typically lasting for 300-500 charge cycles before capacity diminishes noticeably. Mechanical items like aluminum handles can last for the life of the board if not damaged in a crash.

**Can I find good electric skateboard accessories on a budget?**
Yes, absolutely. There are many excellent budget-friendly options that don't compromise on quality. A simple but effective carrying handle or a reliable set of USB lights can often be found for under $25. The key is to read reviews to ensure you're buying a product that is known to be a good value, not just a cheap one.
