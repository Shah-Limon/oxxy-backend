## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Delaney Designer Keyed Door Levers

Choosing the right door lever involves more than just picking a style you like. It's a decision that impacts your home's security, functionality, and overall aesthetic. Here are the key factors to consider to ensure you make the best choice.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine the lever's function. Do you need a keyed entry lever for an exterior door, a privacy lever with a lock for a bathroom, a passage lever for a hallway, or a non-turning dummy lever for a closet? Also, measure your door's thickness and backset (the distance from the door's edge to the center of the borehole) to ensure compatibility. Most levers are adjustable, but it's always best to check.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($30-$100)
Designer door levers can range in price from around $30 for a basic interior privacy lever to over $100 for a high-security, certified entry set. Set a budget based on the number of doors you are outfitting and the level of security you require. While it can be tempting to choose the cheapest option, investing in a quality entry lever is an investment in your home's security.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that add convenience and security. A reversible or non-handed lever can be installed on both left- and right-swinging doors, simplifying purchasing. An adjustable latch fits different backsets. For keyed entry levers, consider the type of keyway (e.g., KW1 or SC1) and whether you want the option to have multiple locks keyed alike. For high-security needs, look for features like anti-pick pins or a certified BHMA rating.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The material and finish of the lever are crucial for its longevity. Look for solid metal construction (like brass or zinc) rather than plated materials, which can feel flimsy and wear out quickly. The finish should be durable and resistant to tarnishing, chipping, and corrosion, especially for exterior doors exposed to the elements. Finishes like PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) are known for their exceptional durability.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Delaney and Schlage have built a reputation for quality, reliability, and customer service. Choosing a product from an established manufacturer often means you're getting a well-engineered product backed by a solid warranty. They are more likely to offer consistent finishes across different product lines, which is important if you're outfitting an entire house.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While not portable, the size and projection of the lever are important. Ensure the lever won't interfere with the door frame or any nearby walls when operated. Consider the ergonomics of the lever—it should be comfortable to grip and use. The size of the rosette (the plate behind the lever) should be large enough to cover any marks or paint rings left by old hardware.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose delaney designer keyed door levers?

Choosing the perfect door lever is a process of balancing aesthetics, function, and security. Following a structured approach can help you make a confident decision.

**1. Assess Your Home's Needs:**
Start by making a list of every door that needs new hardware. Categorize them by function:
- **Exterior Doors:** Require keyed entry levers for security.
- **Bedrooms/Bathrooms:** Need privacy levers with an interior lock.
- **Hallways/Closets:** Use passage levers with no lock.
- **Inactive Double Doors/Shallow Closets:** May require dummy levers.

**2. Define Your Style:**
Consider your home's architectural style and interior decor. Are you aiming for a modern, traditional, rustic, or transitional look? Delaney offers various collections (like the Florini, Lexington, or CIRA series) in multiple finishes (Satin Nickel, Oil Rubbed Bronze, Polished Chrome) to match your vision. Consistency is key to a polished look.

**3. Prioritize Security for Entry Points:**
For front doors, back doors, and garage entry points, security is paramount. Look for levers with solid construction, a deadbolt, and consider options with higher security ratings if your area warrants it. Decide if you want all your exterior locks keyed alike for convenience.

**4. Check Door Specifications:**
Before you buy, measure the thickness of your doors and the backset. While most modern levers are adjustable, confirming these measurements will prevent installation headaches. Also, note the "handing" of your doors (whether they swing left or right), although choosing reversible levers eliminates this concern.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
- **Mixing Styles and Finishes:** An eclectic look can work, but a chaotic mix of hardware styles and finishes often looks unintentional and cheapens the overall design.
- **Ignoring Interior Doors:** Don't just focus on the front door. Upgrading all your hardware creates a cohesive, high-end feel throughout the home.
- **Sacrificing Quality for Price:** A flimsy lever that fails after a year is no bargain. It's better to invest in a durable product that will last.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
If you are planning to stay in your home long-term or are doing a major renovation, investing in a premium series is a worthwhile upgrade that enhances your daily life. If you are preparing a rental property or working on a tight budget, Delaney's more standard collections still offer excellent style and reliability without the premium price tag.
