## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Microwave Cover Not Plastic

Choosing the right microwave cover involves more than just picking the first one you see. Consider these factors to ensure you get a product that's safe, effective, and suits your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, measure the interior of your microwave and your most-used dinner plates and bowls. Ensure the cover's diameter and height will comfortably fit without touching the sides of the microwave or squishing your food. Think about what you heat most often. If you frequently reheat tall items or bowls of soup, a taller cover like the Le'raze is essential.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Non-plastic microwave covers come in a range of prices. Basic but safe plastic options like those from Nordic Ware can be found for $10-$15 for a set. High-quality silicone covers, such as the Tovolo, typically range from $15-$25. Premium glass covers, like those from Le'raze and Mikro Innovations, are an investment, usually costing between $25 and $40. Set your budget based on your preference for material and durability.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance usability and performance.
*   **Vents:** Vents are crucial for allowing steam to escape, which prevents food from becoming soggy and stops pressure from building up. Look for single or dual-vent systems.
*   **Handles:** A good handle or knob is essential for safety. It should be easy to grip and stay cool to the touch. Silicone handles are an excellent feature.
*   **Material:** Your primary choice will be between glass and silicone. Glass is non-porous, won't stain or absorb odors, and is 100% non-toxic. Silicone is lightweight, durable, and often collapsible for easy storage.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
For glass covers, look for tempered or borosilicate glass, which is more resistant to thermal shock and breakage. For silicone covers, ensure they are made from 100% food-grade, BPA-free silicone that is certified heat-resistant to high temperatures. Check for a sturdy, well-balanced design that won't easily tip over.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Nordic Ware have a long-standing reputation for quality kitchenware made in the USA. Newer, innovative brands like Tovolo, Le'raze, and Mikro Innovations are building trust by focusing on high-quality, non-toxic materials and smart design. Reading user reviews can provide insight into a brand's product quality and customer service.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
If kitchen storage is tight, a collapsible silicone cover like the Tovolo or Saebume models is a fantastic choice. They shrink down to a fraction of their size. Glass covers are rigid and heavier, so you'll need dedicated cabinet or drawer space for them. Consider the weight if you have difficulty lifting heavier objects.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy microwave cover not plastic?

Absolutely. Investing in a non-plastic microwave cover is a wise decision for several compelling reasons. First and foremost is health and safety. While many modern plastics are BPA-free, there are ongoing concerns about other chemicals potentially leaching into food when heated. Glass is inert and guaranteed not to release any substances, while food-grade silicone is also a very stable and safe alternative. This peace of mind is invaluable.

Second, durability and long-term value are significant benefits. A glass cover can last a lifetime with proper care, as it won’t warp, melt, stain, or scratch like plastic. Silicone is also incredibly durable and resilient. While the initial cost may be higher than a cheap plastic cover, you avoid the need for frequent replacements, saving money in the long run.

Finally, there's the environmental impact. By choosing a reusable glass or long-lasting silicone cover, you reduce plastic waste. It’s a small but meaningful step toward a more sustainable kitchen. Compared to using disposable paper towels to cover food, a permanent cover is far more eco-friendly and effective. The improved heating performance, which keeps food moist and flavorful, is just the icing on the cake.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose microwave cover not plastic?

Choosing the best non-plastic microwave cover is a straightforward process if you follow a few key steps.

1.  **Assess Your Needs:** Start by measuring your microwave's interior height and turntable diameter, as well as your largest plates. This will give you the maximum dimensions for a cover. Decide what’s more important to you: the purity of glass or the lightweight, space-saving nature of silicone.

2.  **Prioritize Safety Features:** Look for a cover with adequate steam vents. This is non-negotiable for safety and food quality. Also, ensure it has a handle or knob that is designed to stay cool, like the silicone grips on the glass models we reviewed.

3.  **Compare Materials:**
    *   **Glass:** Best for purity, even heating, and resistance to stains and odors. It's heavier and more fragile.
    *   **Silicone:** Best for storage, durability, and lightweight handling. It can sometimes retain odors from very aromatic foods.

5.  **Avoid Common Mistakes:** A frequent error is buying a cover that's too small. Always err on the side of a slightly larger diameter. Another mistake is choosing a cover without vents, which can lead to soggy food or a dangerous pressure buildup. Finally, don't sacrifice quality for a slightly lower price; a well-made cover is a long-term kitchen investment.

### How do I maintain my microwave cover not plastic?
Maintenance is simple. Both glass and silicone covers are typically dishwasher safe. For best results, place them on the top rack. Glass covers can be washed just like any other glass dish and will not stain or retain odors.

### How long do microwave cover not plastic typically last?

Both are significantly more long-lasting than cheap plastic covers.

Yes. While premium glass is more expensive, a high-quality silicone cover like the Tovolo model offers an excellent non-plastic solution at a more moderate price point. ### Is a glass or silicone microwave cover better?
It depends on your priorities. Glass is better for purity, as it's completely non-porous and will never absorb odors or stains. It also provides very even heating. Silicone is better for durability and storage, as it's unbreakable and often collapsible. Both are excellent non-plastic alternatives.
