## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Kaba All Home Securities

Choosing the right security hardware involves more than just picking a lock. To ensure you get the perfect fit for your needs, consider these critical factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, define your exact requirement. Are you securing a front door at home, a high-traffic office, or managing a hotel? A homeowner might need a single, high-security cylinder, while a business might benefit from an interchangeable core (IC) system for easy rekeying. For keyless access, you'll need to decide between mechanical pushbutton locks and RFID systems. Always check compatibility—ensure the cylinder fits your lock housing, the keyway matches your existing keys if desired, and the RFID frequency (e.g., 125kHz vs. 13.56MHz) matches your access control system.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Security hardware prices can vary widely. A basic replacement IC core might cost between $20-$40, while a pack of 100 RFID fobs could be around $30-$60. High-security, heavy-duty mechanical locks like the Kaba Simplex can cost several hundred dollars ($200-$500+). Set a budget based on the level of security you require. While it's tempting to save money, investing in a more durable and secure lock for a critical entry point is often a wise long-term decision.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to the technical details. For mechanical locks, look at the pin count (6-pin or 7-pin is more secure than 5-pin) and the keyway type (e.g., Best A, Schlage C). For IC cores, the inclusion of a control key is a crucial feature for quick rekeying. For RFID products, the frequency is the most important spec. Also, consider whether a card or fob is read-only or re-writable, as this impacts both security and functionality.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The materials and construction of a lock determine its longevity and resistance to attack. Look for products made from solid brass, steel, or other high-grade metal alloys. A quality finish like satin chrome (US26D) not only looks good but also protects against corrosion. For high-traffic areas, choose products rated as heavy-duty or commercial-grade, as they are designed to withstand constant use and abuse.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
In the security industry, brand reputation matters. Established manufacturers like Kaba (now part of dormakaba), Simplex, and Schlage have a long history of producing reliable and effective security products. While newer brands like elox and SUMMIT DOORWARE can offer excellent value and innovative features, it's always wise to check reviews and ensure they have a track record of quality before investing.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While the lock itself isn't portable, the access credentials are. Consider the form factor you and your users will prefer—traditional keys, compact key fobs, or wallet-sized key cards. For the hardware itself, ensure the dimensions (like backset and length) match your door and existing cutouts. Incorrect sizing is one of the most common installation problems, so measure twice before you buy.

### What is an IC Core and why is it useful?
An IC Core, or Interchangeable Core, is a small, figure-8-shaped cylinder that contains the lock's pinning mechanism. Its main advantage is that it can be removed from its housing with a special 'control key'. This allows you to rekey a lock in seconds by simply swapping out the core, without needing a locksmith. It's incredibly useful for landlords, businesses, and institutions that need to manage key control efficiently.

### What's the difference between 13.56MHz and 125kHz RFID systems?
These are two different radio frequencies used for access control. 125kHz is an older, low-frequency technology that is simple and cost-effective, common in residential buildings and offices. 13.56MHz is a high-frequency technology that allows for more security and data storage, often used in hotel key cards and more advanced systems. The two are not compatible, so you must buy cards or fobs that match your system's frequency.
