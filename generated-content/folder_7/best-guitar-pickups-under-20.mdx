## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Guitar Pickups Under 20

Choosing the right pickup involves more than just price. Even in the under-$20 category, there are key factors to consider to ensure you get a product that meets your needs and enhances your playing experience.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, identify your instrument. Are you amplifying an acoustic guitar, a classical guitar with nylon strings, or upgrading an electric guitar? Piezo pickups are for acoustic instruments, while magnetic pickups like humbuckers are for electrics. For acoustic guitars, check if you need an under-saddle, soundhole, or contact pickup. Consider your use case: is this for casual open mics, home recording, or band practice? A simple piezo might be fine for casual use, but you might want a humbucker with more tonal options for rock music.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
While all the products on this list are under $20, prices can still vary. A basic piezo element might cost just a few dollars, while a full humbucker set or a more advanced passive piezo system will be closer to the $20 mark. Set a realistic budget but be aware that spending a little more can sometimes get you significant upgrades, like better materials (e.g., Alnico 5 magnets) or more versatile features (e.g., 4-conductor wiring).

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications

## What Is the Best Way to Choose guitar pickups under 20?

Choosing the right budget pickup is a straightforward process if you follow a few key steps. It's about matching the product to your specific instrument and musical goals.

**Step-by-Step Selection Guide:**
1.  **Identify Your Guitar Type:** The first step is absolute. Is your guitar an acoustic (steel-string), a classical (nylon-string), or an electric? This will determine the entire category of pickup you need.
2.  **Define Your Goal:** What do you want to achieve? Are you looking for simple amplification for an open mic night? In that case, a basic under-saddle piezo is perfect. Are you trying to get a heavier rock tone from your electric guitar? Then a high-output Alnico 5 humbucker is what you need.
3.  **Check Compatibility:** For an under-saddle pickup, measure your guitar's saddle slot to ensure a good fit. For an electric pickup, confirm that it's the right size (humbucker, single-coil, etc.) to fit into your guitar's body. Also, check the output jack requirement (e.g., 2.5mm for many piezos).
4.  **Read Reviews and Watch Demos:** Search for reviews or video demonstrations of the specific pickup you're considering. Hearing how it sounds, even through computer speakers, can give you a much better idea of its tonal character than a written description alone.
5.  **Assess Installation Requirements:** Be honest about your technical skills. A simple piezo installation is a fairly easy DIY project. Installing electric guitar pickups involves soldering and is more complex. If you're not comfortable with it, factor in the cost of having a professional luthier or tech install it for you.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring String Balance:** For under-saddle pickups, ensure the bottom of your saddle and the slot in the bridge are perfectly flat. An uneven surface will lead to some strings sounding much louder than others.
*   **Buying the Wrong Type:** Don't buy a magnetic humbucker for your acoustic guitar (unless it's a soundhole pickup specifically designed for it). It won't work, as magnetic pickups require steel strings to function.
*   **Expecting a Miracle:** A budget pickup will make a huge improvement, but it won't make a poorly-made guitar sound like a custom-shop instrument. It enhances the sound that's already there.
