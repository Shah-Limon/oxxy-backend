## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 0.7 Cu Ft Microwave

Choosing the right compact microwave involves more than just picking the one that fits on your counter. To ensure you get an appliance that meets your needs, consider these crucial factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you'll use the microwave. Are you a student who mainly needs to reheat leftovers and make popcorn? Or are you part of a small family that will use it for defrosting meats and cooking side dishes? If you need precise control, look for a model with multiple power levels and programmable cooking stages. For simple reheating, a model with strong one-touch presets will be more convenient. Consider the types of dishes you use; ensure the turntable diameter (usually 9.5-10.5 inches in this size) can accommodate your dinner plates.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
For a 0.7 cu ft microwave, you can expect to spend between $70 and $150. Models on the lower end ($70-$90) will provide basic, reliable functionality, perfect for simple tasks. In the mid-range ($90-$120), you'll start to see better build quality, more advanced features like mute functions or eco modes, and more sophisticated designs. Premium models ($120-$150) from top-tier brands like GE or Toshiba will offer superior durability, more precise cooking technology like advanced defrosting, and a more solid overall feel. Set your budget based on the features that are most important to you.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Wattage:** Most 0.7 cu ft models are 700 watts. This is sufficient for most tasks but will cook more slowly than larger, more powerful microwaves.
- **Power Levels:** Look for 10 or more power levels. This allows you to do more than just blast food on high; you can gently melt butter, soften ice cream, or slowly cook delicate items.
- **Presets/One-Touch Buttons:** These are huge time-savers. Look for presets that match what you cook most, like popcorn, potato, pizza, and beverage. An "add 30 seconds" button is also incredibly useful.
- **Defrost Options:** A good microwave should offer both time defrost and weight defrost for more accurate results.
- **Child Lock:** This is a non-negotiable feature for any household with children.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Pay attention to the materials and overall build. A stainless steel front is not only stylish but often more durable than an all-plastic construction. Check the feel of the door—does it open and close smoothly and securely? A push-button release can be more convenient than a pull handle. The reputation of the brand often correlates with quality control and the longevity of the appliance. Reading user reviews for comments on long-term durability can also be very insightful.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like GE, Toshiba, BLACK+DECKER, and Farberware have been making appliances for decades. Choosing an established brand often means better customer service, more reliable warranty support, and a product that has undergone more rigorous testing. While newer brands can offer innovative features, a trusted manufacturer often provides peace of mind and a higher likelihood of long-term reliability.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While all these microwaves are 0.7 cu ft internally, their external dimensions can vary. Measure your available counter space (width, depth, and height) carefully before you buy. Remember to leave a few inches of clearance around the sides and back for proper ventilation, as recommended by the manufacturer. Consider the unit's weight if you plan on moving it occasionally. A lighter model might be more portable but could also slide around on the counter during use.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 0.7 cu ft microwave?

Investing in a 0.7 cu ft microwave is an exceptionally wise decision for a significant number of people, primarily because it perfectly addresses the modern need for space efficiency without sacrificing core functionality. In today's world of smaller living spaces, from studio apartments and dorm rooms to downsized homes and office breakrooms, countertop real estate is a precious commodity. A compact microwave provides the essential convenience of quick cooking and reheating while occupying a minimal footprint.

Compared to its larger counterparts (1.0 cu ft and up), a 0.7 cu ft model is not only more space-conscious but also more energy-efficient and affordable. For individuals, couples, or anyone who doesn't need to cook large family-sized meals, the power and capacity are perfectly adequate for daily tasks. The long-term benefits are clear: you save space, reduce your energy bill, and spend less upfront. While a toaster oven or air fryer can handle some similar tasks, neither can reheat a cup of coffee in 30 seconds or defrost a pound of ground beef as effectively as a microwave. For its specific purpose, the compact microwave remains an unbeatable and practical kitchen tool.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 0.7 cu ft microwave?

Choosing the best 0.7 cu ft microwave involves a thoughtful, step-by-step process to ensure the appliance you select perfectly matches your lifestyle and kitchen.

1.  **Assess Your Space and Use:** First, measure the exact spot where the microwave will live. Note the maximum width, depth, and height. Next, be honest about your primary use. Will it be for 90% reheating and 10% cooking, or do you need more advanced features like multi-stage cooking and precise defrosting? This will help you prioritize features.

2.  **Define Your Must-Have Features:** Create a short list of non-negotiable features. For a family with kids, a reliable child lock is essential. For someone sensitive to noise, a mute function (like on the COMFEE' or Toshiba models) is a priority. If you hate scrubbing, look for easy-to-clean interiors and exteriors.

3.  **Set a Realistic Budget:** Based on our guide, decide if you're looking for a basic workhorse ($70-$90) or a more feature-rich model ($90-$150). Sticking to a budget helps narrow down the options significantly.

4.  **Read Reviews with a Critical Eye:** Look beyond the star rating. Read reviews from users who have owned the microwave for several months to get a sense of its long-term durability. Pay attention to recurring complaints about noise, uneven heating, or faulty buttons.

5.  **Compare Top Contenders:** Once you have 2-3 models that fit your criteria, compare them side-by-side. Look at the turntable size, the specific presets offered, and the warranty. Sometimes a small detail, like a push-button door versus a handle, can be the deciding factor for your personal convenience.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring External Dimensions:** Don't assume all 0.7 cu ft microwaves are the same size. Always measure first.
*   **Buying on Wattage Alone:** While important, 700 watts is standard. Features like power levels and smart cooking sensors often have a bigger impact on performance.
*   **Forgetting About Ventilation:** Placing a microwave in a tight, unventilated space is a fire hazard and can shorten the appliance's lifespan.

## FAQ

**What is the best 0.7 cu ft microwave for beginners?**
The best option for beginners is typically one with simple, intuitive controls. The BLACK+DECKER EM720CB7 is an excellent choice due to its clear, easy-to-read control panel and straightforward one-touch buttons. Its reliability and no-fuss operation make it perfect for someone who wants to heat food without navigating complex settings.

**How much should I spend on 0.7 cu ft microwave?**
You should expect to spend between $70 and $150. A budget of around $80-$100 will get you a very reliable model with all the essential features from a good brand. Spending more, in the $100-$150 range, typically gets you enhanced features like quieter operation, advanced defrosting technology, or a more premium build quality.

**What features are most important in 0.7 cu ft microwave?**
The most important features are at least 10 power levels for cooking flexibility, one-touch buttons for common items (like popcorn and potato), a reliable defrost function (by weight or time), and a child safety lock. An "add 30 seconds" button and a clear digital display are also crucial for everyday convenience.

**How do I maintain my 0.7 cu ft microwave?**
Maintenance is simple. Wipe up spills immediately with a damp cloth to prevent them from baking on. Regularly remove the glass turntable and wash it in the sink or dishwasher. To eliminate odors, microwave a bowl of water with a few tablespoons of lemon juice or vinegar for a few minutes, then let it stand to steam before wiping the interior clean.

**Are expensive 0.7 cu ft microwave worth it?**
They can be, depending on your needs. More expensive models from brands like GE or Toshiba often offer better build quality, which can mean a longer lifespan. They may also include genuinely useful features like the Toshiba's ChefDefrost or the COMFEE's mute function. If these features solve a specific problem for you, the extra cost is often justified.

**What brands make the best 0.7 cu ft microwave?**
Several brands consistently produce high-quality compact microwaves. Farberware, BLACK+DECKER, Toshiba, and GE are all highly reputable and known for their reliability and performance. Chefman and COMFEE' are also excellent choices that often innovate with unique, user-friendly features.

**How long do 0.7 cu ft microwave typically last?**
With proper care, a good quality 0.7 cu ft microwave should last between 5 and 10 years. The lifespan depends on the build quality of the specific model and how heavily it is used. Investing in a model from a reputable brand can often lead to a longer operational life, making it a better value over time.

**Can I find good 0.7 cu ft microwave on a budget?**
Absolutely. Brands like BLACK+DECKER and Commercial CHEF offer fantastic models that are very budget-friendly without sacrificing essential performance. You can easily find a reliable, effective microwave with all the necessary features for under $100. You might miss out on some premium features, but for core heating and defrosting, budget options are excellent.

**Is 700 watts enough power for a microwave?**
Yes, for a 0.7 cu ft microwave, 700 watts is the standard and is perfectly sufficient for most tasks like reheating, defrosting, and cooking small- to medium-sized items. While it will take slightly longer to heat food compared to a 1000-watt or 1200-watt model, the difference is often negligible for everyday use.

**What's the difference between a push-button door and a pull handle?**
A push-button door release mechanism unlatches the door with the press of a button, allowing for easy one-handed operation. A pull handle requires you to grip the handle and pull the door open. The push-button style can be more convenient, but it adds a mechanical part that could potentially fail over time. The choice is largely a matter of personal preference.
