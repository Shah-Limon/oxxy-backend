## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Lawn Mower Blades For Exmark

Choosing the right blades involves more than just finding something that fits. To get the best performance from your Exmark mower and the healthiest lawn, consider these key factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First and foremost, you must confirm compatibility. Check your Exmark mower's manual for the correct OEM part number for your blades. You will also need to know your mower's deck size (e.g., 48-inch, 52-inch, 60-inch). Beyond the basics, consider your typical mowing conditions. Do you prefer to bag, mulch, or side-discharge clippings?
- **High-Lift Blades:** Best for bagging, as they create maximum suction.
- **Mulching Blades:** Ideal for returning nutrients to the lawn.
- **Standard Blades:** A good all-around choice for side-discharging.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Lawn mower blade prices can vary significantly. A single set of three aftermarket blades might cost between $30 and $60. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose lawn mower blades for exmark?

Following a simple, step-by-step process will ensure you select the perfect blades for your needs and avoid common purchasing mistakes.

**1. Identify Your Mower's Specifications:**
Start by finding your Exmark's model number and deck size. This information is usually on a sticker or plate on the mower's frame. Consult your owner's manual to find the specific OEM part number for your replacement blades. This number is the most reliable way to find a compatible match.

**2. Measure Your Old Blades:**
If you can't find the part number, carefully remove one of your old blades. Measure its length diagonally from tip to tip. Also, measure the diameter of the center mounting hole. This hands-on approach guarantees you are looking for the correct size.

**3. Decide on Blade Type:**
Consider your desired outcome. Do you want the cleanest possible lawn for bagging? Choose a high-lift blade. Do you want to improve your soil's health and avoid bagging? A mulching blade is your best bet. For general-purpose side-discharging, a standard or medium-lift blade works great.

**4. Compare Brands and Read Reviews:**
Decide between genuine Exmark OEM blades and high-quality aftermarket options like Oregon or Maxpower. OEM offers a guaranteed fit, while aftermarket brands provide excellent value. Read user reviews for the specific blades you're considering to see how they perform in real-world conditions.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
The biggest mistake is buying based on price alone. An ultra-cheap blade is likely made from inferior steel that won't hold an edge. Another error is ignoring the center hole size and shape—if it doesn't match, it's useless. Finally, always replace blades in a full set to ensure the mower deck is balanced and cuts evenly.
