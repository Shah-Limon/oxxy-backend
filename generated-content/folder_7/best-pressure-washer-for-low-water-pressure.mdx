Before you start shopping, think about what you'll be cleaning. Are you planning to wash your car, clean a wooden deck, or strip paint from concrete? Different tasks require different levels of power.

-   **Medium-Duty (2000-2800 PSI):** Excellent all-rounders for fences, decks, siding, and driveways. This is the sweet spot for most homeowners.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
-   **PSI (Pounds per Square Inch):** This measures the cleaning force or pressure. Higher PSI means more power to blast away tough grime.
-   **GPM (Gallons Per Minute):** This measures the volume of water flow. Higher GPM means faster cleaning and rinsing. A good balance of PSI and GPM is key. Some models are specifically designed to maximize pressure even with a lower GPM input.
-   **Motor Type:** Electric pressure washers typically use universal or induction motors. Induction motors (found in premium models) are quieter, run cooler, and last significantly longer.
-   **Nozzles:** Quick-connect nozzles allow you to change the spray pattern. A 0° nozzle is a concentrated jet, while a 40° nozzle is a wide fan. A good set (0°, 15°, 25°, 40°, soap) offers the most versatility.
-   **Total Stop System (TSS):** This is a crucial feature that automatically shuts off the motor when the trigger is released. It saves energy, reduces noise, and dramatically extends the life of the pump.

A sturdy frame, whether it's a compact cube or a steel dolly, and never-flat wheels also contribute to a longer service life.
