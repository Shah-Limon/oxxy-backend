### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Brightness (Nits):** For a high-brightness TV, this is paramount. Look for TVs that can achieve high peak brightness, measured in nits. While manufacturers don't always advertise this spec, reviews often do. Higher nits mean the picture will look vibrant and clear even in a bright room.
- **HDR (High Dynamic Range):** This technology enhances contrast and color. Key formats are HDR10, HLG, HDR10+, and Dolby Vision. Dolby Vision and HDR10+ use dynamic metadata for scene-by-scene optimization and are generally considered superior.
- **Smart Platform:** The operating system (OS) determines your user experience. Roku TV is known for simplicity, Fire TV for Alexa integration, and Samsung's Tizen for speed and features. Try to test them out if possible.
- **Connectivity:** Ensure the TV has enough HDMI ports for all your devices (game consoles, soundbar, etc.). Look for at least one HDMI 2.1 port if you have a modern gaming console to take advantage of features like 4K at 120Hz.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Pay attention to the build materials. Premium models often use metal in their stands and bezels for a sturdier, more elegant feel. Cheaper models are typically all plastic.
