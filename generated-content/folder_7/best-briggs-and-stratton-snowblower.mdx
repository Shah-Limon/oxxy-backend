## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Briggs And Stratton Snowblower

When your snowblower needs a repair, choosing the right replacement part is crucial. Here are the key factors to consider to ensure you get the right component the first time.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy anything, you must correctly identify your snowblower's engine model and serial number. These are usually found on a metal plate or sticker on the engine housing. Once you have this information, you can accurately cross-reference it with the part numbers listed in the product description. A carburetor that looks right might have different jetting or linkage points. Always double-check that the replacement part number (e.g., 798917) is listed as compatible with your specific engine model.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Replacement parts for Briggs & Stratton engines come in a wide price range. Genuine OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) parts are typically the most expensive, ranging from $50 to $150 for a carburetor. High-quality aftermarket parts, like many on our list, offer a great balance of performance and value, usually priced between $20 and $50. Basic components like primer bulbs or shear pins can be found for under $10. Set your budget based on the criticality of the part and whether you prefer the guarantee of OEM or the value of aftermarket.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
For a part like a carburetor, key features include whether it comes with new gaskets, fuel lines, or adjustment tools. For an oil, the viscosity (e.g., 5W-30) and type (synthetic vs. conventional) are critical. For mechanical parts like a recoil starter, look for durable materials like a strong rope and a robust spring mechanism. Always look for kits that include all the necessary hardware (bolts, clamps, etc.) to avoid extra trips to the store.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for parts made from high-quality materials. For carburetors, this means corrosion-resistant metal bodies and well-machined jets. For shear pins, you want a metal that is strong enough for normal use but will break cleanly under extreme stress. Reading user reviews can provide insight into the long-term durability of a part. Aftermarket parts from reputable brands often match or even exceed the quality of the original components.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While Briggs & Stratton is the original manufacturer, many aftermarket brands like Ferilter, ZHRANXZ, and A-Premium have built strong reputations for producing reliable and affordable replacement parts. A brand with consistently high user ratings and positive feedback is generally a safe bet. They often specialize in small engine parts and have a good understanding of what users need for a successful repair.

### Part Compatibility & Fitment - Considerations for space and storage
This is the most critical factor. Beyond matching the model number, visually compare the new part in the product photos to your old part. Pay close attention to the orientation of fuel inlets, linkage connection points, and mounting holes. Sometimes a manufacturer makes small changes mid-production year, so a visual confirmation can save you a lot of trouble. Ensure the part will fit without interfering with other engine components.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose briggs and stratton snowblower?

Choosing the right replacement parts for your Briggs & Stratton snowblower involves a methodical approach to ensure you get a component that fits, works, and lasts. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

**1. Diagnose the Problem Accurately:** Before you buy anything, be sure of what is broken. If the engine won't start, is it a fuel problem (carburetor, primer bulb), an ignition issue (spark plug), or a mechanical failure (broken pull cord)? A correct diagnosis prevents you from buying parts you do not need.

**2. Locate Your Engine Model Number:** This is the single most important step. Find the model, type, and code numbers stamped into your engine's metal housing. These numbers are the key to finding the exact parts diagram for your engine.

**3. Look Up the Official Part Number:** Go to the official Briggs & Stratton website or a parts lookup site. Enter your engine numbers to pull up the illustrated parts list. Find the component you need on the diagram and write down the official OEM part number.

**4. Use the OEM Part Number to Shop:** Now, you can search on Amazon or other retailers using that specific part number (e.g., 'Briggs and Stratton 798917'). This will show you both the official OEM part and compatible aftermarket alternatives.

**5. Compare OEM vs. Aftermarket:** OEM parts guarantee a perfect fit but are more expensive. High-quality aftermarket parts can offer significant savings. Read reviews for the aftermarket options, paying close attention to comments about fitment and quality. Often, a well-reviewed aftermarket part is the best value.

**6. Verify with Visuals:** Look closely at the product pictures. Compare them to the part you are replacing. Check the location of fuel lines, mounting holes, and any linkages. This final visual check can prevent you from ordering a part that is subtly different.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Guessing the part:** Never buy a part just because it 'looks right'.
*   **Ignoring the engine model number:** A snowblower model number is different from the engine model number. You need the engine number for engine parts.
*   **Buying the cheapest option without reading reviews:** Some very cheap parts have poor quality control and may not work correctly.

By following this process, you can confidently choose the right part to get your snowblower running like new again.

## FAQ

### What is the best briggs and stratton snowblower part for beginners to replace?
For beginners, the easiest and most common parts to replace are the primer bulb and shear pins. A primer bulb often just pops out and the new one pops in, instantly fixing many starting issues. Shear pins are also designed for easy replacement with basic tools like pliers or a wrench. Both are low-cost, high-impact repairs that require minimal mechanical skill.

### How much should I spend on parts for my briggs and stratton snowblower?
This depends on the part. For common maintenance items like oil, spark plugs, or primer bulbs, expect to spend $5-$20. A complete aftermarket carburetor kit typically costs between $20 and $50. A genuine OEM carburetor might be closer to $100. It is wise to invest in quality parts, but high-quality aftermarket options often provide the best overall value for your money.

### What features are most important in replacement parts?
The most important feature is compatibility. The part must match your specific engine model to ensure it fits and functions correctly. For kits, look for completeness—does it include all the necessary gaskets, seals, and hardware? For mechanical parts, look for durable materials. For maintenance items like oil, ensure it meets the manufacturer's specifications (e.g., 5W-30 synthetic).

### How do I maintain my briggs and stratton snowblower?
Regular maintenance is key to longevity. Before each season, change the oil using a winter-grade synthetic oil, install a new spark plug, and inspect the belts for wear. At the end of the season, either drain the fuel tank or add a fuel stabilizer to prevent the carburetor from getting gummed up. Also, always have spare shear pins on hand.

### Are expensive OEM briggs and stratton snowblower parts worth it?
Sometimes, yes. For critical electronic components or parts with very precise tolerances, OEM can offer peace of mind and a guaranteed fit. However, for many common replacement parts like carburetors, fuel lines, and pull starters, high-quality aftermarket brands offer comparable performance and durability at a much lower price. Reading reviews is key to finding a reliable aftermarket part.

### What brands make the best aftermarket parts for briggs and stratton snowblower?
Brands like Ferilter, HZ, ZHRANXZ, and A-Premium have established strong reputations for producing reliable and well-fitting aftermarket parts for Briggs & Stratton engines. They consistently receive positive user reviews for their quality, completeness of their kits, and overall value. Choosing a reputable aftermarket brand is a great way to save money without sacrificing performance.

### How long do briggs and stratton snowblower engines typically last?
With proper maintenance, a Briggs & Stratton snowblower engine can easily last for 15-20 years, or even longer. The key is regular oil changes, using fuel stabilizer, and replacing worn parts as needed. The engine itself is very durable; it is usually the smaller components like the carburetor or starter that wear out first and need replacement to keep the machine running.

### Can I find good replacement parts for my briggs and stratton snowblower on a budget?
Absolutely. The aftermarket for Briggs & Stratton parts is very competitive, which means you can find high-quality components at very reasonable prices. Products like primer bulb two-packs, multi-packs of shear pins, and complete carburetor kits often provide a full repair solution for less than $30, making it very affordable to keep your snowblower in top condition.
