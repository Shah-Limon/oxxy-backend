## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Electric Stove With Microwave Above

Choosing the right over-the-range microwave is a significant decision for your kitchen's functionality and aesthetics. Consider these key factors to ensure you select a model that perfectly suits your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, assess your kitchen space and cooking habits. The standard width for over-the-range microwaves is 30 inches to match the width of a standard stove. Measure the height and depth of your available cabinet space to ensure a proper fit. Consider your cooking style. Do you cook for a large family? Look for a larger capacity (1.7 cu. ft. or more). Do you do a lot of high-heat stovetop cooking? Prioritize a model with a high CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) rating for better ventilation.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Over-the-range microwaves have a wide price spectrum. You can find basic, reliable models in the $250-$400 range. These will offer standard wattage, capacity, and ventilation. Mid-range options, from $400-$600, often include more advanced features like sensor cooking, more powerful fans (400 CFM), and more versatile cooking presets. Premium models, which can cost $600 and up, typically feature smart technology, convection or air frying capabilities, and high-end designs. Set a budget that aligns with the features you truly need.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Wattage:** Look for at least 1000 watts for fast and even cooking. Lower wattage models will take longer to heat food.
- **Capacity:** Measured in cubic feet, capacity ranges from about 1.1 cu. ft. (slim models) to over 2.0 cu. ft. A standard family will be well-served by a 1.6 to 1.9 cu. ft. model.
- **Ventilation (CFM):** This measures the fan's power. 300 CFM is standard and suitable for most electric ranges. If you have a gas range or do a lot of frying, consider 400 CFM or higher.
- **Sensor Cooking:** This feature uses sensors to detect steam and automatically adjusts cooking time and power, preventing overcooking.
- **Presets & One-Touch Buttons:** These add convenience for common tasks like making popcorn, defrosting, or reheating.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for models with a sturdy build and quality materials. Stainless steel is a popular choice for its durability and ease of cleaning; many now come with a fingerprint-resistant finish. Check the quality of the door handle and the control panel. Reading user reviews can provide insight into the long-term durability of a specific model. A longer warranty, like the 2-year warranty offered by Empava, can also be an indicator of the manufacturer's confidence in their product.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with well-known brands like GE, Samsung, Frigidaire, and BLACK+DECKER often provides a better experience in terms of product quality, customer service, and parts availability. These companies have years of experience in appliance manufacturing and typically offer more reliable products and better support if issues arise.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While these appliances are not portable, their size is a critical consideration. Ensure you have the correct 30-inch width. Pay close attention to the height. A standard model is around 16-17 inches tall. If you have lower cabinets or want more space over your cooktop, a "slim" or "low-profile" model (around 10-12 inches tall) like the Samsung or Midea options could be a perfect fit.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy electric stove with microwave above?

Investing in an electric stove with a microwave above, commonly known as an over-the-range (OTR) microwave, is a very wise decision for most modern kitchens. The primary benefit is its brilliant space-saving design. By combining two essential appliances into one unit, it frees up valuable counter space that would otherwise be occupied by a standalone microwave. This creates a cleaner, more open, and more efficient workspace for meal preparation.

Compared to the alternative of a separate countertop microwave and a dedicated range hood, an OTR microwave offers a more integrated and streamlined solution. The built-in ventilation system, while sometimes less powerful than a high-end dedicated hood, is more than sufficient for the needs of most home cooks, effectively removing smoke, steam, and odors. This dual functionality not only saves space but also often proves more cost-effective than purchasing two separate high-quality appliances.

The long-term benefits are clear. You gain a more functional kitchen layout, improved air quality, and the convenience of having your microwave in an ergonomic, easy-to-access location. While the initial cost might be higher than a simple countertop model, the value it adds in terms of space, convenience, and kitchen workflow makes it a smart and practical investment for years to come.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose electric stove with microwave above?

Choosing the best over-the-range microwave involves a systematic approach to ensure you get the right appliance for your specific needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

1.  **Measure Your Space First:** Before you even look at models, take precise measurements. You need to confirm the standard 30-inch width, but also measure the height and depth of your cabinet space. Note the location of your power outlet and venting access (whether it will be recirculating or vented to the outside).
2.  **Define Your Core Needs & Budget:** What is non-negotiable? Do you need a high-power vent (CFM)? Is large capacity a must? Do you want smart features? List your top three priorities. Then, set a realistic budget based on those needs.
3.  **Prioritize Key Technical Specs:** Focus on wattage (aim for 1000W+), CFM (300+), and capacity (1.6 cu. ft.+ for families). Decide if features like sensor cooking or convection are worth the extra cost for your cooking style.
4.  **Research and Compare Models:** Use lists and reviews to narrow down your options to 3-4 models that fit your criteria. Compare their features, pros, and cons side-by-side. Look at brands with strong reputations for reliability.
5.  **Read User Reviews:** Once you have a shortlist, read recent user reviews on retail sites. Look for recurring comments about performance, noise level, and long-term durability. This provides real-world insight that spec sheets can't.
6.  **Avoid Common Mistakes:** A common mistake is ignoring ventilation needs. Don't buy a 300 CFM model if you do a lot of greasy, high-heat cooking. Another mistake is buying a model that is too tall, leaving you with too little space over your cooktop. Always leave at least 13-16 inches of clearance.
