## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 20 Built In Microwave

Choosing the right built-in microwave involves more than just picking a brand. It's about finding an appliance that fits your space, matches your cooking habits, and integrates seamlessly into your kitchen's design. Here’s a guide to the key factors you should consider.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess how you'll use the microwave. Are you primarily reheating leftovers and making popcorn, or do you need advanced features like convection cooking, grilling, or air frying? For families, a large capacity (2.0 cu. ft. or more) is essential. For singles or couples, a compact model (around 1.0 cu. ft.) might suffice. Most importantly, measure your cabinet cutout precisely—width, height, and depth—to ensure the microwave and its required trim kit will fit perfectly.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Built-in microwave prices can vary significantly. Basic models can start around $300-$500, especially if the trim kit is sold separately. Mid-range models with sensor cooking and larger capacities typically fall in the $500-$900 range. High-end options, like microwave drawers or convection/air fryer combination units, can cost anywhere from $1,000 to over $2,000. Set a realistic budget that includes the potential cost of a separate trim kit and professional installation.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Power (Wattage):** Higher wattage (1000W or more) means faster and more even cooking.
- **Capacity:** Measured in cubic feet, this determines how much you can fit inside.
- **Sensor Cooking:** Automatically adjusts time and power based on food's moisture levels.
- **Convection/Grill/Air Fry:** These features add versatility, allowing you to bake, brown, and crisp food.
- **Turntable vs. Flatbed:** Turntables are standard for even heating, while flatbed models (common in drawers) use different technology and are easier to clean.
- **Control Panel:** Look for intuitive controls, one-touch buttons, and a clear display.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A well-built microwave should feel solid. Look for high-quality materials like stainless steel, which is durable and easy to clean. Check the door mechanism; it should open and close smoothly without feeling flimsy. For combination ovens, features like soft-close doors and reinforced hinges (like on the KoolMore model) are indicators of superior build quality.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Panasonic, Frigidaire, and Summit have long-standing reputations for producing reliable kitchen appliances. Reading customer reviews and checking warranty information can provide insight into a brand's reliability and customer service. While newer brands can offer innovative features, established manufacturers often provide greater peace of mind.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
For built-in microwaves, "portability" refers to the flexibility of installation. Standard models fit into wall cabinets, often above a wall oven. Microwave drawers offer a unique, space-saving solution under the counter. Over-the-range models combine a microwave and a ventilation hood. Always confirm the appliance's external dimensions and the required cutout dimensions before purchasing to avoid costly installation mistakes.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 20 built in microwave?

Investing in a built-in microwave is a wise decision for anyone looking to create a modern, efficient, and aesthetically pleasing kitchen. Unlike countertop models that consume valuable prep space, a built-in unit integrates seamlessly into your cabinetry, providing a custom, high-end look. This streamlined appearance not only enhances the overall design of your kitchen but can also increase your home's resale value.

Beyond aesthetics, built-in models often offer superior features and performance. They typically have higher wattage for faster cooking, larger capacities to accommodate family-sized dishes, and advanced technologies like sensor cooking and convection capabilities that are less common in their countertop counterparts. While the initial investment, including potential installation costs, is higher, the long-term benefits are significant. You gain a more functional workspace, a more powerful cooking appliance, and a sophisticated kitchen environment. Compared to the clutter and limitations of a standard countertop microwave, a built-in model is a durable, long-term upgrade that elevates your daily cooking experience.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 20 built in microwave?

Choosing the best built-in microwave requires a methodical approach to ensure it meets all your needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

**Step 1: Measure Your Space Meticulously**
Before you even start browsing, get out your tape measure. You need the exact height, width, and depth of the cabinet cutout where the microwave will be installed. Remember to check the manufacturer's specifications for required clearance space around the unit for ventilation.

**Step 2: Define Your Primary Use**
Be honest about how you'll use it. If it's for reheating coffee and popcorn, a basic, powerful model will do. If you want to bake, roast, or air fry, you need to look specifically at convection or combination models. This will narrow down your options significantly.

**Step 3: Set Your All-In Budget**
Your budget should include the price of the microwave, the mandatory trim kit (if sold separately), and potential costs for professional installation. A licensed electrician may be needed to ensure the outlet is correctly rated, especially for high-power or combination units.

**Step 4: Compare Key Features**
Once you have a list of models that fit your space and budget, compare their core features. Look at wattage, capacity, and special functions. Is a sensor cook feature a must-have for you? Do you prefer a drawer style for accessibility? Prioritize the features that will make your life easier.

**Step 5: Read Recent User Reviews**
Look for reviews from the last 6-12 months to get an idea of the product's current quality control and long-term reliability. Pay attention to comments about ease of use, noise level, and customer service experiences.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Forgetting the Trim Kit:** Many built-in microwaves require a specific trim kit for a safe and proper installation. It's often sold separately.
- **Ignoring Electrical Requirements:** High-wattage models, especially combination ovens, may require a dedicated 20-amp circuit. Check your home's wiring and the product specs.
- **Guessing Measurements:** An "almost" fit is not good enough. Being off by even a quarter of an inch can ruin an installation.

## FAQ

**What is the best 20 built in microwave for beginners?**
For beginners, a great choice is a model with simple one-touch controls and reliable sensor cooking, like the Summit 27/30” Built-In Microwave. Its straightforward interface and included trim kit simplify the selection and installation process. It offers powerful performance for everyday tasks without overwhelming the user with complex features, making it easy to get great results right away.

**How much should I spend on 20 built in microwave?**
You can expect to spend between $400 and $1,500. Models in the $400-$700 range offer solid performance and good capacity. Mid-range options from $700-$1,000 often include advanced features like convection. Premium models, such as microwave drawers or high-end combination ovens, can exceed $1,000. Your budget should align with the features and design you prioritize.

**What features are most important in 20 built in microwave?**
The most important features are accurate measurements for a perfect fit, sufficient wattage (1000W+ for fast cooking), and a capacity that suits your household size. Sensor cooking is a highly valuable feature for convenience and perfect results. For added versatility, consider models with convection, grilling, or air frying capabilities to expand your cooking options.

**How do I maintain my 20 built in microwave?**
Maintenance is simple. Wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent regularly to prevent food buildup. For stubborn splatters, heat a bowl of water with lemon juice for a few minutes to create steam, which makes cleaning easier. Ensure the ventilation grilles on the trim kit are kept free of dust and debris for proper airflow.

**Are expensive 20 built in microwave worth it?**
Expensive built-in microwaves are worth it if you will use their advanced features. The higher cost often buys you a more stylish design (like a microwave drawer), enhanced functionality (convection/air frying), superior build quality, and better cooking technology for more even results. If you just need basic reheating, a more budget-friendly model will suffice.

**What brands make the best 20 built in microwave?**
Several brands are known for quality built-in microwaves. Frigidaire and Panasonic are praised for their powerful performance and reliable sensor technology. Summit offers excellent all-in-one packages with included trim kits. Brands like KoolMore and AAOBOSI are leading the way in versatile combination ovens, while COSMO and ThermoMate produce stylish and functional microwave drawers.

**How long do 20 built in microwave typically last?**
A well-maintained built-in microwave should last approximately 7 to 10 years. The lifespan depends on the quality of the unit, how frequently it's used, and whether it's kept clean. Models from reputable brands with solid construction often have a longer lifespan. The magnetron, which generates the microwaves, is typically the component that fails first.

**Can I find good 20 built in microwave on a budget?**
Yes, you can find good budget-friendly options. Look for models where the trim kit is included, like the Summit 24” Over The Range Microwave, to save on costs. You may have to forgo advanced features like convection, but you can still find powerful, reliable microwaves with sensor cooking and ample capacity that offer great value for the price.
