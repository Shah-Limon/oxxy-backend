## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Constructor Door Knobs

Choosing the right door knob involves more than just picking a style you like. To ensure you get the perfect hardware for your needs, consider these important factors before you make a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, identify the function of the door. There are four main types of knobs:
*   **Entry Knobs:** These have a keyed lock on the outside and a turn-button lock on the inside. They are essential for exterior doors and any interior door requiring high security.
*   **Privacy Knobs:** These have a lock (usually a push-pin or turn-button) on the inside but no key on the outside. They often have an emergency release slot. They are perfect for bathrooms and bedrooms.
*   **Passage Knobs:** These do not lock at all. They simply have a latch to keep the door closed and a knob or lever to open it. Use them for closets, hallways, and pantries.
*   **Dummy Knobs:** These are single, non-turning knobs used on doors that don't latch, like shallow closets or the inactive side of a French door. They are used simply to pull the door open.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Constructor door knobs offer excellent value, but prices can still vary. You can expect to pay between $15 and $30 for most standard passage or privacy knobs. Keyed entry knobs or more ornate lever styles might range from $25 to $50. Set a budget based on how many knobs you need to replace. Buying in multi-packs can sometimes offer additional savings, which is ideal for larger projects. Remember that investing a little more in a quality knob can save you the hassle and expense of replacement down the line.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that make installation and use easier. An **adjustable backset** is crucial, as it allows the latch to fit the two common standards (2-3/8" and 2-3/4"). **Universal or reversible handing** is another key feature, especially for levers, as it means the handle will work on both left-handed and right-handed doors. Also, check the **door thickness compatibility**, which is typically 1-3/8" to 1-3/4" for residential doors.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The best door knobs are made from solid materials like steel, zinc, or brass. These materials ensure the internal mechanisms will withstand thousands of uses. Pay attention to the finish as well. High-quality finishes like oil-rubbed bronze or satin nickel are applied to resist chipping, scratching, and tarnishing. A good indicator of quality is the product's warranty. A limited lifetime warranty on mechanical parts and the finish, like the one offered by CONSTRUCTOR, shows that the manufacturer stands behind its product's durability.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with a reputable brand like CONSTRUCTOR is a smart move. Established brands have a track record of producing reliable hardware that meets industry standards for security and durability. They are also more likely to offer strong customer support and honor their warranties if you encounter any issues. While a generic, unbranded knob might be cheaper, it often comes with the risk of poor quality, inconsistent manufacturing, and no support after the sale.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While 'portability' isn't a typical concern for door knobs, the related concept of 'fit and scale' is very important. Ensure the knob or lever is appropriately sized for your door. A massive, ornate lever might look out of place on a small pantry door. Conversely, a tiny knob might look lost on a grand entryway. Consider the projection of the knob or lever—how far it sticks out from the door—to ensure it doesn't interfere with walls or furniture in tight spaces like hallways.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose constructor door knobs?

Choosing the right constructor door knobs can be simple if you follow a methodical approach. By breaking down the process, you can ensure you get the perfect hardware for every door in your home and avoid common pitfalls.

**Step 1: Assess Each Door's Function**
Go through your house room by room and make a list of every door that needs new hardware. For each door, determine its required function. Is it an exterior door needing a keyed lock (Entry)? Is it a bathroom needing privacy (Privacy)? Or is it a closet that just needs to latch shut (Passage)? This initial step is the most critical to avoid buying the wrong type of knob.

**Step 2: Take Key Measurements**
Before you shop, measure your doors. You'll need two measurements:
*   **Backset:** The distance from the edge of the door to the center of the hole for the door knob. This is typically either 2-3/8" or 2-3/4". Most CONSTRUCTOR knobs have an adjustable backset, but it's always good to know.
*   **Door Thickness:** Most interior doors are 1-3/8" thick, and exterior doors are 1-3/4" thick. Confirm this to ensure the knob you choose will fit.

**Step 3: Choose Your Style and Finish**
With the technical requirements sorted, you can focus on aesthetics. Decide between a traditional knob or a more modern lever. Levers are often easier to use, especially for children or those with mobility issues. Then, select a finish. Do you want the warm, classic look of Oil Rubbed Bronze or Polished Brass, or the cool, contemporary feel of Satin Nickel? Try to match the finish to other hardware in your home, like light fixtures and cabinet pulls, for a cohesive look.

**Step 4: Avoid Common Mistakes**
*   **Don't buy all the same function:** It's a common mistake to buy a box of privacy knobs for the whole house, only to realize you don't need or want locks on your closets.
*   **Don't forget about handing for levers:** While most CONSTRUCTOR levers are reversible, always double-check. A non-reversible lever will only work for a door that swings in one direction.
*   **Don't throw away old hardware immediately:** Keep one of your old knobs handy to compare measurements and parts if you run into any installation issues.

By following these steps, you can confidently select and install the best constructor door knobs, enhancing both the style and functionality of your home.
