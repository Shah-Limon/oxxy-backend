## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 21 Inch Built In Microwave

Choosing the right built-in microwave involves more than just picking a model that looks good. It's about finding an appliance that fits your space, meets your cooking needs, and offers reliable performance for years to come. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to consider.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, measure your cabinet cutout precisely. A '21 inch' microwave often refers to the internal unit size, but the required cutout with a trim kit can be much larger (typically 24, 27, or 30 inches). Check the manufacturer's specifications for exact cutout dimensions (width, height, and depth). Consider your cooking habits. Do you just reheat and defrost, or do you want to bake, roast, or broil? This will determine if you need a standard microwave, a convection model, or one with a grilling element.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Built-in microwaves vary widely in price. Standard models can range from $300 to $600. For models with advanced features like convection cooking or inverter technology, expect to pay between $600 and $1,200. High-end microwave drawers can cost $1,200 or more. Set a realistic budget, but remember to factor in the potential cost of a separate trim kit if it's not included, which can add another $100-$300.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
*   **Power (Wattage):** Higher wattage (1000W or more) means faster cooking.
*   **Capacity (Cubic Feet):** 0.9-1.2 cu. ft. is good for singles or couples, while 1.6-2.2 cu. ft. is better for families.
*   **Sensor Cooking:** Automatically adjusts cooking time and power based on food's moisture levels. This is a must-have for preventing overcooked food.
*   **Inverter Technology:** Delivers a constant stream of power for more even cooking and defrosting, unlike traditional microwaves that cycle power on and off.
*   **Convection/Broil:** Allows for baking, roasting, and browning, turning your microwave into a second oven.
*   **Turntable:** A rotating turntable ensures food cooks evenly. Some models offer the option to turn it off for large, rectangular dishes.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for models with a stainless steel exterior and interior. Stainless steel is durable, easy to clean, and resists rust and stains. Check for a sturdy door and handle; these are the most frequently used parts of the appliance. A solid, well-built door will feel substantial and close securely.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with well-known brands like Panasonic, Frigidaire, Summit, and KoolMore. These companies have a long history of producing reliable appliances and typically offer better customer service and warranty support. Reading user reviews for specific models can also provide insight into long-term reliability and common issues.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
For built-in models, 'portability' is about installation flexibility. Ensure the model you choose is designed for built-in installation and has proper ventilation as specified by the manufacturer (front, top, or back). Double-check the depth of the unit to ensure it won't protrude too far from your cabinetry. For drawer models, ensure you have adequate clearance below the counter for the drawer to open fully.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 21 inch built in microwave?

Investing in a built-in microwave is a very wise decision for anyone looking to enhance their kitchen's functionality and aesthetic appeal. Unlike countertop models that consume valuable prep space, a built-in unit integrates seamlessly into your cabinetry, creating a custom, high-end look. This streamlined appearance not only declutters your kitchen but can also increase your home's resale value.

Beyond aesthetics, built-in microwaves often offer superior features and performance. Many come equipped with convection cooking capabilities, turning them into a versatile second oven perfect for baking, roasting, and browning. Features like sensor cooking and inverter technology, more common in built-in models, provide more precise and even results than their countertop counterparts. While the initial cost is higher, the long-term benefits of saved space, improved cooking quality, and a more cohesive kitchen design make it a worthwhile investment. It transforms a simple appliance into an integral part of your kitchen's workflow and style.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 21 inch built in microwave?

Choosing the best built-in microwave involves a methodical approach to ensure it meets all your needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

1.  **Measure, Then Measure Again:** The most critical step is accurately measuring your cabinet cutout. Record the height, width, and depth. Use these measurements to filter your options, always referring to the 'cutout dimensions' in the product specifications, not just the appliance dimensions.

2.  **Define Your Primary Use:** Be honest about how you'll use it. If it's primarily for popcorn and reheating leftovers, a powerful standard model will suffice. If you envision baking cookies or roasting a chicken, a convection model is essential. This decision will narrow your search significantly.

3.  **Set Your Budget:** Determine your price range, remembering to account for a trim kit if it's sold separately. A higher budget often opens the door to features like inverter technology, convection, and drawer designs.

4.  **Compare Key Features:** Create a shortlist of 3-4 models that fit your space and budget. Compare them based on wattage, capacity, and special features like sensor cooking or one-touch presets. Prioritize the features that will make your life easier.

5.  **Read Reviews and Check Warranties:** Look for user reviews on multiple retail sites to get a balanced view of a model's long-term performance and reliability. Pay attention to comments about noise level, ease of use, and customer service. Also, check the manufacturer's warranty for parts and labor.

**Common Mistake to Avoid:** A common mistake is buying a microwave based on its external dimensions alone. You must use the required **cutout dimensions** provided by the manufacturer to ensure a proper fit and adequate ventilation. Ignoring this can lead to a microwave that doesn't fit or one that overheats due to improper airflow.

## FAQ

**What is the best 21 inch built in microwave for beginners?**
For beginners, a model with intuitive controls and sensor cooking is ideal. The Frigidaire PMBS3080A is a great choice because its sensor cook and reheat functions take the guesswork out of timing, ensuring great results with minimal effort. Its clear display and simple quick-start options make it very user-friendly for someone new to built-in appliances.

**How much should I spend on 21 inch built in microwave?**
You can expect to spend between $400 and $1,500. A solid, reliable model with good capacity and sensor cooking will typically fall in the $500 to $800 range. If you want premium features like convection cooking or a sleek drawer design, your budget should be closer to $1,000 or more. Remember to factor in the cost of a trim kit if it's not included.

**What features are most important in 21 inch built in microwave?**
The most important features are accurate sensor cooking, adequate power (at least 1000 watts), and a capacity that fits your family's needs. Inverter technology is also highly valuable for even cooking and defrosting. For added versatility, a convection feature that allows for baking and roasting is a fantastic upgrade that can transform your cooking capabilities.

**How do I maintain my 21 inch built in microwave?**
Maintenance is simple. Wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent regularly to prevent food buildup. For tough stains, heat a bowl of water with lemon juice or vinegar inside for a few minutes to create steam, which will loosen grime. Ensure the vents are kept clear of dust and debris for proper airflow.

**Are expensive 21 inch built in microwave worth it?**
Yes, they often are. More expensive models typically offer better build quality, more advanced cooking technology like Cyclonic Inverter systems, and added functionality such as convection cooking. They also include premium designs like microwave drawers. If these features will enhance your cooking routine and kitchen design, the investment is usually justified by improved performance and convenience.

**What brands make the best 21 inch built in microwave?**
Panasonic is renowned for its innovative Inverter and Cyclonic Wave technology, offering superior cooking performance. Frigidaire is a trusted brand known for large-capacity, reliable, and user-friendly models. Summit Appliance excels at creating compact solutions for smaller spaces, while brands like COSMO and thermomate are leaders in the stylish and convenient microwave drawer category.

**How long do 21 inch built in microwave typically last?**
A good quality built-in microwave should last approximately 7 to 10 years with regular use and proper care. The lifespan can be affected by the frequency of use and the quality of the components. Brands with a strong reputation for durability may last even longer. The magnetron, which generates the microwaves, is often the first major component to fail.

**Can I find good 21 inch built in microwave on a budget?**
Absolutely. While premium models have more features, you can find excellent budget-friendly options. Look for models like the Summit OTR24, which often includes the trim kit, providing great value. Focus on core performance features like sufficient wattage and a user-friendly interface rather than advanced cooking modes to get the best appliance for your money.
