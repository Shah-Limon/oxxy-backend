## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Full Spectrum Saunas

Choosing the right full spectrum sauna is a significant decision. To ensure you make a wise investment, consider these key factors before you buy.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you'll use the sauna. Will it be your personal retreat or a wellness tool for the whole family? Capacities typically range from one to four or more people. A 2-person model is often a versatile choice, offering enough space for one person to stretch out or for two to sit comfortably. Also, consider your primary health goals. If deep muscle and tissue repair is a priority, a model with strong near-infrared (NIR) output is crucial. For general detoxification and relaxation, mid and far-infrared (FIR) are key.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Full spectrum saunas are an investment in your health, with prices varying based on size, materials, and features. Entry-level, high-quality 1-2 person models generally range from $1,800 to $3,000. The term 'full spectrum' means the sauna should emit near, mid, and far infrared wavelengths. The type of wood used is critical for a sauna's durability and safety.
