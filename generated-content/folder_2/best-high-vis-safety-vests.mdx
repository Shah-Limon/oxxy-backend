## What Is the Best Way to Choose high vis safety vests?

Choosing the best high vis safety vest involves a methodical approach to ensure it meets your specific safety needs and comfort requirements. Follow these steps for the best results.

**1. Identify Your Environment and Risk Level:**
First, determine the ANSI/ISEA 107 Class you need.
*   **Class 1:** For workers in areas with slow-moving traffic (under 25 mph) and ample separation from it.
*   **Class 2:** For workers near traffic exceeding 25 mph or in environments with poor visibility. This is the most common class for road work and construction.
*   **Class 3:** For workers in the highest-risk environments, such as highway crews working at night or in blizzard conditions, where visibility is minimal.

**2. Select the Right Material:**
Choose between solid polyester and mesh fabric. If you work in a hot or humid climate, a mesh vest will provide crucial breathability and comfort. If you work in cooler conditions or need maximum durability and brightness, a solid polyester vest is an excellent choice.

**3. Evaluate Functional Features:**
Think about your daily tasks. How many pockets do you need? Do you require a clear ID badge holder? Do you use a radio that would benefit from a mic tab? A vest with a sturdy zipper closure is often preferable for a secure fit throughout the day.

**4. Check for Proper Fit:**
Consult the manufacturer's sizing chart. A vest that is too tight can restrict movement, while one that is too loose can be a snag hazard. If you plan to wear the vest over a jacket or other layers, consider sizing up. For women, seeking out a vest with a women-specific cut can significantly improve comfort and safety.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
A common mistake is buying the cheapest option without checking for ANSI/ISEA compliance. An uncertified vest may not provide adequate reflectivity and can offer a false sense of security. Another mistake is neglecting to replace a vest that is faded, dirty, or damaged, as this severely compromises its effectiveness. Regularly wash your vest according to instructions to maintain its visibility.
