### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to the specs.
*   **Lumens:** Measures brightness. A 60W equivalent bulb is typically around 800 lumens.
*   **Color Temperature (Kelvin):** Lower values (2700K) produce warm, yellow light, while higher values (5000K) produce cool, blue-white light.
*   **Wattage:** Indicates energy consumption. Lower wattage for the same lumen output means higher efficiency.
*   **Dimmability:** If you have dimmer switches, ensure your bulbs or fixtures are compatible.
*   **Smart Features:** Look for Wi-Fi/Bluetooth connectivity, app control, and compatibility with voice assistants like Alexa or Google Home if you want a smart home setup.

Professionals think in layers:
*   **Ambient:** The overall illumination of a room (e.g., recessed downlights, ceiling fixtures).
*   **Task:** Focused light for specific activities (e.g., undercabinet lights for cooking, a lamp for reading).
*   **Accent:** Light used to highlight architectural features or decor (e.g., puck lights in a display case).
Decide which layers you need for each room.

**3. Choose Your Technology:**
Decide if you want standard, smart, or a mix of both. If you're building a smart home, GE's Cync line is an excellent choice. If you just need reliable, efficient bulbs, the Bright Stik or standard LED lines are perfect. Don't pay for smart features you won't use.

*   **Underestimating Brightness:** Trying to light a large room with a single, low-lumen bulb will create a dim, uninviting space.
