## What to Look for Before Buying the Best High End Toilet

Choosing the right high-end toilet involves more than just aesthetics. It's about finding a fixture that fits your space, meets your needs, and provides long-term value. Consider these key factors to make an informed decision.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, measure the 'rough-in' distance in your bathroom. This is the distance from the wall behind the toilet to the center of the drainpipe or the toilet's floor bolts. The standard is 12 inches, but 10-inch and 14-inch rough-ins exist. An incorrect measurement will lead to an incompatible toilet. Also, consider who will be using the toilet. For households with elderly members or tall individuals, an ADA-compliant 'comfort height' toilet (around 17-19 inches high) is a much better choice than a standard-height one (around 15 inches).

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
High-end toilets can range significantly in price. A quality one-piece, comfort-height toilet from a reputable brand might cost between $400 and $800. If you are looking for advanced smart toilets with features like a bidet, heated seat, and automatic flushing, expect to budget anywhere from $900 to over $3,000. While the initial cost is higher, these models often provide long-term benefits in hygiene, comfort, and water savings.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Flushing System:** Look for advanced systems like TOTO's TORNADO FLUSH or powerful siphon jets. A high MaP (Maximum Performance) score (800g-1000g) indicates a powerful flush that prevents clogs.
- **Water Consumption (GPF):** Modern toilets are much more efficient. Look for WaterSense certified models that use 1.28 GPF or less. Dual-flush models offer a lower-volume flush for liquid waste, saving even more water.
- **Smart Features:** Popular high-end features include a built-in bidet, heated seat, warm air dryer, automatic deodorizer, night light, and hands-free flushing or lid operation.
- **Bowl Shape:** Elongated bowls are generally considered more comfortable for adults, while round bowls save space in smaller bathrooms.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The best toilets are typically made from vitreous china or high-quality porcelain, which is durable and non-porous. Look for models with special glazes, like TOTO's CEFIONTECT, which create an ultra-smooth surface that prevents waste from sticking and makes cleaning easier. One-piece toilets are often considered more durable and easier to clean as they have no seam between the tank and bowl where leaks or bacteria can develop.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with well-known brands like TOTO, WOODBRIDGE, and Kohler often ensures better quality control, reliable performance, and better access to customer service and replacement parts. These companies have a long history of innovation and stand behind their products with solid warranties.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While a toilet isn't portable, its dimensions are critical. Carefully check the product's overall depth, width, and height to ensure it will fit comfortably in your space, allowing enough clearance around it. One-piece toilets often have a slightly more compact footprint than their two-piece counterparts. A skirted design, while elegant, can sometimes make accessing the bolts more difficult during installation in a tight space.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy high end toilet?

Investing in a high-end toilet is a decision that pays dividends in comfort, hygiene, and home value. While the upfront cost is higher than a standard model, the long-term benefits often justify the expense. For starters, superior performance is a key advantage. High-end toilets feature advanced flushing technologies that are not only more powerful at preventing clogs but are also more water-efficient, leading to noticeable savings on your utility bills over time.

Beyond performance, the improvements in comfort and hygiene are transformative. Features like heated seats, built-in bidets with warm water and air dryers, and automatic deodorizers turn a daily necessity into a luxurious and pleasant experience. For many, the enhanced cleanliness offered by a bidet function is a life-changing upgrade that reduces toilet paper use and promotes better personal health.

Furthermore, a high-end toilet can be a significant aesthetic upgrade. Sleek one-piece designs, skirted trapways, and artistic forms can serve as a focal point in a bathroom renovation, contributing to the overall value of your home. When you consider the durability, water savings, enhanced comfort, and improved hygiene, a high-end toilet is not just a purchase; it's a wise investment in your quality of life and your property.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose high end toilet?

Choosing the best high-end toilet requires a methodical approach to ensure it meets all your needs and fits your space perfectly. Follow these steps for a successful selection process.

**1. Measure Your Space:** The first and most critical step is to measure your toilet's rough-in size (from the wall to the center of the floor drain). This single measurement will determine which toilets are compatible with your bathroom's plumbing. Also, measure the available width and depth to ensure your chosen model won't make the space feel cramped.

**2. Define Your Must-Have Features:** Make a list of what is most important to you. Is it accessibility? Prioritize ADA-compliant comfort height models. Is it hygiene? Look for smart toilets with bidet functions and self-cleaning features. Is it water conservation? Focus on dual-flush and WaterSense certified options.

**3. Set a Realistic Budget:** Determine how much you are willing to spend. This will help you narrow your search. Remember to factor in potential installation costs if you are not planning a DIY project.

**4. Compare Flushing Performance:** Don't just look at GPF; look at the MaP score. A score of 1000g is the highest rating and indicates the toilet can handle a large amount of waste in a single flush, preventing clogs.

**5. Read Reviews and Consider Brand Reputation:** Once you have a shortlist, read user reviews to get real-world feedback on performance, ease of installation, and long-term reliability. Opting for a reputable brand can provide peace of mind regarding quality and warranty support.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Ignoring the Rough-In:** Buying a toilet with the wrong rough-in size is a costly and frustrating mistake.
- **Forgetting an Outlet:** If you choose a smart toilet, ensure you have a GFCI electrical outlet within a few feet of the toilet.
- **Overlooking Aesthetics:** Make sure the style of the toilet (modern, traditional, etc.) complements your overall bathroom design.

## FAQ

### What is the best high end toilet for beginners?
For someone new to high-end toilets, the WOODBRIDGE T-0018 is an excellent starting point. It offers key premium features like a sleek one-piece design, a powerful and quiet dual flush, and a comfortable height without the complexity or cost of a smart toilet. It's a significant upgrade in style and performance from a standard model and provides a great introduction to the benefits of high-end fixtures.

### How much should I spend on high end toilet?
A good quality, non-smart high-end toilet typically costs between $400 and $800. For a smart toilet with bidet functions, heated seats, and other advanced features, you should expect to spend between $900 and $3,000 or more. The price depends on the brand, features, and design complexity. Set a budget based on the features that are most important to you.

### What features are most important in high end toilet?
The most important features are a powerful and efficient flushing system (look for high MaP scores and WaterSense certification), comfort (elongated bowl and comfort height), and ease of cleaning (one-piece, skirted designs are best). For smart toilets, a quality bidet function with adjustable settings, a heated seat, and a self-cleaning nozzle are the most crucial features for an enhanced experience.

### How do I maintain my high end toilet?
Maintenance is similar to a standard toilet but with a few considerations. Use non-abrasive cleaners to protect special glazes and finishes. For smart toilets, regularly run the nozzle-cleaning function. For toilets with foam shields, you will need to occasionally refill the soap reservoir. The easy-to-clean designs of most high-end toilets generally make routine cleaning much faster and more effective.

### Are expensive high end toilet worth it?
Yes, for many people, they are worth it. The investment often pays off in water savings, improved personal hygiene, enhanced comfort, and increased home value. The reliability and superior performance of a high-end model can also prevent the costs and hassles associated with clogs and repairs that are more common with cheaper toilets. The daily comfort and luxury they provide are a significant lifestyle upgrade.

### What brands make the best high end toilet?
TOTO is widely regarded as a leader in the industry, known for its innovation, quality, and performance with features like the TORNADO FLUSH and CEFIONTECT glaze. Other reputable brands that produce excellent high-end toilets include WOODBRIDGE, which offers great style and value, and brands like Casta Diva and HOROW, which are making a name for themselves in the feature-rich smart toilet market.

### How long do high end toilet typically last?
A high-quality porcelain toilet bowl and tank can last for decades, often 50 years or more. The internal flushing mechanisms and electronic components of a smart toilet will have a shorter lifespan. Typically, you can expect the mechanical parts to last 10-15 years and electronic components 5-10 years before they may need replacement. Investing in a brand with good parts availability is wise.

### Can I find good high end toilet on a budget?
Yes, you can. Brands like WOODBRIDGE offer models that provide a high-end look and excellent features, such as a one-piece design and a powerful dual flush, at a more accessible price point, often under $500. While you may not get advanced smart features, you can still get a significant upgrade in terms of design, performance, and ease of maintenance compared to a basic toilet.

### What is a MaP score?
The MaP (Maximum Performance) score is an independent rating of a toilet's flush power. It measures how many grams of solid waste a toilet can completely clear in a single flush. A score of 1000g is the highest possible rating, indicating an extremely powerful and effective flush that is highly resistant to clogging. It is a much better indicator of performance than GPF alone.

### What is the difference between a one-piece and a two-piece toilet?
A two-piece toilet has a separate tank and bowl that are bolted together during installation. This is the traditional design. A one-piece toilet has the tank and bowl integrated into a single, seamless unit. One-piece toilets are generally considered more stylish, are much easier to clean because they have no seam, and have a lower risk of leaks between the tank and bowl.
