### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations

Consider what you want to filter out. If you're primarily concerned with chlorine taste, most standard filters will suffice. Pay close attention to the filter's capabilities. Look for NSF/ANSI certifications, which are independent verifications of a filter's contaminant-reduction claims. A longer life means fewer changes and less hassle. Features like an electronic filter change indicator (like Brita's SmartLight) are more accurate and convenient than simple sticker reminders.

Consider your daily water consumption. How many people will be using the pitcher?

- **Office/High-Use:** A large-capacity dispenser (18+ cups) is the most convenient option.

**3. Set Your Budget (Initial and Long-Term):**
Look at both the pitcher's price and the cost of replacement filters. A cheaper pitcher might use expensive filters that need frequent replacement. Calculate the cost per gallon to get a true understanding of the long-term expense. Sometimes, investing in a pitcher with a longer-lasting filter can be more economical.

- **Filtration Certification:** Look for NSF/ANSI certifications (e.g., 42 for taste, 53 for health contaminants). This is your guarantee of performance.
