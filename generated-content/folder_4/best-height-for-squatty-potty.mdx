Choosing the right toilet stool can significantly impact your comfort and health. Before you make a purchase, consider these key factors to ensure you select the perfect model for your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, measure your toilet. The height from the floor to the top of the toilet seat will determine the best stool height for you. Standard toilets (14-16 inches) usually pair well with a 7-inch stool. Taller, 'comfort height' toilets (16-18 inches) often require a 9-inch stool. Also, consider who will be using it. If multiple people of different heights or ages will use the stool, an adjustable model is your best bet.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Toilet stool prices can range from around $20 for a basic plastic model to over $50 for premium wood or highly adjustable versions. A simple, fixed-height plastic stool like the Squatty Potty Original offers excellent value in the $20-$30 range. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
The most critical feature is height. If you're unsure, choose an adjustable model. Other key features include foldability for storage, material (plastic, bamboo, or wood), and weight capacity. If you travel, a portable model is essential. For safety, look for non-slip surfaces and feet to prevent accidents.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for stools made from high-quality, durable materials. Hard polyurethane plastic is strong, waterproof, and easy to clean. A well-constructed stool should feel solid and stable, with no wobbling.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Squatty Potty have a long-standing reputation for quality and effectiveness. They pioneered the market and have millions of positive reviews. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the size of your bathroom. Most toilet stools are designed with a C-shape or arc to fit snugly against the toilet base. However, if space is extremely limited, a foldable model is a game-changer. These can be tucked away beside the toilet or in a cabinet when not in use. For travel, look for a model that folds completely flat and is lightweight.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy height for squatty potty?

Investing in a toilet stool, often called a Squatty Potty, is one of the wisest and most cost-effective decisions you can make for your long-term digestive health. While some might see it as just another bathroom gadget, its benefits are rooted in human physiology. For centuries, humans squatted, which is the body's natural and most effective position for elimination. The modern sitting toilet, while comfortable, creates a kink in the colon that can lead to straining, incomplete evacuation, and a host of related health issues like constipation, hemorrhoids, and bloating.

A toilet stool simply elevates your feet, changing your posture from sitting to squatting. This small adjustment relaxes the crucial puborectalis muscle, straightens the colon, and allows for a faster, easier, and more complete process. Compared to alternatives like laxatives or fiber supplements, which can have side effects or create dependency, a toilet stool is a one-time, non-invasive, mechanical solution. The long-term benefits far outweigh the modest initial cost. You are not just buying a piece of plastic or wood; you are investing in preventative care, daily comfort, and the overall well-being of your digestive system.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose height for squatty potty?

Choosing the best height for a Squatty Potty is a straightforward process if you follow a few simple steps. Here is a guide to help you make the perfect choice.

**Step 1: Measure Your Toilet and Yourself**
The first and most important step is to measure the height of your toilet from the floor to the top of the seat. Standard toilets are typically 14-16 inches high, while comfort height toilets are 16-18 inches. As a general rule, a 7-inch stool is best for standard toilets, and a 9-inch stool is better for taller toilets. Also, consider your own height. Taller individuals may prefer a 9-inch stool even on a standard toilet.

**Step 2: Assess Your Flexibility**
If you are new to squatting or have limited flexibility in your hips and knees, starting with a 7-inch stool is highly recommended. It provides a gentler angle and makes the adjustment period easier. More flexible individuals or experienced squatters might find a 9-inch stool more effective.

**Step 3: Consider Other Users**
If the stool will be used by multiple people in your household, an adjustable model is the ideal solution. Models that offer both 7-inch and 9-inch heights (or even more options) ensure that everyone, from adults to children, can find a comfortable and effective position.

**Step 4: Avoid Common Mistakes**
A common mistake is thinking 'higher is always better'. A stool that is too high can be uncomfortable and even unsafe. Stick to the recommended guidelines based on your toilet height. Another mistake is prioritizing looks over function. While a stylish wooden stool is nice, ensure it provides the correct height and has essential safety features like a non-slip surface.

**Step 5: Budget vs. Premium**
A budget-friendly plastic stool provides the same core health benefits as a premium wooden one. If your budget is tight, a basic model is an excellent choice. Upgrade to a premium or adjustable model if you value aesthetics, have multiple users, or need specific features like foldability for storage. The most important thing is to get a stool that puts your body in the right position.
