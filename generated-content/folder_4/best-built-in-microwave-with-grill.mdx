## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Built In Microwave With Grill

Choosing the right built-in microwave with a grill function requires careful consideration of your kitchen space, cooking habits, and budget. This guide will walk you through the essential factors to ensure you select an appliance that meets all your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess how you plan to use your microwave. Are you primarily reheating leftovers and making popcorn, or do you envision grilling steaks, crisping pizzas, and baking with a convection feature? A basic microwave with a grill might suffice for occasional browning, but a multi-functional unit with convection and air fry capabilities will serve a more adventurous cook. Measure your existing cabinet cutout precisely (width, height, and depth) to ensure compatibility. Remember to account for any required ventilation space specified by the manufacturer.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Built-in microwave and grill combos can range significantly in price. Basic models may start around $300-$500, offering standard microwave and grilling functions. Mid-range options, often including convection cooking, typically fall between $500 and $1,000. High-end models, which may feature advanced sensor technology, air frying, and premium finishes, can exceed $1,000. Set a realistic budget based on the features that are most important to you, but be prepared to invest a bit more for enhanced durability and versatility.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look beyond the basic functions. Key features to consider include:
*   **Wattage:** Higher wattage (1000W or more) means faster cooking.
*   **Capacity:** Measured in cubic feet, capacity determines how much you can cook at once. 1.0-1.5 cu. ft. is suitable for individuals or couples, while families should look for 1.6 cu. ft. or larger.
*   **Sensor Cooking:** This feature automatically adjusts cooking time and power based on food moisture, preventing overcooking.
*   **Convection/Air Fry:** For those who want to bake, roast, or achieve crispy results without oil, these features are essential.
*   **Turntable:** Ensure it's large enough for your typical dishes. Some models offer a rectangular tray or the option to turn the turntable off for larger items.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A built-in appliance is a long-term investment, so construction quality matters. Look for units with a stainless steel exterior and interior, as this material is durable, easy to clean, and resists rust and odors. Check for a sturdy door and handle, as these are the most frequently used parts. A well-constructed unit will feel solid and operate smoothly.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with reputable brands known for reliability and good customer service. Brands like Panasonic, Frigidaire, and Emerson have a long history of producing quality kitchen appliances. Reading customer reviews and checking consumer reports can provide valuable insight into a model's long-term performance and the manufacturer's responsiveness to any issues.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While "built-in" implies a permanent installation, the dimensions are critical. Standard built-in microwave widths are typically 24, 27, or 30 inches to match common cabinet sizes. Always double-check the required cutout dimensions against your available space. If you are outfitting an RV, look for models specifically designed for mobile use, as they are often more compact and built to withstand vibrations.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy built in microwave with grill?

Investing in a built-in microwave with a grill is an excellent decision for anyone looking to maximize kitchen functionality and save space. This single appliance combines the rapid cooking of a microwave with the browning, crisping, and searing capabilities of a grill. This means you can achieve a perfectly melted and browned cheese on a casserole, grill a sandwich, or even put a nice char on vegetables without needing a separate countertop grill or firing up your main oven. The space-saving aspect is a major benefit, as it frees up valuable counter real estate and creates a sleek, integrated look in your kitchen.

Compared to a standard microwave, the grill function opens up a whole new world of culinary possibilities, allowing for more texture and flavor in your meals. While the initial cost is higher than a basic microwave, the value comes from its versatility. It can often stand in for a toaster oven or even a small conventional oven for certain tasks, making it a cost-effective choice in the long run. For those who value efficiency, aesthetics, and culinary flexibility, a built-in microwave with a grill is a wise and practical upgrade for any modern kitchen.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose built in microwave with grill?

Choosing the best built-in microwave with a grill involves a methodical approach to match your specific needs with the right features. Follow these steps to make a confident choice.

**1. Measure Your Space First:** Before you even start browsing, get precise measurements of the cabinet cutout where the microwave will be installed. Measure the height, width, and depth. This single step will narrow down your options significantly and prevent the disappointment of buying a unit that doesn't fit.

**2. Define Your Cooking Style:** Be honest about how you'll use it. If you just want to occasionally crisp up a pizza, a basic grill function will do. If you're a passionate cook looking to roast a chicken or bake cookies, you should prioritize models with true convection technology in addition to the grill.

**3. Set Your Feature Priorities:** Create a list of must-have features. Is sensor cooking essential for you? Do you need one-touch buttons for popcorn and potatoes? How important is an air fry function? Ranking your priorities will help you decide where to allocate your budget.

**4. Research and Read Reviews:** Once you have a shortlist of models that fit your space and feature requirements, dive into user reviews. Look for patterns in feedback regarding performance, reliability, and ease of use. Professional reviews can also provide valuable insights into how different models compare in head-to-head testing.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:** A common mistake is focusing only on wattage. While important, features like inverter technology (for even cooking) or a quality grill element can have a bigger impact on your results. Another mistake is forgetting to budget for a trim kit if it's not included, as this is essential for a true "built-in" look.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:** If your current microwave is simply a reheating box, upgrading to a model with a grill and convection will revolutionize your cooking. It's a worthy investment. If you're on a tight budget, focus on finding a reliable model with a solid grill function and good user reviews, even if it lacks fancier features like air frying or extensive sensor menus.

## FAQ

**What is the best built in microwave with grill for beginners?**
For beginners, the Emerson MWG1191SB is an excellent choice. It offers a straightforward combination of a powerful microwave and an effective grill function without overwhelming you with complex features. Its retro-style controls are intuitive and easy to navigate, and the preset cooking options simplify common tasks. It provides great versatility at a very approachable price point, making it perfect for learning the benefits of a grill combo.

**How much should I spend on built in microwave with grill?**
You can expect to spend between $300 and $1,200. A solid, reliable model with good microwave and grill functions will typically cost between $400 and $700. If you want premium features like true convection cooking, air frying, and advanced sensor technology, your budget should be in the $700 to $1,200 range. Spending more often gets you better build quality, more power, and greater cooking versatility.

**What features are most important in built in microwave with grill?**
The most important features are an effective grilling element, adequate microwave wattage (1000W or higher), and a size/capacity that fits your needs. Beyond that, sensor cooking is highly valuable as it takes the guesswork out of heating food properly. For more versatility, look for a model that also includes a convection function, which allows you to bake and roast.

**How do I maintain my built in microwave with grill?**
Maintenance is simple. Regularly wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent to prevent food splatter from baking on. Pay special attention to the grill element at the top of the cavity. Clean the glass turntable in the dishwasher or by hand. For the stainless steel exterior, use a cleaner specifically designed for stainless steel to avoid streaks and keep it looking new.

**Are expensive built in microwave with grill worth it?**
Yes, they can be worth it if you will use the advanced features. Expensive models often include true convection systems, air fry capabilities, more precise sensor cooking, and higher-quality construction. If you plan to use your appliance as a secondary oven for baking, roasting, and creating complex meals, the investment in a high-end model will pay off in performance and versatility.

**What brands make the best built in microwave with grill?**
Several brands are known for producing high-quality built-in microwaves with grills. Panasonic is a leader in cooking technology with its Inverter systems. Frigidaire is renowned for large-capacity, family-friendly models. Brands like thermomate and AAOBOSI offer feature-packed combination ovens, while Emerson provides excellent value. For RV-specific models, Tough Grade and Rugged Trail are reliable choices.

**How long do built in microwave with grill typically last?**
A good quality built-in microwave with a grill should last between 7 and 10 years with proper care and regular maintenance. The lifespan can be affected by usage frequency and the quality of the components. Models from reputable brands often have a longer lifespan due to better construction and more durable parts.

**Can I find good built in microwave with grill on a budget?**
Absolutely. While they won't have all the bells and whistles of premium models, you can find excellent budget-friendly options. Look for models like the Emerson microwaves, which provide a reliable grill function, solid power, and stylish designs at a fraction of the cost of high-end units. Prioritize the core functions you need and you can find a great appliance without overspending.
