## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 800 Watt Microwave Oven

Choosing the right 800-watt microwave involves more than just picking a color you like. To ensure you get an appliance that meets your needs, consider these key factors before making a purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you will use the microwave. Are you primarily reheating leftovers and making popcorn? Or do you plan to cook full meals and defrost large items? An 800-watt microwave is perfect for basic tasks in smaller households, dorms, or offices. If you have a large family or frequently cook complex meals, you might consider a model with a larger capacity (around 1.0 cu. ft. or more) or a higher wattage for faster cooking times. Also, measure your available counter space to ensure the microwave's external dimensions will fit comfortably.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
800-watt microwave ovens are generally affordable, but prices can vary based on brand, features, and design. You can expect to find basic, reliable models in the $80-$120 range. For microwaves with special features like a grill function, smart technology, or premium retro designs, the price may be closer to $130-$200. Set a realistic budget based on the features that are most important to you. Remember that investing a little more in a model with better durability or useful functions can provide better long-term value.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look beyond the wattage. Pay attention to the number of power levels; 10 levels offer more precise control than 5. Check for pre-programmed settings for common foods, as these add convenience. The size of the turntable is also important—ensure it is large enough for your typical dinner plates. Other features to look for include a child safety lock, a mute function to silence beeps, and an easy-to-clean interior. For tech enthusiasts, features like sensor cooking or WiFi connectivity can be worth the extra cost.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A well-built microwave will last for years. Look for models with a sturdy door that opens and closes smoothly. The quality of the materials matters; a stainless steel interior is more durable and easier to clean than a painted one. Check the control panel—are the buttons or dial firm and responsive? Reading user reviews can provide insight into the long-term durability and common issues of a particular model.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Opting for a reputable brand can provide peace of mind. Brands like Toshiba, GE, and Emerson have a long history of producing reliable kitchen appliances. They typically offer better customer support and more accessible warranty services. While lesser-known brands can offer good value, established manufacturers are often a safer bet when it comes to quality control and product longevity.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
The primary appeal of many 800-watt microwaves is their compact size. Capacities usually range from 0.7 to 1.1 cubic feet. A 0.9 cu. ft. model is a popular sweet spot, offering enough interior space for most needs without a large footprint. Always check the external dimensions (width, depth, and height) listed in the product specifications to ensure it will fit in your designated space, leaving enough clearance for ventilation (usually a few inches on the top, back, and sides).

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 800 watt microwave oven?

Investing in an 800-watt microwave oven is a very wise decision for a significant number of people, particularly those with specific needs related to space, energy use, and cooking habits. These compact appliances are champions of efficiency. For individuals in apartments, dormitories, or those with smaller kitchens, an 800-watt model provides essential cooking and reheating capabilities without monopolizing precious counter space. Compared to their higher-wattage counterparts (1000W or 1200W), they consume less electricity, which can lead to modest but noticeable savings on your energy bill over time.

While a higher-wattage microwave will cook food faster, the difference is often only a matter of seconds or a minute for most common tasks like reheating coffee, cooking a frozen meal, or popping popcorn. For users who don't require lightning-fast cooking speeds, the trade-off for a smaller footprint and lower energy use is well worth it. Furthermore, 800-watt models are often more affordable, making them an accessible entry point for students, young professionals, or as a secondary appliance in a larger home. The long-term benefit is having a perfectly sized, efficient, and cost-effective tool that handles the vast majority of daily microwave tasks with ease.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 800 watt microwave oven?

Choosing the best 800-watt microwave oven involves a methodical approach to ensure it aligns perfectly with your lifestyle and kitchen. Follow these steps for a confident purchase.

**Step 1: Assess Your Space and Capacity Needs**
Before anything else, measure the countertop or shelf space where the microwave will live. Note the maximum width, depth, and height. Remember to allow for a few inches of clearance around the unit for proper ventilation. Next, consider capacity. A 0.9 cubic foot model is a versatile choice for most individuals and couples, fitting a standard dinner plate comfortably.

**Step-by-Step Selection Guide:**
1.  **Define Your Primary Use:** Be honest about what you will use it for. Mostly reheating and defrosting? Or do you need presets for specific meals? This will determine which features are essential.
2.  **Set Your Budget:** Decide on a price range you are comfortable with. This will help you narrow down your options and avoid overspending on features you do not need.
3.  **Compare Core Features:** Look at the number of power levels, the presence of one-touch presets, and the type of control interface (dial vs. buttons). A turntable is a must-have for even cooking.
4.  **Evaluate Advanced Features:** Decide if you want extras like a grill function, sensor cooking, or smart home integration. These add cost but can also add significant convenience.
5.  **Read User Reviews:** Look for patterns in feedback regarding reliability, noise level, and ease of cleaning. Real-world experience is invaluable.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Dimensions:** The most common mistake is buying a microwave that doesn't fit. Always measure twice.
*   **Paying for Unused Features:** Don't be swayed by a long list of features you will never use. Stick to what is practical for you.
*   **Forgetting About Cleaning:** Look for models with easy-to-clean interiors. A smooth, non-porous surface will save you a lot of scrubbing.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
Choose a budget option if your needs are simple: reheating, defrosting, and popcorn. These models are workhorses that get the job done. Consider upgrading to a premium model if you value convenience features like sensor cooking that eliminates guesswork, multi-functionality like an air fryer to save space, or smart tech that integrates with your connected home.

**What features are most important in 800 watt microwave oven?**
The most important features are a turntable for even cooking, multiple power levels (at least 5) for cooking flexibility, and a capacity that fits your dinner plates (0.9 cu. ft. is a good standard). One-touch presets for common items like popcorn and beverages are also extremely useful for daily convenience. A child lock is a crucial safety feature for households with young children.

**How do I maintain my 800 watt microwave oven?**
Maintenance is simple. Wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild soap after spills to prevent food from baking on. For tougher stains, heat a bowl of water with lemon juice or vinegar for a few minutes; the steam will loosen grime. Regularly wash the glass turntable and support ring in the sink. Keep the exterior clean and ensure the vents are free of dust and debris.

**Are expensive 800 watt microwave oven worth it?**
It depends on your needs. If an expensive model includes features you will genuinely use, like the GE Profile's Scan-to-Cook technology or the Toshiba Air Fryer Combo's versatility, then it can be worth the investment. **How long do 800 watt microwave oven typically last?**
With proper care and average use, a good quality 800-watt microwave oven can be expected to last between 7 and 10 years. The lifespan can be affected by factors like frequency of use and how well it is maintained. Sticking to well-known brands often ensures better build quality and components, which can contribute to a longer operational life for the appliance.

**Can I find good 800 watt microwave oven on a budget?**
Absolutely. There are many excellent budget-friendly options available that do not compromise on core performance. To find one, focus on essential features like a turntable, adequate power levels, and a user-friendly interface. You may have to forgo premium finishes or advanced features, but you can easily find a reliable and effective 800-watt microwave that handles all the basics for under $100.
